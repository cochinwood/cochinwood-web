Questions buyers ask

Commercial plywood, answered.

What is the difference between BWR and MR plywood?

BWR (boiling-water-resistant) uses a fortified glue line for kitchens and humid areas; MR (moisture-resistant) is urea-formaldehyde bonded for dry interiors and furniture. Both are IS 303 commercial grades. IS 710 vs IS 303 explained →

Is commercial plywood waterproof?

No — for constant water you need IS 710 BWP marine plywood. BWR resists occasional wetting; MR handles humidity only.

What thickness and sizes do you supply?

4–25 mm at 1220 × 2440 mm and custom cut-to-size, in BWR and MR grades.
