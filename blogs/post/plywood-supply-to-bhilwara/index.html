Plywood Supply to Bhilwara

Cochin Wood Industries

Bhilwara is one of those Indian cities where the demand for industrial packaging is not driven by one or two factories — it is driven by an entire ecosystem. The synthetic textile mills, the suiting and shirting weavers, the polyester and viscose spinners, and a heavy carbon-graphite cluster all generate steady outbound freight, and each of those flows needs different timber and plywood. This post breaks down what the Cochin Wood group ships into the Bhilwara belt, why specific grades match specific loads, and how the Kochi-to-Bhilwara lane actually works week to week.

Why Bhilwara matters for a plywood supplier

Bhilwara is commonly called the Manchester of India for synthetic textiles. The district produces a very large share of the country's polyester-viscose suiting fabric, and the cluster has matured into a complete textile chain — spinning, weaving, processing, garmenting and machinery support. Alongside the textile economy sits HEG's carbon graphite operation at Mandpam, one of the largest graphite electrode plants in the world. The combination means a Bhilwara plywood buyer is not just a "packing case" buyer; they are buying timber for export crates, machinery skids, container floors and shuttering ply for plant expansion — sometimes within the same purchase order.

That mix is exactly the type of demand the Cochin Wood group is set up for. We are a Kerala-based plywood manufacturer with group operations in Perumbavoor since 1986 — about forty years of group manufacturing behind the spec sheet — and we ship pan-India through contract factories operating under CWI specifications. Bhilwara is a long lane from Kochi, but it is a high-value lane because the average crate from this belt is heavier, larger and more export-bound than the national average.

The Bhilwara industrial picture

Industrial areas to know

  • RIICO Industrial Area, Bhilwara — the original textile and processing hub, dense with weaving and dyeing units.
  • RIICO Growth Centre, Hamirgarh — the larger expansion belt south of the city; home to several integrated spinning and weaving plants and the segment most likely to need full container loads of plywood at a time.
  • Mandpam — HEG's graphite electrode complex; specialised heavy-duty crating and skid demand.
  • Gulabpura and Mandalgarh corridors — secondary industrial pockets along NH-79; textile machinery service units and ancillary fabrication.

Sectors we serve in this belt

  • Synthetic yarn and polyester-viscose fabric exporters needing strong, lightweight outer packing.
  • Textile machinery OEMs and service contractors shipping looms, sizing machines, draw-texturising units and spare assemblies.
  • Graphite and refractory-adjacent units requiring heavy-duty timber crates and reinforced skids.
  • Engineering ancillaries, cement and mineral processing units in the wider Bhilwara-Chittorgarh corridor that need shuttering plywood for capex builds and pallets for inbound raw material flow.

What we ship into Bhilwara

Packing-grade plywood

Our core SKU for this belt is commercial / rubberwood packing-grade plywood in the standard thickness range — 6mm, 8mm, 10mm, 12mm, 14mm, 15mm, 16mm and 18mm. For Bhilwara's typical fabric and yarn export box, 8mm and 12mm do most of the work; 12mm walls with 18mm bases is a common build for heavier textile machinery crates. All rates are ex-factory, GST and transport extra, and we maintain a fixed ₹2.50 per square foot margin over our purchase rate so the buyer sees a transparent build-up rather than a black-box price.

Crate and case construction

For full-blown export crates we usually combine packing-grade plywood panels with sawn jackwood or rubberwood runners and battens. Jackwood runs at roughly ₹400 per cubic foot (with the standard quarter-inch-minus tolerance buyers in this segment expect). Heavy textile machinery shipments to the Middle East and Vietnam typically need ISPM-15 compliant softwood framing — we coordinate fumigation stamps and heat-treatment certificates with the crate builder so the cargo is not held at port.

Pallets

The Bhilwara belt uses two pallet families:

  • Two-way and four-way export pallets for outbound yarn and fabric — generally 1200×1000 mm or 1100×1100 mm for GCC and Southeast Asia destinations.
  • Heavy-duty domestic pallets in 1200×800 mm and Indian standard sizes for inbound raw material movement inside the larger mills.

Container flooring

For exporters loading 20ft and 40ft containers directly at the plant, we supply 18mm and 28mm container-floor-grade plywood when the use case calls for a one-trip reinforced floor or a custom loading skid. This is a smaller line item by volume but a high-value one for a polished export operation.

Shuttering plywood

For capex projects — new spinning halls, dyeing additions, warehouse roofs and the periodic civil work around HEG and the textile parks — film-faced shuttering plywood in 12mm and 18mm goes in for slab and wall formwork.

Kochi-to-Bhilwara logistics

Bhilwara is roughly 2,100 km from Kochi by road. The realistic trucking lane runs Kochi (Ernakulam) → Bengaluru → Hyderabad → Nagpur → Indore → Chittorgarh → Bhilwara, or the western variant through Mumbai-Nashik-Udaipur-Bhilwara when the load originates near the port. Transit time for a full truckload (32ft single-axle or multi-axle) is typically 6 to 9 days door-to-door depending on monsoon conditions, RTO checkpoint queues and the buyer's unloading window.

Practical points we manage on this lane:

  • Truck capacity matched to thickness mix. 18mm plywood loads heavier than 8mm at the same square-footage — we plan the loading sheet so the buyer is not overpaying freight on cubed-out trucks.
  • Headroom for monsoon detours. The Konkan and Ghat sections see frequent diversions July to September; we build a 1-2 day headroom into committed delivery dates.
  • Unloading charges quoted upfront. Bhilwara mill yards usually unload by forklift or manual gang; either way the unloading charge sits in the PI so there is no surprise at the gate.
  • Single-stop or split-drop. For buyers in both Bhilwara and Chittorgarh / Bhim / Gulabpura, we can split a truck across two delivery points at a small additional charge rather than forcing a second LCL.

FAQ — Bhilwara buyers

Do you supply ISPM-15 compliant crates ready for export?

We supply the plywood and timber components; ISPM-15 stamping is done by certified fumigation partners at or near the buyer's premises. We can coordinate the documentation so the crate is export-ready.

What is the minimum order quantity for a Kochi-to-Bhilwara shipment?

The lane economics start to work cleanly at one full truckload (roughly 600-700 sheets of mixed thickness, depending on the load plan). Smaller quantities are possible via part-load aggregators, but the per-sheet freight component rises sharply.

How do you price Okoume versus standard packing-grade?

Standard packing-grade is on our published EX-factory price card with the fixed ₹2.50 per square foot margin. Okoume is priced separately on request because the face veneer pricing moves with the import market — we quote it order-wise with current landed-cost backing.
