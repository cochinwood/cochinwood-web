Plywood Supply to Mysuru

Cochin Wood Industries

Mysuru sits in an unusual sweet spot for the packing-plywood trade. It is a Tier-2 city with Tier-1 export weight — IT hardware moving out of Hebbal, coffee leaving for Hamburg and Trieste via Chennai and Mangalore ports, and a steady drumbeat of light engineering shipments out of the KIADB industrial estates. Each of those flows ends in a wooden box, a crate or a pallet, and that is where the Cochin Wood group has been quietly building its Mysuru book over the last few years. This guide walks through what we ship into the city, why our Kochi-to-Mysuru lane works on cost, and how to choose the right grade for your load.

The Mysuru industrial picture

Mysuru's packing demand is concentrated across a few well-defined belts, and the plywood specification changes meaningfully depending on which one you sit in.

Hebbal Industrial Area and the IT corridor

Hebbal is the city's anchor for IT hardware, electronics manufacturing and back-office infrastructure. The sector traditionally hosts large software and services campuses, and the spillover into hardware assembly, server-rack manufacture and electronic sub-assembly means a constant pull on close-tolerance crates — boxes where the plywood face has to take printing, the corners have to survive multi-modal handling, and moisture migration during a monsoon transit is a real concern.

Belagola, Metagalli and Hootagalli

These older KIADB estates carry the city's light and medium engineering load: pumps, electrical switchgear, auto components, machine-tool sub-assemblies. Crates here are heavier, often above 500 kg gross, and the spec sheet is dominated by ISPM-15 stamps for export and heat-treatment compliance for domestic rail movement.

Coffee and agri-export cluster

Mysuru is a national centre for coffee processing and roasting, supplied by the Kodagu and Chikkamagaluru estates upstream. Bulk coffee — green bean in jute bags, instant coffee in steel drums, specialty roasts in cartons — moves out in containerised loads that need either solid wooden pallets or composite plywood-deck pallets to keep cargo off the container floor through humid sea voyages.

Mysuru Aerospace and the upcoming corridor

The aerospace SEZ and the proposed Mysuru-Bengaluru expressway expansion are pulling in precision-engineering tenants. These buyers tend to want premium veneer faces, tighter dimensional tolerance and traceability on the plywood — a different conversation from the volume packing-case market.

What we ship into Mysuru

The Cochin Wood group manufactures through contract factories under CWI specifications, with group operations running in Perumbavoor since 1986. For the Mysuru lane, four product families do most of the work.

Packing-grade plywood (the workhorse)

Our MR-Packing grade is the default for box and crate manufacturers across Karnataka. Standard panel: 8 ft x 4 ft, thicknesses from 6 mm to 18 mm. The most common pulls into Mysuru are:

  • 6 mm and 8 mm for inner partitions, lids and lightweight cartons
  • 10 mm and 12 mm for general-purpose export boxes up to ~300 kg gross
  • 16 mm and 18 mm for heavy engineering crates, machinery skids and pallet decks

Glue line is phenolic-bonded for the BWR variant where moisture or sea transit is in the picture. Face veneers are typically commercial hardwood; Okoume and Gurjan face options are available on the same MR-Packing and BWR cores when the customer needs a cleaner printing surface or improved cosmetic finish.

Assembled crates and packing cases

For exporters who would rather receive a finished crate than build one in-house, we ship knocked-down (KD) or fully-assembled cases cut to internal-dimension drawings. ISPM-15 heat-treatment marking and fumigation certification are coordinated for export-bound consignments.

Wooden pallets

Two-way and four-way entry pallets in standard footprints — 1200 x 1000 mm and 1200 x 800 mm being the most-asked — with plywood-deck or sawn-timber-deck options. Pallet thickness and deck-board specification are matched to the load: 12 mm plywood deck for ~500 kg static loads, stepping up to 18 mm or solid timber for the 1-tonne-plus engineering crates.

Container flooring plywood

For the freight-forwarding and container-refurbishment trade, 28 mm IICL-spec container flooring sheets in 2400 x 1160 mm and similar formats are available on indent.

Which grade for which load?

The Mysuru order mix is broad enough that a single specification rarely fits. A working decision rule:

  1. MR-Packing grade — IT hardware crates, domestic-bound engineering crates, light-to-medium export boxes where the journey is overland or a short sea leg. This is 70-80 percent of our Mysuru volume.
  2. BWR (boiling-water-resistant) with Okoume or Gurjan face — long-haul sea export to Europe, GCC and ASEAN; cosmetic crates where the wood grain shows on the finished package; coffee and agri-export loads that sit in humid containers for 3-5 weeks.
  3. Shuttering ply — for the Mysuru construction sector, including the expressway projects and commercial real estate around the ring road. Film-faced shuttering plywood in 12 mm and 18 mm is the volume call.

If you are unsure, share the gross weight, the dispatch destination and the mode of transport, and we will reverse-engineer the lightest specification that still survives the journey. There is no commercial reason to over-engineer a domestic box with marine-grade plywood, and we will say so.

FAQ

Do you have a depot in Mysuru?

No — and that is a deliberate cost decision. We dispatch direct from the Perumbavoor manufacturing belt on a confirmed-order basis, which keeps overhead out of the per-sheet price. For repeat buyers we maintain a rolling production slot so lead time stays under a week.

What is the minimum order quantity?

For sheet plywood, we prefer to dispatch part-loads of 200 sheets and above to keep freight efficient; smaller orders are possible but the per-sheet landed cost climbs. For assembled crates and pallets, minimum is typically one full truckload to make the dispatch viable.

Can you handle ISPM-15 stamping and export documentation?

Yes — heat-treatment, fumigation, and the IPPC mark are coordinated through our compliance partners for export-bound consignments. Share the destination country and the consignee's wood-packaging requirements and we will build the specification accordingly.
