Plywood Supply to Dammam

Cochin Wood Industries

Dammam runs on heavy iron. Saudi Aramco's headquarters sits in Dhahran a few minutes inland, the Jubail petrochemical complex anchors the coast an hour north, and King Abdulaziz Port at Dammam handles the largest container throughput in the Arabian Gulf. Every valve skid, compressor module, pre-fabricated pipe spool, and spare-parts kit that moves into or out of this belt needs wooden packaging that survives sea salt, 48°C summers, and rough port handling. That is the conversation we have with packers and freight forwarders in the Eastern Province almost every week, and it is the reason the Cochin Wood group has built a steady FCL lane out of Kochi to Dammam.

The Dammam industrial picture

"Dammam" in shipping documents usually means one of four overlapping zones. Understanding which one a consignment is headed to changes the packing brief.

Dammam's industrial belts at a glance

  • Dammam 2nd & 3rd Industrial Cities (MODON) — metal fabrication, engineering workshops, MRO yards, electrical panel builders. Lots of mid-weight crates (200–2,000 kg) headed out as project cargo.
  • Jubail Industrial City (Jubail 1 & 2) — SABIC, Saudi Kayan, Petro Rabigh feed lines. Heavy petrochem equipment, catalyst drums, exchanger bundles. Crates here are big, weatherproof, and frequently ISPM-15 stamped for re-export.
  • Dhahran Techno Valley & Aramco service belt — oilfield services (drill bits, wireline tools, downhole equipment) shipped in returnable plywood cases that need to handle multiple field trips.
  • King Abdulaziz Port logistics zone — consolidators and 3PLs running container freight stations. Their daily diet is pallets, dunnage, container flooring for re-conditioning, and packing-grade ply by the bundle.

The sectors driving plywood demand are therefore upstream oil & gas services, downstream petrochem maintenance, power transformer movements between Aramco and contractor yards, and a healthy steady stream of food, beverage and white-goods crating linked to the broader Eastern Province consumer economy.

Crate construction notes

For Jubail-bound petrochem cargo we default to an 18 mm BWR-glued plywood shell on a 75×75 mm or 100×100 mm hardwood frame, corner-braced, with skid runners sized for the buyer's forklift truck (usually 7 t–15 t at Eastern Province yards). Every batten and runner is ISPM-15 heat-treated and stamped at source so the crate clears Saudi customs and is re-exportable to Bahrain, UAE, or onward to Europe without a second fumigation cycle.

Pallet sizes that actually move in Dammam

Eastern Province buyers split roughly between the 1200×1000 mm footprint (used by GCC retail and most consumer-goods distributors) and the 1100×1100 mm "Aramco-friendly" size that fits oilfield kit boxes and warehouse racks at contractor yards. We build both as 4-way-entry with a notched bottom deck so a hand pallet truck can pick from any side — small detail, but it is the one buyers call out most often.

Kochi to Dammam: the logistics picture

The sea distance from Cochin Port (Kochi) to King Abdulaziz Port (Dammam) via the Arabian Sea, Gulf of Oman, Strait of Hormuz and the Persian Gulf is roughly 1,900 nautical miles. Direct services are rare; almost all FCL bookings transship at Jebel Ali (UAE), and increasingly at Khalifa Port (Abu Dhabi) or Hamad (Qatar).

  • Port-to-port transit: typically 14–21 days from Cochin to Dammam depending on the transshipment window at Jebel Ali.
  • Door-to-port lead time from our factory cluster in Perumbavoor: 3–4 days for inland trucking to Cochin Port + customs and stuffing.
  • Realistic total lead time: plan 4–5 weeks from PO to vessel arrival at Dammam, plus the buyer's customs clearance.
  • Documentation: commercial invoice, packing list, Certificate of Origin, ISPM-15 declaration for wood packaging, and any SASO / SABER paperwork the buyer needs at the Saudi end.

One operational note: for Aramco-linked contractors we keep the ISPM-15 stamp visible on at least two faces of every crate and every pallet stringer. Inspectors at King Abdulaziz Port reject loads where the heat-treatment mark is on a hidden surface, even if the paperwork is in order.

Which grade for which Dammam job?

A simple decision rule we share with first-time Dammam buyers:

  1. Standard MR packing-grade plywood — default for inland-handled cargo, short-cycle crates, dunnage and case linings. Lowest landed cost.
  2. BWR packing-grade plywood — the right call for anything moving by sea container to the humid Gulf coast, anything that may sit at a port yard, and any returnable case used by oilfield services that bounces between Aramco fields and contractor workshops.
  3. Okoume-faced BWR plywood — chosen by exhibition logistics, instrumentation packers, and downstream petrochem buyers who want a clean light-coloured face on crates that go into client-facing locations. Higher price point; the face holds up better through repeat handling.
  4. Phenolic shuttering ply (film-faced) — used in Dammam mainly for container re-flooring and for crate floor panels under very heavy point loads (skid-mounted compressors, transformers).

Note on terminology: Okoume in our catalogue refers to the face veneer applied to a BWR-glued plywood, not a separate grade. Specify the glue (MR or BWR), the face veneer (commercial, Okoume or Gurjan), and the thickness. Our team can match a sample if the buyer is replacing an existing import line.

FAQ — Dammam buyers

Do you provide ISPM-15 certification with every shipment?

Yes. All wood packaging headed to Saudi Arabia is heat-treated and stamped at source, and the ISPM-15 declaration is included in the shipping documents. Stamps are placed on visible faces of crates, pallets and dunnage.

Can you ship LCL for trial orders?

For first-time Dammam buyers we routinely ship LCL through Jebel Ali consolidation for trial loads of 5–10 cbm. Once specs are locked, most buyers move to 20'/40' FCL on a recurring lane.

What is the minimum order quantity?

For plain sheet plywood, one 20' FCL (roughly 320–360 sheets of 18 mm depending on configuration) is the practical minimum. For ready-made crates and pallets we quote either by unit count or by the container, depending on what the buyer's project needs.
