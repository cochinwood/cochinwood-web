Plywood Supply to Khobar

Cochin Wood Industries

Khobar sits at the centre of Saudi Arabia's Eastern Province, the country's hydrocarbon engine. Between Dhahran, Dammam Port and the Aramco operating belt, this corridor moves more oilfield service equipment, valves, instrumentation skids and heavy spares than almost any other industrial cluster in the Gulf. Every piece of that equipment leaves a workshop in a crate, sits on a pallet, or travels on a container floor. That is where the demand for engineered wood packaging quietly comes from, and that is the trade Cochin Wood Industries ships into from Kochi.

The Khobar industrial picture

To understand the plywood need in Khobar, it helps to map the industrial belt the city anchors. Khobar is one corner of the Dammam–Dhahran–Khobar triangle, the densest manufacturing and oilfield-services cluster in the Kingdom. The buyers we encounter are clustered in a few specific zones.

Dammam Second Industrial City

This is the heavyweight estate of the Eastern Province. It hosts fabrication shops, oilfield equipment assemblers, downhole tool refurbishers, wellhead and Christmas-tree builders, valve manufacturers and instrumentation houses. These workshops crate up high-value equipment for Aramco contracts, for export to other GCC operators, and for back-shipment to OEM principals in Houston, Aberdeen and Stavanger. Crate demand here is constant, and the boxes are heavy — typically 800 kg to 6 tonnes per unit, occasionally more.

Dammam Third Industrial City and the Modon estates

The newer Modon-managed parks around Dammam, including the Third Industrial City and the satellite zones running west toward Abqaiq, house lighter fabrication, electrical panel builders, HVAC skid makers, pipe-coating yards and plastic/polymer converters. Crates here are mid-weight and high-volume — long, shallow boxes for piping spools and skid-mounted units.

King Abdulaziz Port (Dammam) and King Fahd Industrial Port (Jubail)

Khobar's industrial output ships through two ports. Dammam Port handles the general cargo, project cargo and break-bulk equipment. Jubail's industrial port, a short trucking lane north, handles petrochemicals and heavy project moves. Both ports drive a steady stream of one-trip crates, plus a recurring need for IPPC-compliant pallets for stuffing 20-foot and 40-foot containers.

Jubail Industrial City

Sixty-odd miles up the coast, Jubail is the petrochemical capital. Its buyers are catalyst handlers, instrumentation OEMs and spare-parts distributors who ship temperature- and shock-sensitive equipment in cushioned plywood crates with internal bracing. This belt also pulls in shuttering-grade plywood when expansion projects break ground.

What we ship into Khobar

The Cochin Wood group has been operating from Perumbavoor since 1986 — about forty years of group manufacturing. Cochin Wood Industries Pvt Ltd carries that production capacity into the Khobar lane through three main product families.

Most Eastern Province crate builders order plywood in sheet form and convert to boxes locally. Crates (built-to-spec, knocked down or assembled)

For buyers who prefer finished crates landed at Dammam, we build to drawing in Perumbavoor and ship either assembled or in knock-down form. Specifications are confirmed against the load: internal dimensions, gross weight, centre of gravity, lashing eyes, stencil markings and ISPM-15 heat-treatment for the softwood frame members. Standard configurations include cleated plywood boxes, framed crates with diagonal bracing, and skid-base crates for forklift handling.

Pallets and container flooring

Our pallets are built in Indian standard (1200 x 1000 mm), Euro (1200 x 800 mm) and US (1219 x 1016 mm) footprints, two-way or four-way entry, in hardwood or plywood-deck configurations. For Khobar exporters consolidating into containers, the 1200 x 1000 mm two-way pallet is the most-asked footprint. Container flooring boards — 18 mm packing-grade plywood, sized to fit the 20-foot and 40-foot floor — are shipped as flat-pack stacks.

Kochi-to-Khobar logistics

The shipping lane runs from Cochin Port (Kochi) to King Abdulaziz Port (Dammam), with Jebel Ali as the typical transhipment hub. Direct routings via Salalah are also used. The relevant numbers:

  • Sea distance: roughly 1,900 nautical miles, port-to-port
  • Transit time: 14–22 days door-to-port, depending on routing and consolidator
  • Trucking from Dammam Port to Khobar: 25–40 minutes, no border crossing
  • Container types: 20-foot dry, 40-foot dry, occasional 40-foot HC for larger crate stacks
  • Compliance: all softwood members are ISPM-15 heat-treated and stamped; documentation includes phytosanitary certificate, commercial invoice, packing list and Saudi conformity papers as relevant

For Khobar buyers we typically quote FOB Cochin or CIF Dammam — the call depends on whether the buyer is consolidating with other Indian-origin cargo. We can also quote ex-works Perumbavoor if the buyer's freight forwarder picks up at our yard.

Decision rules — which grade for which Khobar load

Not every crate needs premium veneer. A short rule-of-thumb we use when quoting:

  • Packing-grade (MR, commercial hardwood): the default. Use for oilfield service spares, valve crates, instrumentation boxes, general industrial export. Cost-efficient and tough enough for one-way sea movement.
  • Okoume face-veneer (on packing or BWR substrate): use when the buyer's spec asks for a clean export face, or when the crate goes through multiple handling legs and visual presentation matters. Common on equipment heading from Khobar onward to European OEMs.
  • BWR / shuttering-grade plywood: use when the load is humidity-exposed for long periods, when crates may sit outdoors at the port, or when the buyer is on the construction side rather than the equipment side. The Jubail expansion projects pull in shuttering ply directly.

For most Khobar oilfield service buyers, MR-packing grade with 12 mm walls and 18 mm base, on a 4-way pallet, lands the right balance of cost and protection.

FAQ

Do you handle ISPM-15 compliance for Saudi entry?

Yes. All softwood members in our crates and pallets are heat-treated and stamped to ISPM-15. The plywood faces are exempt under the standard since they are engineered panels, but the load-bearing framework carries the mark.

Can you supply in knocked-down (flat-pack) form to save container space?

Yes. For Khobar buyers shipping in 20-foot containers, knocked-down crates with pre-drilled cleats and a numbered assembly sheet are a common request. We supply the fasteners separately, packed by crate.

What is the minimum order quantity for a Khobar shipment?

The practical minimum is one 20-foot container — roughly 280 sheets of 12 mm plywood, or about 60–80 medium crates flat-packed. Smaller volumes are possible through a freight consolidator, but the unit economics are usually better at one full container.
