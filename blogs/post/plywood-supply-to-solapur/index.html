Plywood Supply to Solapur

Cochin Wood Industries

Why Solapur sits in our daily despatch register

Solapur is not a glamour city in the industrial press, but anyone moving plywood in western India keeps a column for it. The district runs on three engines that all need wood-based packaging: the cotton-textile cluster around the Solapur MIDC and Chincholi belt, the beedi-tobacco trade that pushes processed leaf out by the truckload, and a growing agri-equipment and oil-engine vertical sitting along NH-65 toward Pune. Add the cement, foundry and granite work around Tuljapur Road and you have a steady call for packing-grade plywood, crates, pallets and the occasional container floor. This note walks through what we ship into Solapur from the Cochin Wood group's Perumbavoor base, how the lane works, and which grade fits which load.

The Solapur industrial picture

The city's manufacturing footprint is wider than the textile reputation suggests. Buyers from these clusters routinely call our desk asking for packing-grade plywood by the bundle:

  • Solapur MIDC (Akkalkot Road) and Chincholi MIDC — textile processing, terry-towel weaving, bedsheet finishing, and ancillary engineering. Bale packing, finished-goods cartons over wooden pallets, and crates for export terry-towel consignments are the recurring asks.
  • Beedi and tobacco trade in the old city and Barshi belt — processed-leaf cases, plywood-lined wooden boxes for despatch to UP, Karnataka and the GCC, and pallets for warehouse stacking.
  • Agri-equipment and oil-engine units along NH-65 toward Mohol and Pandharpur — heavy machine crates with ply lids and shooks, full-frame export boxes for diesel engines and water-pump sets, and ISPM-15 treated pallets.
  • Foundry, granite and cement ancillaries near Hotgi and Tuljapur Road — heavy-duty pallets for casting movement, occasional shuttering ply for in-plant civil work.
  • Cold storage and grape/pomegranate exporters in the wider Solapur-Pandharpur belt — crates and pallet runners for outbound containers headed to JNPT.

The common thread is bulk, repeat, and price-sensitive. Solapur buyers know their per-sheet rates to the rupee, and they expect a packing grade that holds nails, takes a screw, and survives a 1,400 km road-haul to the port without delamination.

What we ship into Solapur

Packing-grade plywood (commercial / rubberwood)

The workhorse. Most Solapur crate-makers and bale-packing units run on 8 mm, 12 mm and 18 mm sheets. Our packing range is poplar-core and rubberwood-faced where needed, calibrated for nail-holding and saw-cut clean edges. Standard size 8 ft x 4 ft, with 6 ft x 3 ft and odd cuts available on bundle orders. Indicative ex-factory selling rates (₹/sq.ft, GST and freight extra): 6 mm at 24, 8 mm at 28, 10 mm at 31.5, 12 mm at 33.5, 14 mm at 36, 15 mm at 39, 16 mm at 39.5, 18 mm at 42. These are packing-grade numbers, not furniture grade — buyers comparing against a BWR brand quote should expect the gap.

Okoume plywood

Premium grade, priced on request. We send Okoume into Solapur mainly for export crates where the consignee specifies a hardwood face — typically agri-equipment headed to Africa or the GCC, and high-value engineering goods where the outer face needs to take a stencil or screen-printed shipping mark cleanly. Lighter than gurjan-faced ply at comparable thicknesses, which matters when the crate plus cargo is fighting a road-freight weight limit.

Crates, full boxes and shooks

For Solapur loads we usually quote three formats: full assembled crates for one-shot exporters, knock-down shook sets where the buyer's own carpenter assembles on the shop floor, and ply tops/lids supplied separately when the buyer is using sawn-timber framing locally. Beedi-trade boxes tend to be small and standardised; agri-equipment crates are one-off and CAD-driven.

Pallets

Two-way and four-way entry, 1200 x 800 and 1200 x 1000 mm being the most common. ISPM-15 heat treatment with the stamp is available on the sawn-timber components for export-bound pallets — non-negotiable for anything headed to JNPT for an EU or GCC container.

Container flooring

Apitong / keruing container floorboards on request, mainly for the trailer-body fabricators in the wider Maharashtra belt rather than Solapur city itself. Quoted by the lot.

Sawn timber

Jackwood hardwood runners at ₹400/cft (¼ inch minus tolerance) and rubberwood runners to size — these go alongside ply bundles for buyers building crates in-house.

The Kochi-to-Solapur lane

Solapur sits about 900 km from Kochi by road via NH-66 up the Karnataka coast, then NH-50 / NH-65 inland through Hubballi and Vijayapura. Practical road-freight transit is three to four days door-to-door for a single full-truck consignment, longer if the buyer is consolidating a part-load. We despatch ex-Perumbavoor (Ernakulam district), which sits about 30 km inland from Cochin port and feeds directly onto NH-544 for the Karnataka leg. Full-truck economics work best at 18-20 tonnes of finished plywood, which lines up to roughly 280-340 sheets of 18 mm packing grade, or proportionally more at lower thicknesses. For Solapur buyers who cannot absorb a full truck, we consolidate with other Maharashtra-bound loads heading to Pune, Sangli or Kolhapur. Port-to-port via Cochin and JNPT is an option for export-bound crates that the buyer wants delivered already-stuffed, but the road lane wins on cost and time for raw plywood and pallet stock.

FAQ

Minimum order to despatch to Solapur?

We prefer full-truck loads of around 18-20 tonnes for the best per-sheet landed cost. Part-loads are possible when we are consolidating other Maharashtra deliveries in the same week — ask the desk what is moving.

Are the sheets ISI-marked or BWP-grade?

The packing range is graded for packaging use, not BWP/IS:303 furniture grade. If a buyer needs BWP/BWR for a specific application, that is a separate quote — speak to us about Okoume or our BWR line.

How are payment and freight handled?

Standard practice is part-advance against PI, balance against LR copy at despatch. Freight is quoted either inclusive (door-delivered Solapur) or ex-Perumbavoor, depending on whether the buyer has their own preferred transporter on the lane.
