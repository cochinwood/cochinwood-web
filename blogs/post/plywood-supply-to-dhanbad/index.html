Plywood Supply to Dhanbad

Cochin Wood Industries

Why Dhanbad's industrial belt needs plywood from down south

Dhanbad is shorthand for coal in India. Bharat Coking Coal Limited (BCCL) is headquartered here, Eastern Coalfields Limited (ECL) operates immediately adjacent in the Asansol-Raniganj belt, and the city anchors a deep cluster of coal washeries, mining-equipment OEMs, refractory makers and heavy fabrication shops feeding the steel corridors at Bokaro, Jamshedpur and Durgapur. Every one of those activities throws off a downstream packing requirement — long-tail mining spares for export, drawcut conveyor parts going to a project site in Odisha, refractory shapes on pallets for a Bhilai despatch, transformer accessories crated for a port load-out. That is the plywood demand the Cochin Wood group ships into Dhanbad from Kerala.

The Dhanbad industrial picture

The buying universe around Dhanbad sits in a few recognisable clusters. We size our packing-grade plywood, crates and pallets against what these clusters actually move.

Coal and mining majors

BCCL (a Coal India subsidiary) runs washeries, workshops and central stores across the Jharia coalfield. ECL extends the same demand pattern westwards into Asansol-Raniganj. Both buy packing material indirectly through their vendor base — mining-equipment dealers and spares packers who crate dragline parts, conveyor idlers, gear assemblies and electrical panels for despatch to project sites and overseas refurbishment lines.

Mining machinery and heavy fabrication

Dhanbad and the contiguous Govindpur / Nirsa belt host fabrication shops supplying coal-handling plants, crushers, hydraulic roof supports and conveyor systems. The Indian School of Mines (IIT-ISM) ecosystem has also pulled in a steady mining-tech vendor base. Export crates for this segment are the highest-spec work we do for the region — ISPM-15 heat-treated timber framing, plywood skinning that holds shape on a 30-day sea leg, and pallets sized to the equipment's actual centre of gravity rather than a standard footprint.

Refractories, steel-corridor feeders and ancillaries

Dhanbad's proximity to the Bokaro Steel Plant (~50 km) and onward to Jamshedpur and Durgapur means a steady stream of refractory bricks, alloy castings and rolled product moves through pallet-and-strap operations. These are typically 8-12mm plywood-topped pallets rather than full crates.

Okoume plywood for export-grade crates

For mining machinery going overseas — especially the refurbished dragline parts and crusher assemblies that ship to Africa and Southeast Asia — Okoume is a step up in face cleanliness, dimensional stability and humidity tolerance. We quote Okoume separately from the commercial card; it is the right call when the crate has to survive a coastal warehouse stint and a buyer inspection at the other end.

Crates, pallets and container flooring

Standard pallet sizes we run for the region are 1200x1000 mm and 1200x800 mm, with custom decks for odd-footprint equipment. Container flooring — the plywood sheet that goes down inside a 20'/40' container to spread load and protect machinery during handling — is typically 18 mm packing-grade ply cut to container ID. Crate framing uses jackwood or rubberwood runners; we quote jackwood at ₹400/cft and rubberwood by size.

Shuttering ply

For infra and EPC contractors working coal-handling plant civils or substation builds in the wider belt, shuttering ply is supplied film-faced for repeat pours.

Kochi to Dhanbad logistics

Dhanbad sits roughly 1,950 km from Kochi by road via the NH-44/NH-19 corridor through Bengaluru-Hyderabad-Nagpur-Raipur-Ranchi. Practical lane economics:

  • Mode: Direct truck (32-ft single-axle or 22-ft taurus) is the default for plywood loads. A full-truck consignment of 6-18 mm sheets moves cleanly on a single lane.
  • Transit time: 6-8 days door-to-door, weather and seasonal road closures permitting. The Ranchi-Dhanbad final leg adds half a day.
  • Sea + rail option: For very large repeat orders, FOB Cochin to Haldia/Kolkata by coastal vessel, then road into Dhanbad (approximately 280 km), can come out cheaper per CBM. We size this against the truck quote on each enquiry.
  • Documentation: E-way bills, GST invoicing and inter-state movement papers are issued from Kochi. ISPM-15 stamping is done at the packing-case stage where required.

Decision rules — which grade for which Dhanbad load

  1. Domestic mining-spares crates (BCCL/ECL vendor jobs, regional fabricators): Commercial packing-grade plywood, 12-16 mm walls, 18 mm deck. The packing card rates apply.
  2. Export crates for refurbished mining machinery: Okoume face, 12-18 mm depending on weight class. Quote separately; the face quality and dimensional stability are what the overseas buyer is paying for.
  3. Refractory and steel-ancillary pallets: 8-12 mm packing-grade top sheet, hardwood runners. High-cycle, low-spec.
  4. Coal-handling plant shuttering and substation civils: Film-faced shuttering ply, not packing grade.
  5. Container flooring for outbound machinery containers: 18 mm packing-grade ply, cut to container ID, supplied loose.

FAQ

Do you supply ISPM-15 heat-treated crates for mining exports from Dhanbad?

Yes. ISPM-15 stamping is standard on export crates we build. Heat-treated timber framing with plywood skinning is the spec for mining-machinery and electrical-assembly exports going through Kolkata, Haldia, Nhava Sheva or Chennai.

Can you handle repeat monthly volumes for a Dhanbad packer?

Yes. We run monthly programmes for packing-case houses across India. Pricing is locked against a forecast volume; despatch is scheduled against the customer's truck plan. The right starting point is a thickness-and-quantity sheet by SKU.

Which is more economical for Dhanbad — truck from Kochi or coastal sea via Haldia?

Truck wins on speed and small-to-medium volumes (one to two trucks per consignment). Coastal sea + Haldia road can win on large repeat lots, typically four-plus truck-equivalents per shipment. We benchmark both on each enquiry.
