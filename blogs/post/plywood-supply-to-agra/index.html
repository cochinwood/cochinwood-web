Plywood Supply to Agra

Cochin Wood Industries

Agra is one of those cities where the freight bill writes itself: the world's second-largest footwear cluster, a leather goods belt that exports to Europe and the GCC, and a marble inlay handicraft trade that ships fragile, high-value stone to buyers across continents. Every one of those flows ends the same way — boxed, crated, palletised, and lashed inside a container. That is the part of Agra's economy we sit inside. This post lays out what the Cochin Wood group ships to Agra, why, and how the Kochi-to-Agra lane actually works.

The Agra industrial picture

Agra district carries roughly four packaging-relevant clusters worth naming. Get the cluster right and the plywood spec almost picks itself.

Footwear belt (Hing ki Mandi, Sikandra, EPIP Shastripuram)

Agra accounts for an outsized share of India's footwear output — the city is widely cited as the second-largest footwear hub in the country after Chennai. Production runs from the dense Hing ki Mandi cluster through Sikandra industrial area and the EPIP (Export Promotion Industrial Park) at Shastripuram. Larger exporters operate in and around the Agra–Mathura belt, with finishing and packing happening in the city. The packing brief here is high-volume, low-individual-weight cartons: shoes go out in inner cartons stacked into master cartons, master cartons stacked onto pallets, pallets stuffed into 20-ft or 40-ft containers bound for JNPT/Nhava Sheva or, for GCC moves, sometimes Mundra.

Leather goods and tanneries (Nunhai, Agra trans-Yamuna)

Adjacent to the footwear belt, leather goods and small-leather manufacturing run their own packing line — wallets, belts, bags. Cartons are smaller and lighter than footwear masters, but volumes per consolidation are large and the buyer is usually the same exporter who needs the bigger footwear pack.

Marble inlay and handicraft (Tajganj, Kinari Bazaar, Fatehabad Road)

The Pietra Dura marble inlay trade is the opposite problem: low units, very high value, very fragile, often awkward shapes. Tabletops, plaques, decorative panels, occasional architectural pieces. This is custom crating work — bespoke wooden cases, internal bracing, sometimes corner protection and shock indicators. Plywood spec here is unforgiving: the case has to hold the piece through a multi-modal journey without flexing.

Engineering and auto-component spillover

Agra's industrial estates also house engineering and auto-ancillary units, and the wider Agra–Mathura–Aligarh axis pulls in foundry and lock-hardware buyers. For these we see standard heavy-engineering crating: thicker ply, framed sides, sometimes ISPM-15 stamped pallets for sea export.

What we ship to Agra

Cochin Wood Industries supplies the Agra buyer base across four product lines. We are a plywood manufacturer working through contract factories under Cochin Wood specifications, with the Cochin Wood group operating in Perumbavoor since 1986 — forty years of manufacturing inside a single tight cluster. The product list:

Packing-grade plywood (commercial / rubberwood)

Our packing range covers 6 mm to 18 mm in standard 8x4 ft sheets, with intermediate stops at 8, 10, 12, 14, 15, 16 mm. For Agra footwear and leather exporters, the typical pull is 8 mm and 10 mm — light enough to keep the carton tare down, stiff enough to survive stacking. Marble handicraft crating usually steps up to 12 mm or 18 mm for the side panels and base. We hold MR (moisture resistant) as the default for the packing line; for monsoon-window shipments or sea-export crates we move buyers to our BWR grade.

Made-to-spec wooden crates and cases

Bespoke crating is built out of our packing ply plus jackwood or rubberwood runners. Jackwood runs ₹400/cft at quarter-inch-minus tolerance; rubberwood runners price as per size. For the marble inlay trade we build framed cases with internal cleating and corner blocks; for footwear and leather we more often supply flat-pack ply sheets the exporter's in-house carpenters convert to cases on site.

Pallets, including ISPM-15 compliant

Standard four-way entry pallets in 1200x1000, 1200x800 (euro), and custom sizes. ISPM-15 heat-treatment stamping is mandatory for sea-export pallets going to most jurisdictions — we coordinate this through our packing-line suppliers in Kerala and ship pre-stamped where the buyer needs it.

Container flooring

Heavy-duty plywood flooring for container repair and reefer rebuilds — typically 18 mm BWR or marine-grade, hardwood-faced, 28 mm thickness for full container floor replacements. This is a smaller flow into Agra than the packing line but shows up periodically through CHA-linked rebuild yards.

Kochi to Agra logistics

The road distance Kochi/Ernakulam to Agra is roughly 2,300 km via NH 44 and the Mumbai–Agra corridor. Typical road transit for a full truckload is 5 to 7 days door-to-door depending on driver pairing and checkpoint delays through Maharashtra and Madhya Pradesh. Sample lane economics:

For Agra buyers who export through JNPT/Nhava Sheva, we can also route from Kochi directly to Mumbai-area packing-line consolidators and skip the Agra leg entirely — useful for exporters whose Agra unit only does production and ships through a Bhiwandi or Panvel consolidator.

Decision rules — which plywood for which Agra load

A practical mapping for buyers comparing grades:

  • Footwear master cartons stacked on pallets, dry-warehouse to dry-container: our MR packing grade, 8 mm or 10 mm, is the right answer. Okoume is overspec.
  • Leather goods cartons going air-freight or premium-export route: the buyer often asks for Okoume-faced ply because the customer's QA wants a clean blonde face. Quote on request.
  • Marble inlay export crates, sea route, GCC or EU destination: BWR-grade packing ply 12-18 mm with jackwood framing and ISPM-15 pallets. The crate is structural, not cosmetic.
  • Engineering and auto-component crates for sea export: 18 mm BWR for base and floor, 12 mm sides, hardwood runners, ISPM-15 stamped.
  • Shuttering / formwork for an Agra construction project: different conversation entirely — film-faced shuttering ply, separate spec sheet, separate quote.

For every grade, our selling rule is purchase rate plus ₹2.50/sq.ft, ex-factory, GST and transport extra. Indicative MR-grade packing prices today: 8 mm at ₹28, 10 mm at ₹31.50, 12 mm at ₹33.50, 18 mm at ₹42 per sq.ft. Okoume and BWR are quoted separately.

FAQ for Agra buyers

Do you supply directly to Agra or through a North India distributor?

Direct from Kerala. We dispatch from our Perumbavoor cluster on full-truckload basis to the buyer's Agra address — EPIP Shastripuram, Sikandra, Nunhai, Tajganj, or any of the surrounding industrial pockets. No North India distributor layer.

Can you supply ISPM-15 stamped pallets and crates ready for sea export?

Yes. We coordinate heat-treatment and ISPM-15 stamping through our packing-line partners. Lead time adds about 2-3 working days to a standard pallet order.

What's the minimum order for a trial dispatch to Agra?

For a road dispatch, the truckload economics improve sharply above 18-20 tons. Below that we can route through part-load networks but the per-sheet freight roughly doubles. For first-time Agra buyers we usually recommend either a full-truck trial or a co-load with another North India order in the same week.
