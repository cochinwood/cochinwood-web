Plywood Supply to Kanpur

Cochin Wood Industries

Kanpur runs on three industrial currents that all end in a wooden box: finished leather goods bound for European and US buyers, ordnance and defense components going to military depots across India, and fertilizer and chemical drums moving inland by rail. Each of those flows generates a steady demand for packing-grade plywood, ISPM-15 crates, four-way pallets and container flooring. We ship those four products into Kanpur on a regular basis from our Kochi/Ernakulam operations, and this page walks through what actually leaves our gate, how it travels the 2,300 km north, and which grade fits which load.

The Kanpur industrial picture

Kanpur is the leather capital of India. The Jajmau cluster on the south bank of the Ganga concentrates more than 350 tanneries and finishing units, and the goods that leave Jajmau — saddlery, full-grain leather garments, industrial gloves, shoe uppers — are almost all crated in plywood before they hit a 20-ft container at ICD Panki or move overland to Nhava Sheva and Mundra. Beyond leather, the city carries weight in defense and ordnance:

  • Ordnance Factory Kanpur (OFK) and Small Arms Factory (SAF), Kanpur — manufacture small arms, ammunition and field equipment that ship to depots in ISPM-15-compliant crates.
  • Field Gun Factory and Ordnance Equipment Factory, Hazratpur — large, heavy castings and assemblies needing custom-built skids and crates.
  • Panki Industrial Area — fertilizer (IFFCO Aonla is close, Krishak Bharati and Tata Chemicals operate in the region), engineering, and pharma.
  • Dada Nagar and Fazalganj industrial estates — hosiery, textile machinery, auto components, and a long bench of engineering job-shops.
  • UPSIDC Site IV and V at Panki and the Jajmau Leather Cluster (CETP zone) — concentrations of MSME exporters who order plywood by the truckload.

Add to that the Kanpur–Lucknow corridor on NH-27 and the upcoming Defence Industrial Corridor node at Sandila, and you get a city whose packaging demand is denser than its sticker would suggest.

What we ship into Kanpur

Packing-grade plywood (commercial / rubberwood)

This is the workhorse and most of what moves to Kanpur. We supply 8 ft × 4 ft sheets in the standard packing thicknesses — 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 15, 16 and 18 mm. Leather exporters and engineering crate-makers in Dada Nagar typically buy 8 mm and 10 mm for the walls and lids and step up to 18 mm for the bottom skid where a 250–500 kg load sits over a forklift slot. Rates are EX-FACTORY, GST and freight extra; we quote against your size and monthly draw rather than off a printed list.

ISPM-15 export crates

For Jajmau leather exporters and OFK shipments leaving the country, ISPM-15 heat-treated, IPPC-stamped crates are mandatory. We build to your packing list — knock-down (KD) for inland freight efficiency, or pre-assembled where the buyer takes delivery and packs in-house. Standard wall is 12 mm packing ply on a 50 × 75 mm rubberwood or hardwood frame; bottoms run 18 mm over 75 × 100 mm bearers for forklift entry. IPPC marking and treatment certificate travel with each crate.

Wooden pallets

Four-way entry, perimeter-base pallets in the standard 1200 × 1000 mm and 1200 × 800 mm Euro footprint, plus 1100 × 1100 mm for containers heading to GCC and Vietnam buyers via Kanpur consolidators. Deckboards are 18 mm packing ply or 22 mm sawn rubberwood depending on dynamic load. Heat-treated stamping available on order.

Container flooring plywood

28 mm phenolic-bonded film-faced ply in the 2400 × 1200 mm container floor size, for reefers and dry vans being refurbished at workshops around Panki and along the Lucknow–Kanpur highway. Edges are sealed, surface is anti-skid film. We ship in bundle quantities; minimum order is typically a full truck.

Kochi to Kanpur — the route

The lane runs 2,300–2,400 km depending on origin point inside the Perumbavoor cluster. Two practical options:

ModeRouteTransitWhen it makes sense
Road (32-ft SXL / 22-ft)Perumbavoor → Salem → Bangalore → Hyderabad → Nagpur → Jhansi → Kanpur6–8 daysCrates, pallets, mixed loads under 18 tonnes, or buyers without ICD access
Rail + last-mileCochin Port / Ernakulam → ICD Panki via container rake10–12 daysFull-container plywood orders to Panki, Dada Nagar; cheaper per sheet at scale

For most Kanpur MSME orders we quote FOR (freight on road) and put a 32-ft single-axle on the route with a 14–18 tonne payload. Larger draws — 4,000+ sheets a month, typical of mid-size packing-case houses — work better through Cochin Port to ICD Panki. We can also originate from FOB Cochin for buyers nominating their own forwarder.

Which grade fits which load

Quick decision rules we use when a Kanpur buyer calls in:

  • Domestic dispatch, light-to-medium loads (under 200 kg) — commercial packing-grade plywood, 8–12 mm walls, 15 mm base. Most Jajmau leather garment exports, hosiery from Fazalganj, auto components from Dada Nagar fit this bracket.
  • Export crates, ISPM-15, sea freight — same grade but heat-treated and IPPC-stamped, with the base stepped up to 18 mm. This is the default for Jajmau finished leather, saddlery, and ordnance components moving via Nhava Sheva.
  • Heavy castings, defense and ordnance equipment (over 500 kg) — BWR-grade or premium packing ply at 18 mm on a hardwood frame; for the largest pieces leaving Hazratpur or Field Gun Factory we recommend a sawn-timber skid base with plywood superstructure.
  • Okoume face-veneer (on MR-Packing or BWR grades) — when the carton aesthetics matter for branded leather exports or premium gift packing. Okoume is a face-veneer option, not a separate grade, and is quoted on request.
  • Concrete formwork on Kanpur Metro extensions and infra projects — film-faced shuttering plywood, 12 or 18 mm, sold separately from the packing range.

FAQ

What is the typical lead time from order confirmation to truck arrival in Kanpur?

For a single truck of packing-grade sheets or pallets, count 10–14 days door to door — that is 2–3 days from PI confirmation to material at our loading point, then 6–8 days transit. ISPM-15 crates built to a specific packing list take longer because of the build cycle; budget 15–18 days. Container flooring is typically available within a week if the size is standard 2400 × 1200 × 28 mm.

Do you offer ISPM-15 heat treatment and IPPC stamping?

Yes — for every export crate and pallet we ship to Jajmau or OFK that needs sea-freight clearance. The treatment certificate travels with the consignment and the IPPC mark is burned onto two opposite faces of each unit. We do not ship untreated wood as "export-ready" — that is where DG-shipping rejections start.

Can you handle a recurring monthly draw rather than one-off orders?

Yes, and that is most of what we move on the Kanpur lane. Mid-size packing-case houses and leather exporters typically commit to a monthly sheet count and a fixed price for the quarter, with deliveries staggered as call-offs. That smooths the truck-load economics and protects against rate swings in the rubberwood market.
