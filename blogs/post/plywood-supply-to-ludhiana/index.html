Plywood Supply to Ludhiana

Cochin Wood Industries

Why Ludhiana matters for a Kochi plywood operation

Ludhiana is one of the most export-dense industrial cities in northern India. The Focal Point belt alone packs out hosiery and woollens that ship into Europe, North America and the Gulf every season; the bicycle cluster around Gill Road and Industrial Area A sends CKD kits into Africa, Latin America and Southeast Asia; and the auto-components ring on the GT Road side feeds Tier-1 suppliers across Pune, Chennai and Manesar. Each of those flows ends the same way at the warehouse door — goods need to leave Ludhiana in a wooden crate, on a wooden pallet, or inside a container with a plywood floor that can take a forklift. That is where the Cochin Wood group fits in, even at a 2,400 km distance. Plywood is light per cubic foot, the truck lane from Kerala into Punjab is mature, and the volumes Ludhiana exporters move justify a planned-supply relationship rather than spot buys from the local mandi.

The Ludhiana industrial picture

Before getting into specs, it is worth being concrete about who actually buys plywood-based packaging in this city. The buyer profile is not one homogeneous market — it splits across at least four belts, each with its own packing logic.

Focal Point (Phases I–VIII) — hosiery and woollens

Focal Point is the workhorse industrial estate of Ludhiana, running across multiple phases east and south-east of the city. The dominant tenants are hosiery manufacturers, woollen and knitwear units, and the dye-houses and finishing units that support them. Their export packing requirement is fairly specific: light-weight crates and cartons that have to survive container stuffing at ICD Dhandari Kalan or Sahnewal and then a long sea leg through Mundra or JNPT. The wood content is usually a plywood lid + base with sawn timber cleats, or full plywood crates for higher-value woollen garments and made-ups.

Industrial Area A, B and Gill Road — bicycle and bicycle parts

Ludhiana's bicycle cluster is internationally known. CKD (Completely Knocked Down) bicycle exports are the high-volume use case here — frames, forks, rims, hubs and components packed flat into multi-layer crates designed to fit a 20' or 40' container's internal dimensions exactly. This is one of the most plywood-intensive packaging segments in India: a single export order can pull hundreds of crates a week, and consistency of thickness and core quality matters because the crates get re-stacked five and six high during ocean transit.

GT Road and Doraha belt — auto components and forgings

Auto-component, forging and fastener units, especially toward Doraha and Khanna, ship dense, heavy goods. Their crates need thicker plywood walls, heavier sawn-timber framing and properly nailed bases. They also consume a meaningful volume of pallets — both standard 1200×800 mm Euro and 1200×1000 mm "industrial" sizes — to handle inbound steel and outbound finished parts on forklifts.

Sewing-machine, hand-tools and small machinery

Ludhiana still has a strong sewing-machine and hand-tools base. These units typically run smaller export crates with mid-range packing-grade plywood lids and bases, and consume sawn timber for cleats and edge protection.

Crate construction patterns

Three patterns cover most of what Ludhiana actually builds: open crates with a ply lid + ply base + sawn-timber sides (cheapest, used for non-fragile woollens and knitwear); closed plywood cases with 6–9 mm ply on all six faces over a hardwood frame (the bicycle CKD standard); and heavy nailed crates with 12–15 mm ply walls and 18 mm bases for forgings and machinery. We supply the plywood and sawn timber as raw inputs; the assembly happens at the packer or in-house at the exporter.

Pallets and container flooring

Pallet demand from Ludhiana is split between the auto-components belt (industrial sizes for forklift handling) and the hosiery belt (lighter pallets for warehouse stacking and stuffing). For container flooring — replacement plywood when an existing ISO container's floor has rotted or the operator wants to convert to a flush plywood floor — 18 mm BWR is the standard call.

Kochi-to-Ludhiana logistics

The lane is mature and we plan around it rather than against it.

  • Distance: roughly 2,400 km by road from Kochi/Ernakulam to Ludhiana.
  • Mode: primarily 32-foot multi-axle trucks, full-truck-load. A standard 32-ft truck takes a useful 8'×4' plywood payload of meaningful volume depending on thickness mix — we plan the loading sheet thickness-by-thickness to maximise it.
  • Transit: 6–8 days door-to-door is the realistic working window, longer in monsoon and during Punjab's harvest-season trucker shortage.
  • Route: Kochi → Bengaluru/Salem belt → Maharashtra → MP → Rajasthan → Punjab. Toll, diesel and detention build the freight component.
  • Order economics: for Ludhiana, full-truck loads make sense; LTL/part-loads from Kerala are slow and unreliable. We typically advise customers to consolidate a month's requirement into one or two trucks.

All rates are quoted ex-factory; GST and transport extra — once destination, exact size mix and monthly quantity are confirmed, we build the proforma against an actual lane rate rather than a thumb-rule.

Decision rules — which grade for which load

Use commercial / rubberwood packing-grade when…

The crate is one-way export, the goods are moderately fragile but not delicate (knitwear, woollens, hardware, bicycle CKD without premium branding), and you are buying in volume. This is the workhorse SKU for Ludhiana and covers the majority of Focal Point demand.

Use Okoume-faced when…

The lid or visible face matters — branded woollens, premium bicycle exports, anything where the buyer at the other end opens the crate in a showroom or distributor warehouse and surface appearance reflects on the brand. Okoume is also lighter for the same thickness, which helps when ocean freight is charged by weight.

Use BWR / shuttering ply when…

Crates will be reused, are exposed to outdoor staging in monsoon, carry forging or heavy-machinery loads, or you are doing container floor replacement. BWR is the moisture-and-load answer; it is not the right call for light hosiery cases where you are paying for performance you do not need.

FAQ

What is the minimum order for shipping from Kochi to Ludhiana?

Full-truck-load is the economical unit on this lane. Below FTL, freight per sheet rises sharply and transit times stretch unpredictably through transhipment hubs. Most Ludhiana buyers consolidate a 3–4 week requirement into one truck.

How are payment and delivery handled at this distance?

Typical sequence: WhatsApp or phone qualification (thickness, size, monthly volume, delivery point), proforma invoice from us with ex-factory + transport + GST broken out, advance against PI, dispatch, balance against POD/lorry receipt. Detention at Ludhiana drop-off is on the buyer's account.

Do you supply finished crates, or only raw plywood and timber?

We are a plywood and sawn-timber supplier. The crate assembly itself is best handled at a local Ludhiana fabricator close to the packing line — both for cost and for handling rework. We supply the raw inputs in the exact thickness, size and grade mix the fabricator needs.
