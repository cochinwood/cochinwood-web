Plywood Supply to Panipat

Cochin Wood Industries

Panipat is one of north India's busiest packaging-consumption clusters that very few south-Indian mills actively service. The city's economy runs on three load-heavy verticals — cotton and woollen textiles, an IOCL refinery on its western edge, and the NTPC thermal corridor a short haul east — and every one of them needs wood. Bales need ply caps, refinery spares need ISPM-15 crates, turbine consumables travel on hardwood pallets. This guide walks through what the Cochin Wood group ships into Panipat from Kochi, why the 2,300-km lane still works on price, and how to pick the right grade for the typical loads moving out of Haryana.

The Panipat industrial picture

Panipat's industrial footprint is unusually concentrated. Within roughly 15 km of the city centre you find four distinct buyer pockets, each with its own packing rhythm:

  • Panipat Textile Cluster & HSIIDC Sector 29 — the famous "handloom city" power-loom belt: bedsheets, blankets, furnishings, towels, durries. Bale-pressing is continuous, and the export-grade fraction is the part that actually consumes plywood (compressed bales need a top-and-bottom ply cap plus end boards to survive sea travel).
  • Old industrial area & Sector 25 HSIIDC — the woollen and shoddy-yarn mills concentrated around the GT Road, plus the recycled-textile units that ship to Africa and the Middle East. Volumes are smaller per consignment but the frequency is high.
  • IOCL Panipat Refinery & petrochemical complex on the Baholi side — a continuous flow of MRO spares, pumps, valves and exchanger tubes that need close-tolerance crates, often ISPM-15 marked for re-export of warranty returns.
  • NTPC Panipat thermal station at Asan Khurd and the surrounding ancillary belt — boiler-tube bundles, coal-handling spares, transformer accessories. Loads are heavy, point-loaded, and need hardwood-bottom pallets rather than soft-pine substitutes.

Add to that the National Highway 44 logistics corridor that puts Panipat 90 km from ICD Tughlakabad and 100 km from ICD Sonipat, and you have a city that pushes a steady volume of export-spec packaging through its gates every week.

2. Wooden crates and ISPM-15 export cases

For the IOCL refinery belt and any Panipat exporter pushing engineering goods out through Mundra, Pipavav or Nhava Sheva, we build closed and open-frame crates to ISPM-15 specification — heat-treated timber, HT stamp, fumigation certificate where the destination requires it. Standard build uses a hardwood skid base, plywood sheathing on the four walls and lid, and steel strapping. Internal blocking and load-spreaders are designed against the actual equipment dimensions, not a generic template.

3. Pallets and container flooring

Two-way and four-way entry pallets in standard 1200 x 1000 mm and 1200 x 800 mm footprints, plus custom sizes for textile-bale conveyors. For the NTPC corridor and the heavier refinery flows we run a hardwood-bottom pallet that handles point loads above 1.5 tonnes without crushing. Container-flooring plywood (BWP-bonded, 18-21 mm) is supplied for export box-fabricators in the Sonipat–Panipat belt who refurbish 20' and 40' containers for road use.

Kochi-to-Panipat logistics

The straight-line lane from our Perumbavoor/Kochi dispatch point to Panipat is approximately 2,300 km by road. Realistic options:

  • Direct trailer (32-ft single-axle or 40-ft multi-axle) — 6 to 8 days transit door-to-door depending on driver changes and the Maharashtra–MP corridor. Best for full-load orders of 18-22 tonnes (roughly 9,000-11,000 sq.ft of 18 mm plywood, or one full container-equivalent of crates).
  • Part-load via Delhi NCR consolidators — 8 to 10 days, useful for trial orders or 3-5 tonne shipments. Final-mile delivery from a Delhi or Sonipat hub to Panipat is short.
  • Coastal + road combination — for very large volumes, container by sea to Mundra (~5 days) plus 1,150 km trucking to Panipat. Competitive when the order touches 40+ tonnes.

Prices are quoted ex-Kochi by default; GST and freight are itemised separately so the buyer can compare against local Yamunanagar-supplied plywood on a clean apples-to-apples basis. For most textile-bale and crate-grade orders above 8,000 sq.ft, the landed Panipat cost still works in our favour because the input timber and core stock economics in Kerala are different from the Yamunanagar belt.

Decision rules — which grade for which load

A short field guide for the Panipat buyer:

  • Cotton-textile and woollen bales for domestic dispatch — packing-grade ply in 6-8 mm caps is sufficient. No need to over-spec.
  • Export bales going through Mundra/Nhava Sheva — packing-grade ply, but step up to 8-12 mm and pair with ISPM-15 hardwood end-frames if the buyer's BOL calls for it.
  • Refinery spares, valves, pump assemblies — crate build with 12-16 mm packing-grade sides and a hardwood skid; for high-value or moisture-sensitive cargo, upgrade the faces to Okoume for a tighter surface and better screw-holding.
  • NTPC heavy spares, transformer accessories — pallets must be hardwood-bottom, never soft-pine. Crate lids in 18 mm packing-grade or BWR if the load sits in an outdoor yard for weeks before despatch.
  • Shuttering ply — relevant when Panipat construction buyers (commercial real estate along GT Road, refinery shutdown civil works) need film-faced ply for repeat-use formwork. Different product line; quoted separately.

FAQ — common Panipat buyer questions

Do you supply directly to Panipat or only through a Delhi dealer?

Direct from our Kochi despatch point. The invoice is from Cochin Wood Industries to the Panipat buyer with the buyer's GSTIN. No interim layer.

Can you do ISPM-15 fumigation and HT stamping for export crates?

Yes. Crates can be supplied with HT-stamped timber and the accompanying fumigation certificate where the destination country requires it. Specify destination at the quote stage so the right treatment route is chosen.

What is the minimum order that makes the Kochi lane economical?

Roughly 4-5 tonnes for part-load economics, 18-22 tonnes for a clean full-truck rate. Smaller pilot orders are fine — we will quote them honestly, including the part-load premium, so you can compare against local sources before committing to a larger lane.
