Plywood Supply to Warangal

Cochin Wood Industries

Warangal: a quiet, steady plywood-consuming belt

Warangal does not show up first on most plywood-buyer maps, but the city pulls a surprisingly consistent stream of packing-grade material. Two end-uses drive almost all of it: textile machinery and finished-fabric crates moving out of the Kakatiya Mega Textile Park (KMTP) and surrounding hand-loom and power-loom clusters, and granite slab packing moving south to Chennai port or west to Mundra. Both jobs need plywood that survives long road runs, weather, and rough fork-lift handling. That is the lane the Cochin Wood group has built around for forty years, and Warangal sits cleanly inside our normal pan-India dispatch grid.

The Warangal industrial picture

Warangal Tri-City (Warangal-Hanamkonda-Kazipet) anchors the manufacturing belt of north Telangana. The cluster has a specific shape that matters when sizing packing material.

Textile and apparel

  • Kakatiya Mega Textile Park (KMTP), Shayampet/Geesugonda — Telangana's flagship textile park spread across roughly 1,200 acres. End-to-end value chain from spinning to garmenting, which means heavy machinery inbound (looms, dyeing lines, finishing units) and finished-goods outbound by container.
  • Hand-loom and power-loom clusters in Warangal Rural, Bhupalpally and Chennur — Sircilla power-loom belt sits adjacent. Mostly outbound fabric in compact crates or pallet loads.
  • Warangal IDA-Madikonda and the older industrial estates around Kazipet — mixed engineering and ancillary units that crate spares and small machine sub-assemblies.

Granite and stone

  • The Karimnagar–Warangal granite belt (Tandur black, Telangana black, multi-colour) feeds processing yards around Warangal and Jangaon. Slabs and gangsaw blocks ship in heavy A-frame crates with plywood bottoms and packing-grade ply spacers.

Engineering, food and FMCG ancillaries

  • MSME estates at Madikonda and along NH-163 — light engineering, electrical panels, food-grade machinery — typical buyers of mid-size plywood boxes and four-way pallets.

For machinery exports leaving the textile park, we supply ISPM-15 heat-treated crates built around 12–18 mm packing ply skins on hardwood framing. We can prefab and dispatch nested or knocked-down, depending on container fit. Typical inside-dimensions we build are loom-frame size (roughly 2.4 m x 1.6 m x 1.8 m) and dyeing-machine size (3 m+ long); both ride out the Kochi–Warangal road run without issue when banded correctly.

Two-way and four-way pallets in 1200 x 1000 mm (Indian standard) and 1200 x 800 mm (Euro), built on hardwood blocks or stringers with packing-grade ply deck boards. For granite slab yards we also build heavy-deck pallets (18 mm deck, double bottom) rated for repeated fork-lift cycles.

4. Container flooring plywood

18 mm and 21 mm phenolic-bonded ply for retrofitting 20-ft and 40-ft container floors — useful for the granite exporters running their own captive container fleets to Chennai and Krishnapatnam. We supply in standard container-floor sheet sizes and can pre-drill on bulk orders.

Kochi to Warangal: the lane

The road math is the first thing a Warangal buyer should know, because freight is the swing variable on a plywood quote.

  • Distance: roughly 1,250–1,320 km depending on route (NH-544 Kochi–Salem–Bangalore–Hyderabad–Warangal vs Kochi–Coimbatore–Kurnool–Hyderabad–Warangal).
  • Transit time: 3–4 days door-to-door for a full truck (32-ft single-axle or 22-ft, depending on volume). Part-loads via Hyderabad transhipment add a day.
  • Truck size economics: a 22-ft truck holds roughly 350–400 sheets of 8 x 4 x 12 mm; a 32-ft single-axle takes 600–700 sheets. Granite crate orders are bulkier — we cube the load before quoting.
  • Port alternative: for very large lots (5+ containers), some buyers prefer Cochin Port → Krishnapatnam → road to Warangal. We can quote either way.

For most Warangal buyers, the right anchor question is: full truck or half? Below half a 22-ft load, part-load freight per sheet usually overtakes the savings on the plywood itself, and a local Hyderabad source can look cheaper on paper. Above half-truck, our Kochi pricing competes cleanly on a delivered basis.

Which grade fits which Warangal load

Packing grade (rubberwood/commercial hardwood core)

Default for almost everything moving out of Warangal: textile-machine crates, fabric pallets, granite spacers, engineering-spares boxes. Built for one or two one-way journeys with banding, not for re-use as furniture stock.

Okoume face / BWR construction

Step up to Okoume-faced or BWR-grade construction when (a) the crate is moving by sea container to GCC / South-East Asia from Krishnapatnam or Chennai, (b) the consignee has weight limits and Okoume's lighter density helps the airfreight or volumetric calculation, or (c) the buyer wants a cleaner face for branded engineering exports. Okoume is a face-veneer option on our packing and BWR grades, not a separate grade — we'll quote it on a per-job basis.

Shuttering ply

Not a packing product, but worth a mention. Warangal has continuous infra work (Kaleshwaram support structures, KMTP expansion phases, road widening on NH-163). When a construction buyer asks for "packing ply" for shuttering use, that is the wrong call — film-faced shuttering ply lasts 15–25 pours where packing ply will give up in 2–3. We carry both; we will steer the spec correctly before quoting.

FAQ

Do you supply ISPM-15 stamped crates from Warangal?

We ship ISPM-15 heat-treated, stamped crates from our facility — the wood is treated and stamped at origin, and the crate arrives in Warangal ready to load with the consignor's goods. Most textile-machine exporters take this route to avoid local HT availability gaps.

What is your minimum order for Warangal dispatch?

Economics start to work at around half a 22-ft truck — roughly 150–200 sheets of mixed thickness, or one 20-ft worth of built-up crates. Below that, part-load is possible but the freight per sheet rises sharply.

How long from PI to delivery?

For standard packing-grade sheet stock: 3–5 days production + 3–4 days transit, so plan for around 8–10 days from confirmed PI. Built-up crates add 5–7 days for fabrication depending on the spec sheet.
