Plywood Supply to Kolkata

Cochin Wood Industries

Kolkata sits at a junction that very few Indian cities can claim: a working deep-water river port, a century-old industrial belt across the Hooghly in Howrah, and an export base built on jute, tea, leather, and engineering goods that all have to leave the country inside a wooden box. That last detail is where Cochin Wood Industries comes in. We are a Kerala-based plywood supplier shipping packing-grade ply, crate kits, ISPM-15 pallets and container flooring into the Kolkata-Howrah belt every month, and this page lays out what we send, how we send it, and how to decide what fits a Kolkata buyer's load.

The Kolkata industrial picture in one read

Greater Kolkata is not a single market — it is at least four overlapping ones, and each has its own packing logic.

  • Kolkata Dock System and Haldia Dock Complex (Syama Prasad Mookerjee Port Authority): break-bulk and container traffic for engineering goods, tea chests, jute bales, and project cargo. Crate-heavy.
  • Howrah industrial belt — Liluah, Belur, Bally, Dasnagar, Salkia: foundries, light engineering, wire and bar units, fan and pump makers. Heavy, dense loads that need stiff plywood cases and pallets.
  • Kasba, Topsia and Tangra: leather goods, tannery output and small engineering — lighter export crates, often on jute-and-ply combination packing.
  • Falta SEZ, Bantala Leather Complex, Kalyani and Dankuni industrial zones: export-oriented units with steady ISPM-15 pallet and case demand.

Add the tea auction trade routed through Kolkata from North Bengal and Assam, the jute mills along the Hooghly from Titagarh down to Budge Budge, and the project cargo that moves through Khidderpore for power, metro and refinery work in the East and North-East, and the picture is consistent: Kolkata is a packing-and-shipping city, and packing-grade plywood is one of its working consumables.

What we ship to Kolkata

To Kolkata buyers we typically dispatch the following lines.

Packing-grade plywood (MR / commercial)

The workhorse for crate panels, case lids, internal partitions and engineered packing. Standard sheet is 8 ft × 4 ft, with thickness commonly stocked from 6 mm to 18 mm. The thicknesses Kolkata foundry packers ask for most often are 8 mm, 12 mm and 18 mm — 8 mm for case sides and tops on light engineering loads, 12 mm for medium-duty machinery crates, 18 mm for heavy base panels and pallet decks. Face veneer options on MR-Packing and BWR grades include Okoume and Gurjan; Okoume is the lighter, cleaner face preferred by exporters who pay container freight by weight.

Crate kits and case-ready cuts

For repeat exporters in Howrah and Falta we ship pre-cut panel sets keyed to a drawing: base, four sides, top, and corner runners. The kit lands at the customer's nail-gun line and assembles directly. This saves the buyer cutting wastage and avoids dust on the shop floor — important for tea and food-grade exporters in particular.

Pallets — ISPM-15 stamped

Standard footprints we ship east are 1200 × 1000 mm (the European pallet most engineering exporters use), 1200 × 800 mm (Euro pallet), and 1100 × 1100 mm (used in some South-East Asia and Far-East lanes that Kolkata exporters serve). Heat-treatment and the IPPC stamp are mandatory for all wood packaging leaving Indian ports under ISPM-15; every pallet we dispatch carries the stamp and the lot record.

Container flooring and sawn timber

For the heavier project loads that move out of Khidderpore — transformers, paper-mill rolls, heavy castings — we supply 28 mm container flooring grade plywood and dressed sawn timber runners for skids and dunnage. Jackwood runner stock and rubberwood runners are available in the sizes most lashing crews specify.

Picking the right grade for a Kolkata load

The decision rule is usually about the destination, the freight, and the customer of the customer. A few practical patterns:

  • Domestic / regional engineering crate moving from Howrah to North-East or to a project site: MR-grade packing plywood, 12–18 mm, Gurjan face. Cheapest cost per crate where freight isn't the binding constraint.
  • Export crate (Europe, USA, Australia): BWR / BWP plywood with Okoume face. Lower density means lighter crate, which matters when sea-freight is rated on chargeable weight or when an air-freight conversion is on the table.
  • Wet-zone packing (jute mills, tea cartons holding for monsoon): BWR grade — boiling-water resistant glue line. Higher upfront cost, lower failure rate during Bengal's long humid stretch.
  • Shuttering / formwork for construction: a separate film-faced product. Not the same as packing ply. Use it for civil work, not for crates.

Our pricing is structured ex-factory with GST and transport extra. Detail thicknesses, monthly quantity and the delivery zone (Howrah, Khidderpore, Falta, Dankuni, Kalyani etc.) and we will work a landed cost for your gate.

FAQ — what Kolkata buyers usually ask

Do you supply directly to Howrah and Liluah small-unit packers, or only to large exporters?

Both. Our minimum dispatch is typically a part-truck for sheet stock or a single pallet skid for samples. We work with small Howrah engineering packers as readily as with Falta SEZ exporters.

What is your lead time once a PI is confirmed?

Standard packing-grade ply: 4–6 working days for production-ready stock and dispatch from Kochi, then the road or coastal transit on top. Custom-cut crate kits add 2–3 days of cutting.

Can you handle ISPM-15 documentation for our shipping line?

Yes. Every pallet and crate carries the IPPC stamp and we supply the heat-treatment certificate along with the invoice and e-way bill. Most Kolkata CHA offices accept the format directly.
