Plywood Supply to Lucknow

Cochin Wood Industries

Lucknow sits at an interesting crossroads for anyone who supplies industrial packing material. The city is best known for chikankari handwork and a deep cultural economy, but the industrial belt around it has quietly turned into a meaningful buyer of export-grade plywood, crating timber and pallet stock. Handicraft exporters need shock-absorbing crates for fragile lacquerware, brass and chikankari consignments. The HAL Lucknow division and ancillary defense vendors need ISPM-15 compliant boxes that can stand up to sea freight. IT and electronics assemblers around Sushant Golf City and Chinhat need flat, dimensionally stable plywood for clean packing. That mix is what brings Cochin Wood Industries into the Lucknow conversation.

The Lucknow industrial picture

Lucknow's manufacturing and export activity is spread across a handful of well-defined zones, and knowing the geography helps when you are planning truck deliveries of plywood from Kerala. The main belts our trucks reach into are:

  • Chinhat Industrial Area — light engineering, food processing and packaging units along NH-28; close to the Faizabad Road corridor.
  • Sarojini Nagar Industrial Estate (UPSIDC) — long-established estate with metal fabrication, electrical goods and a steady appetite for shuttering ply during civil expansions.
  • Amausi and Nadarganj — adjacent to the airport; warehousing, third-party logistics and e-commerce fulfillment hubs that buy pallets in volume.
  • Sushant Golf City and Sultanpur Road belt — IT parks, ITeS campuses and the newer commercial construction that pulls shuttering plywood.
  • HAL Lucknow division (Faizabad Road) — accessory and avionics manufacturing for transport aircraft and helicopters; a precision sector that drives demand for export-quality wooden cases with documented heat treatment.
  • Chikankari and zardozi clusters around Chowk, Aminabad and Daliganj — small workshops feed consolidators and exporters in Gomti Nagar who do the actual crating and shipping.

Add to that the Defence Industrial Corridor nodes coming up around Lucknow and Kanpur, and the city's industrial footprint is genuinely growing. None of these sectors buy plywood the same way, which is the whole point.

What we ship to Lucknow

The Cochin Wood group has been operating in Perumbavoor since 1986, and the present-day Cochin Wood Industries supply mix into Uttar Pradesh reflects what we have seen actually move on these routes. Three categories cover almost everything Lucknow buyers ask for:

Packing-grade plywood (commercial / rubberwood)

This is the workhorse for handicraft exporters and engineering crate makers. Standard thicknesses we ship to Lucknow in container loads or part-truck consignments:

ThicknessTypical use in Lucknow
6mm and 8mmLining inside chikankari and lacquerware crates; lightweight handicraft cartons
10mm and 12mmGeneral export crates, machinery sub-assemblies, electronics packing
14mm to 16mmHeavy crates for fabricated parts and HAL-ancillary loads
18mmBase panels of heavy boxes, container side panels, shuttering reuse

All sheets are MR or BWR bonded depending on the destination port and humidity exposure. For sea-bound consignments through Mundra or JNPT we usually push buyers toward BWR.

Export crates and packing cases

We supply both knock-down crate kits (cut-to-size panels, cleats and skids) and fully assembled cases. The HAL ancillary segment and the heavier engineering exporters typically want ISPM-15 heat-treated softwood frames married to plywood skins. Common case sizes for the Lucknow handicraft trade are in the 600 x 400 x 400 mm to 1200 x 800 x 800 mm range; engineering crates run much larger and are often built around the load's center of gravity rather than to a standard size.

Pallets

Lucknow's warehousing and 3PL belt around Amausi runs mostly on two pallet footprints — the standard 1200 x 800 mm Euro and the 1200 x 1000 mm "industrial" pallet. We supply both two-way and four-way entry pallets, in commercial hardwood or rubberwood, with ISPM-15 stamping where the pallet is going onward into export.

Container flooring

For project cargo movers and the occasional containerized HAL shipment that needs reinforced flooring, we supply 18mm or 21mm BWR plywood cut to ISO container floor dimensions. This is a lower-frequency line for Lucknow but worth flagging — it travels in the same truck as the crating ply.

Kochi-to-Lucknow logistics

Lucknow is roughly 2,400 km from Kochi by road. The realistic operating window for a single-truck consignment is 5 to 7 days door-to-door, depending on whether the truck routes via Bangalore-Hyderabad-Nagpur-Jhansi or via Mumbai-Indore-Gwalior. We dispatch ex-factory Perumbavoor; long-haul trucking and unloading are quoted separately so the buyer can compare against their own freight rates if they prefer.

For larger orders — anything above roughly 25 to 30 tonnes — buyers in Lucknow sometimes find it cheaper to take FOB Cochin (port: Cochin / Vallarpadam ICTT) and rail-and-truck the container inland to ICD Kanpur or ICD Loni, then short-haul to their yard. This works well for handicraft consolidators who are already running container traffic. For smaller orders the road lane wins on simplicity. We are happy to quote either way and let the numbers decide.

Decision rules: which grade for which Lucknow load

A practical operator view of when each grade earns its place:

  1. Use commercial / rubberwood packing plywood when the consignment is domestic, going by road to another Indian city, or being air-freighted internationally in well-controlled conditions. This is the most cost-effective option and covers the bulk of chikankari and light handicraft crates.
  2. Step up to BWR-bonded plywood with okoume face veneer when the destination is humid, the sea voyage is long (Middle East, West Africa, ASEAN), or the customer values a cleaner-looking export case. Okoume is a veneer choice on our packing and BWR grades — it is not a separate plywood grade.
  3. Choose shuttering plywood only when the buyer is a civil contractor on the Sushant Golf City or Gomti Nagar Extension projects. It is built for concrete forming, not for crating; using it for packing wastes money on the wrong feature set.

FAQ

Do you supply ISPM-15 stamped boxes for HAL ancillary exports?

Yes. We supply heat-treated softwood frames with our plywood skins, stamped to ISPM-15 by an accredited treatment facility. We share the treatment certificate with the consignment so it clears phytosanitary inspection at the port.

Minimum order quantity for a Lucknow consignment?

Most of our Lucknow buyers run full-truck loads (roughly 20 to 25 tonnes of plywood, depending on thickness mix) because the per-sheet freight on a part-load over 2,400 km is uncomfortable. We will quote part-loads too — just expect a higher landed cost per square foot.

Can you cut-to-size for our standard crate?

Yes. Send the cutting list and tolerance — we cut at the factory in Perumbavoor and ship the panels bundled by crate, which speeds up assembly at your yard and reduces sawdust on your shop floor.
