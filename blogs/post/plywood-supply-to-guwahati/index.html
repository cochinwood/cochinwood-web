Plywood Supply to Guwahati

Cochin Wood Industries

Why Guwahati is a serious packing-plywood market

Guwahati is the freight gateway for the entire North East. Cargo coming out of Assam, Meghalaya, Nagaland, Arunachal, Manipur, Mizoram and Tripura tends to consolidate at LGBI Airport, the Amingaon ICD, or the trucking hubs around the Brahmaputra crossings before moving south. That consolidation pattern is exactly what drives the city's plywood and crate demand. Tea chests for export auctions, oil-field equipment routed out of Numaligarh and Bongaigaon, jute machinery moving between mills, and engineering goods being repacked for line-haul to Kolkata and Mumbai all need predictable, IS-2202 compliant packing material. The Cochin Wood group has been supplying that kind of bulk industrial plywood out of Perumbavoor since 1986, and Guwahati has steadily become one of the more interesting eastern-corridor markets for our packing-grade boards, crates and container flooring.

The Guwahati industrial picture

The buyer base in and around Guwahati is more diverse than people outside the region usually assume. Public-sector heavyweights, mid-size exporters and a long tail of consolidators all pull plywood through the city.

Industrial belts and estates that drive packing demand

  • Amingaon and North Guwahati — the Amingaon ICD/CONCOR terminal is the rail-linked container yard for NE cargo. Most outbound containers are stuffed here, which means crate-makers and packing contractors cluster around it.
  • Bamunimaidam Industrial Estate — older estate with engineering workshops, fabrication units and ancillary packing converters.
  • Brahmaputra Industrial Park (Amingaon) and EPIP Amingaon — export-oriented units that ship finished and semi-finished goods needing ISPM-15 compliant wooden crates.
  • Changsari and Sila — newer warehousing and 3PL clusters along NH-27, taking pallet volumes for FMCG and white-goods distribution.
  • Byrnihat (just across the Meghalaya border) — cement, steel re-rolling, ferro-alloy and chemical units that need heavy-duty crates for machinery dispatch.

Sectors served by Guwahati packing converters

The plywood and crate demand in the city tracks four sectors closely:

  • Tea — Assam supplies roughly half of India's tea output. Auction lots from Guwahati Tea Auction Centre and direct export shipments still rely heavily on plywood-lined chests and corner-reinforced crates.
  • Petroleum and petrochemicals — Numaligarh Refinery, Bongaigaon Refinery and the OIL/ONGC field operations across upper Assam pull drum-packing plywood, gasket crating, and skid-mounted equipment cases.
  • Jute, plywood machinery and engineering — Assam's own plywood and jute mills generate machinery dispatches that need export-grade crating.
  • NE consolidation cargo — handicrafts, agarwood, cane and bamboo products, food processing exports and project cargo all flow through Guwahati packers before line-haul.

What we ship to Guwahati

Most Guwahati orders fall into one of three buckets. The right specification depends on whether the buyer is making the box themselves, buying finished crates, or fitting out containers.

Packing-grade plywood (MR-Packing)

This is the workhorse: BWR-glue rubberwood / hardwood plywood meant for one-trip packing cases, ISPM-15 crates and skids. Common thicknesses we move to Guwahati are 6 mm, 8 mm, 12 mm, 16 mm and 18 mm in 8x4 ft sheets. Buyers ordering for tea-chest reinforcement and small-equipment crating tend to standardize on 6-8 mm; petrochem and machinery exporters typically pick 12-18 mm for sidewalls, skids and base.

Finished crates and ISPM-15 cases

For converters who would rather buy ready-cut and cleated, we ship knock-down plywood crates, cleated boxes and reinforced cases. Heat-treatment and ISPM-15 marking is done at the converter end so the crate clears customs at any port in India or the GCC.

Wooden pallets

Standard sizes that move well into the Guwahati 3PL belt are 1200x1000 mm (Indian standard), 1200x800 mm (Euro) and 1100x1100 mm (Asia). Plywood-top pallets are preferred for FMCG and pharma; plain sawn-timber pallets work for cement, ferro-alloy and heavy industrial use.

Container flooring and lining

For 20-ft and 40-ft container stuffing at Amingaon ICD, 18-21 mm BWR plywood is used as floor protection and as side-lining for tea chests, drum-loads and heavy machinery. We also supply Okoume-faced plywood for buyers who need a cleaner finish on export-visible surfaces.

Kochi to Guwahati: route, distance and lead time

The road distance from Kochi/Perumbavoor to Guwahati is approximately 3,200 km via NH-44 (the Salem-Nagpur-Raipur-Kharagpur-Kolkata-Siliguri-Guwahati corridor). Most of our Guwahati despatches move by full-truckload on 32-ft single-axle and multi-axle trailers, with a normal door-to-door transit of 8-12 days depending on weather and the Siliguri corridor situation. For larger or time-critical loads, rail-on-road through CONCOR's Amingaon terminal is the alternative — typical end-to-end time is 10-14 days but with better predictability during the monsoon.

Which grade fits which Guwahati load

A quick decision rule we share with new buyers in the region:

  • MR-Packing rubberwood plywood — one-trip crates, tea chest reinforcement, light machinery, drum packing. Most cost-efficient for the volume Guwahati moves.
  • BWR hardwood plywood — multi-trip pallets, container flooring, monsoon-exposed crates, anything that may sit on a wharf or wait for rail allotment.
  • Okoume-faced plywood — premium export cases for tea, handicrafts, agarwood and any cargo where the box itself is visible to a buyer overseas.
  • Shuttering ply — for the Byrnihat and Changsari construction belt; film-faced 12 mm and 18 mm for column, slab and retaining-wall shutters.
  • Sawn timber (rubberwood, jackwood) — for pallet runners, crate cleats and battens where a converter prefers in-house cutting.

FAQ for Guwahati buyers

Do you supply ISPM-15 heat-treated material?

The plywood we ship is engineered board, which is exempt from ISPM-15 by construction. For solid-wood components (runners, cleats, battens) and finished crates, heat treatment and stamping is done at the converter or at our partner heat-treatment yard before despatch. We can route material through an HT yard on request.

What is the minimum order size for Guwahati?

Given the lane economics, a single 32-ft full-truck load (roughly 600-750 sheets of 18 mm or equivalent mix) is the most cost-efficient drop. Smaller orders can ride as part-loads but the per-sheet freight goes up materially.

Can you quote ex-works versus delivered?

Yes. Most converters in Guwahati prefer a delivered Guwahati city or delivered Amingaon ICD quote so the freight risk sits with us. Ex-Perumbavoor pricing is available if a buyer has their own NE logistics tie-up.
