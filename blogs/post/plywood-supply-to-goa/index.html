Plywood Supply to Goa

Cochin Wood Industries

Goa is one of those markets that looks small on a map and then turns out to be punching well above its weight on the packing-materials side. Between the pharma majors in Verna, the iron-ore movement around Mormugao, and the steady flow of containerised cargo through MPT, the state burns through a meaningful volume of packing-grade plywood, wooden crates, pallets and dunnage every month. We supply that demand from Kochi, and this post is a working note on what we ship to Goa, why those specs make sense for the local load profile, and how the Kochi to Goa lane actually runs.

The Goa industrial picture

Goa's manufacturing and export base is concentrated in a handful of well-known belts. If you're sourcing packing materials for a unit in any of these, the typical board, crate and pallet conversations look fairly similar.

Verna Industrial Estate (South Goa)

Verna is the centre of gravity for Goa's pharma sector — Cipla, Glenmark, Lupin and a long tail of API and formulation units operate out of the estate. Pharma export packing has very specific requirements: ISPM-15 heat-treated crates for ocean freight, dimensional stability so cartons stack cleanly inside the crate, and clean inner faces because pharma QA inspectors look at everything. Verna also has a steady electronics and engineering component, which adds shuttering-ply demand for civil expansions and plywood crates for heavier machinery exports.

Pilerne, Tuem and Kundaim estates (North Goa)

The northern estates carry a mix of food processing, FMCG, light engineering and beverage units. Pallet demand here tends to be high frequency, lower value — standard four-way ISO pallets, EUR-size pallets for European buyers, and CP-spec pallets for chemical and pharma loads. Plywood-deck pallets become attractive when the buyer wants reusability, hygiene compliance, or pest-free shipment without a separate fumigation step.

Mormugao Port (MPT) and the iron-ore cluster

Mormugao is one of India's older major ports and historically the spine of Goa's iron-ore export. Even with mining cycles fluctuating, the port handles general cargo, container traffic and project consignments. Two demand streams flow from this: heavy-duty wooden dunnage and crating for project cargo and bulk-handled equipment, and container flooring plywood for the steady ICD and CFS traffic feeding the port.

Goa-Karnataka border belt

The Sankhali, Honda and border-adjacent industrial pockets pull in some demand from manufacturers who serve both Goa and the Belgaum-Hubli engineering belt. For these buyers, sourcing packing materials from Kochi via NH-66 is often cheaper than pulling boards from far-northern mills, simply because of the trucking economics.

What we ship to Goa

The range is built around three product families, all manufactured under Cochin Wood specifications at our contract factories near Perumbavoor. The Cochin Wood group has been operating in Perumbavoor since 1986, and the packing range is the segment where forty years of group manufacturing experience translates most directly into board behaviour you can rely on.

Packing-grade plywood

Our packing range is the workhorse for crate, case and pallet-deck manufacturing across pharma, FMCG and engineering exporters.

  • Thicknesses: 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 15, 16 and 18 mm
  • Sheet size: standard 8x4 ft, with custom cut sizes for case manufacturers
  • Core: hardwood / rubberwood combinations, MR-Packing grade
  • Face options: commercial face for standard packing; Okoume face where buyers want a cleaner, lighter export-grade look

For pharma export crates out of Verna, 12 mm and 14 mm boards are the most common conversations — strong enough for stacked cartons of formulations or API drums, light enough to keep crate weight reasonable for sea-freight chargeable weight calculations.

Wooden crates and packing cases

For buyers who don't want to run their own carpentry shop, we manufacture knocked-down or assembled crates to drawing. Typical specs for the Goa pharma lane:

  • ISPM-15 heat-treated runners and battens, stencilled with the IPPC mark
  • 12 mm or 15 mm packing-ply panels, depending on gross weight
  • Internal bracing and corner blocks for cartons that must not shift in a 40-ft container
  • Optional moisture barriers, silica gel pockets, and stencilled shipping marks on request

Pallets

Goa runs the full pallet spectrum — standard 1200x1000 mm, EUR 1200x800 mm, custom footprints for project cargo, and plywood-deck variants for hygiene-sensitive loads. We supply both sawn-timber pallets and plywood-deck pallets in the same factory cluster, which lets a buyer mix and match without juggling two vendors.

Container flooring plywood

For repair yards and container manufacturers around the MPT belt, 28 mm container flooring plywood is the standard reference. We supply matching grades and can cut to standard 20-ft and 40-ft floor sheet sizes.

Decision rules: which board for which Goa job

A simple way to think about board selection for the typical Goa demand:

  • Pharma export crates (Verna): Packing-grade plywood, 12-15 mm. Add an Okoume face if the buyer ships into EU or Japanese clients who value cleaner aesthetics.
  • FMCG and beverage pallets (Pilerne, Tuem): Sawn-timber pallets for cost-driven internal movement, plywood-deck pallets where hygiene or reuse cycles matter.
  • Project cargo and machinery exports (port-route): Heavier 15-18 mm packing ply with reinforced runners; sawn-timber framing for the load-bearing skeleton.
  • Civil construction and infra (estate expansions, port works): Shuttering plywood, 12 mm standard, with film-faced options on request.
  • Container floor repair (MPT belt): 28 mm container flooring plywood in standard ISO sheet sizes.

FAQ

Do you supply ISPM-15 compliant crates direct to Goa?

Yes. Our contract factories operate ISPM-15 heat-treatment chambers, and crates ship with the IPPC stencil applied. This matters for pharma and engineering exports moving through Mormugao or trans-shipped via Nhava Sheva.

What is the minimum order quantity for a Kochi to Goa shipment?

Economics work best at full-truck quantities (typically a 32-ft load of plywood, or an equivalent volume of crates and pallets). Smaller orders are possible on part-load basis, but per-unit freight rises sharply below half-truck volumes.

Can you supply Okoume-faced packing ply for export crates?

Yes. Okoume is offered as a face-veneer option on our MR-Packing and BWR boards. Quote on request, as Okoume sits in a different price band from the standard commercial-face packing range.
