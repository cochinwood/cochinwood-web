Plywood Supply to Rajkot

Cochin Wood Industries

Why Rajkot's industrial belt pulls steady plywood demand

Rajkot is the casting, forging and machine-tool capital of Saurashtra, and that one sentence explains nearly every plywood enquiry that reaches us from the city. When a CNC lathe bed, a ductile-iron casting, a forged crankshaft or a pump housing leaves a Rajkot shopfloor for a buyer in Germany, the United States, Vietnam or the GCC, it does not travel naked. It travels inside a wooden crate or strapped to a pallet, and the panel material that holds that crate together is packing-grade plywood. With more than 10,000 engineering MSMEs concentrated in and around the city, Rajkot generates one of the densest concentrations of export-crate demand anywhere in western India. That is the niche the Cochin Wood group has been building plywood for since 1986.

The Rajkot industrial picture

Rajkot's manufacturing footprint is spread across several well-defined industrial estates, each with its own packaging rhythm. Knowing the geography matters because it shapes truck routing, unloading windows and the kind of crate spec a buyer typically asks for.

Aji GIDC

The oldest and densest of the Rajkot estates, Aji GIDC houses a heavy mix of foundries, machine shops and auto-component units. Castings shipped out of Aji tend to be irregular and dense, which pushes buyers toward heavier panel thickness, typically 12 mm and 18 mm, with reinforced corner blocks.

Lodhika GIDC (Metoda)

Lodhika, on the Rajkot-Jamnagar highway, is the largest industrial estate by area and houses larger forging units, CNC builders and pump manufacturers. Crate orders from Lodhika frequently call for ISPM-15 stencilled material because the goods are export-bound to Europe, the United States and ASEAN.

Shapar-Veraval belt

Shapar-Veraval is the machine-tool and submersible-pump cluster on the Rajkot-Ahmedabad corridor. The work that leaves Shapar is often containerised, which is where 12 mm and 18 mm container flooring panels and full crate kits come into the picture.

Bhavnagar Road and the smaller clusters

The Bhavnagar Road belt and adjacent smaller estates handle a long tail of bearing, fastener, valve and brassware units. Volumes per unit are smaller but the cumulative pallet and small-crate demand is significant and recurring.

Kochi-to-Rajkot logistics

The road distance from our Kochi/Ernakulam dispatch hub to Rajkot is approximately 1,850 km. Transit by full truck load typically runs five to seven days, depending on monsoon conditions and the specific drop point inside the GIDC network. Most of our Rajkot lane volume moves by direct FTL on the Kochi - Bengaluru - Hubballi - Pune - Ahmedabad - Rajkot corridor, with the final leg routing into Aji, Lodhika, Shapar or the Bhavnagar Road belt as needed.

For coastal economics on larger orders, an alternative is rail-container or coastal-shipping movement from Kochi/Tuticorin to Mundra or Pipavav, with a short road leg of 250-350 km into Rajkot. This route gets economically interesting for orders above a couple of truck loads and where the buyer can absorb a slightly longer transit in exchange for lower per-sheet freight. We work out the trade-off at quote stage based on the consignment size and the customer's preferred port.

Decision rules: which CWI grade fits which Rajkot load

Rajkot's load mix is varied, so the right material is not always the same. A simple rule of thumb:

  • Packing grade BWR ply is the right answer for most casting, forging and machine-component crates leaving Rajkot. The bond is moisture-resistant enough to handle a monsoon sea passage, and the cost-per-crate is right for export packaging that is consumed once.
  • Okoume-faced plywood makes sense when the end-buyer is in Europe or the United States and the crate is part of the brand presentation, or when the consignment is high-value machine tools where surface finish and grain matter. Okoume is also lighter, which helps on chargeable-weight-sensitive export lanes.
  • Shuttering ply is a separate conversation. Rajkot's construction activity around Kalavad Road, the 150 Ft Ring Road and the upcoming AIIMS-Rajkot corridor drives steady film-faced shuttering demand. This is not export packaging material and should not be substituted for crate-grade.

Our practical advice to first-time Rajkot buyers: start with a small mixed trial - one or two pallets of 12 mm and 18 mm packing-grade in standard 8x4 ft - confirm the panel performs across your typical crate spec, then move to a recurring monthly indent.

FAQ for Rajkot buyers

Do you supply ISPM-15 compliant crate material?

Yes. Heat-treated, ISPM-15 stencilled timber components are available for any export crate destined for an overseas buyer. Specify on the enquiry so we route the order through the certified line.

What is the minimum order quantity for an FTL into Rajkot?

A full 20-tonne truck typically carries 12-15 m3 of plywood, depending on thickness mix. For smaller indents below FTL, we consolidate with other Gujarat-bound consignments, which adds a few days of dispatch wait but keeps freight workable.

Can you cut to size for a repeating crate spec?

Yes. Once you share the cutting list and confirm the spec is recurring, we cut at the dispatch end and ship as a bundled kit. This saves cross-cut waste and shopfloor time at the Rajkot end.
