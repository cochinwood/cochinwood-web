Plywood Supply to Asansol

Cochin Wood Industries

Asansol sits at the heart of one of the densest heavy-industry corridors in eastern India — the coal pits of Raniganj, the integrated steel works at Burnpur, the Eastern Coalfields Limited (ECL) headquarters at Sanctoria, and the long backbone of NH-19 and NH-2 carrying out everything from coking coal and HR coils to mining electricals and pump skids. Every one of those movements needs wood: packing-grade plywood for export crates, hardwood pallets for forklift handling, runners and gluts for heavy castings, and container-floor-grade ply for stuffed export consignments leaving via Kolkata, Haldia and Paradip. This is the kind of industrial footprint that drives steady, repeating demand for the products the Cochin Wood group has been manufacturing in Perumbavoor since 1986.

The Asansol industrial picture

Asansol-Durgapur is officially treated as a single metropolitan industrial region under the Asansol Durgapur Development Authority (ADDA), and the wood-packaging demand is best understood belt by belt rather than as one block.

Coal and mining: Raniganj coalfield and ECL

Eastern Coalfields Limited, headquartered at Sanctoria in Dishergarh just outside Asansol, runs the Raniganj coalfield — one of the oldest mined coal basins in the country. The downstream packaging demand here is rarely about coal itself; it is about capital equipment going into the mines: longwall shearers, conveyor belt drums, switchgear, transformer panels, hydraulic roof supports, pumps, motors and spare-part shipments inbound from OEMs. All of these move in heavy plywood crates with ISPM-15 certified pallets underneath.

Steel: IISCO Burnpur and the auxiliary belt

The IISCO Steel Plant at Burnpur — now under SAIL — is the anchor steel facility, modernised to roughly 2.5 MTPA of crude steel. Around it sits a dense ring of rolling mills, foundries, refractory plants and steel-fabrication units across the Burnpur-Kulti-Hirapur axis. Demand here is for heavy-load crates around mill rolls, gear boxes, refractory blocks, and finished steel parcels being skidded for road movement. Pallets and runners tend to be 19 mm and thicker, with sawn hardwood gluts to take the point loads off the ply.

Engineering, cement and chemicals

The Durgapur side of the corridor adds Alloy Steels Plant, Mining and Allied Machinery, Bharat Heavy Electricals' Durgapur unit and several private engineering shops; the cement clusters around Chittaranjan and Salanpur add bagged-cargo and machinery packing demand. ADDA's Mangalpur and Bamunara industrial estates round out the SME base — typical buyers here are engineering job shops crating sub-assemblies for OEMs further down the line.

Railways and exports

Chittaranjan Locomotive Works (CLW) — one of the largest locomotive builders in the world — is a short drive from Asansol and ships spares and sub-assemblies in heavy ply crates. Export-oriented consignments out of the region typically route through Kolkata (Haldia or Kolkata Dock System) for break-bulk, or stuff into containers for sea export, which is where container-floor-grade and BWR ply come in.

Okoume-faced packing plywood

Where the consignment is high-value or the receiver is overseas — typically OEM machinery, export-grade castings or anything that has to look the part inside an air-freight crate — we offer Okoume face veneer on the same MR-Packing or BWR core. Standard sizes we build to: 1200 x 1000 mm and 1200 x 800 mm two-way and four-way entry. Heavier 1219 x 1016 mm (48 in x 40 in) GMA-style pallets for North American export consignments. Jackwood and rubberwood runners are supplied at ₹400/cft (jackwood) and as-per-size (rubberwood); the standard tolerance reads as ¼ inch minus on jackwood.

Container flooring and shuttering ply

For stuffed export containers we supply 18 mm BWR-grade ply suited for floor lining. For the construction side — slab work at the cement, power and infra projects in the corridor — film-faced shuttering plywood is available in standard sheet sizes.

Kochi to Asansol: the logistics economics

Asansol is roughly 2,000 km by road from Kochi. We do not pretend that is a short trip — but for repeating bulk orders the trucking lane is well-established and predictable.

  • Road, full-truck: 32-ft single-axle or multi-axle from Perumbavoor / Kochi, routed via Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Nagpur, Raipur and into West Bengal — transit typically 7 to 9 days door-to-door, depending on the trailer type and the season.
  • Coastal + road: for very large volumes we can break the trip — sea leg from Cochin Port (Vallarpadam/ICTT) up to Haldia or Kolkata, then short-haul road into Asansol (around 230 km from Kolkata). This usually only pencils out above 2-3 container-equivalents per consignment.
  • Rail container: CONCOR / private container trains from south India terminals to ICDs in the east are available; lane economics shift quarterly so we price each load individually.

Volume-wise, a single 32-ft trailer carries roughly 1,000-1,200 sheets of 12 mm packing ply depending on the stacking plan. Truck-load economics are the single biggest variable in the landed price into Asansol, which is why we quote ex-factory and transport separately rather than bury it in the sheet rate.

Decision rules: which grade for which load

  1. Inland packing, single-trip crates inside India: MR-bonded packing-grade ply, 12 mm sides, 18 mm base. Lowest landed cost per crate.
  2. Export crates, OEM machinery, sea voyage: step up to BWR core, Okoume face on the visible surfaces, 18 mm base with hardwood gluts under heavy point loads.
  3. Container floor lining and dunnage: 18 mm BWR. Single sheet across the container width where possible to avoid joints under wheel loads.
  4. Mining / heavy electrical equipment crates: 15 to 18 mm sides, hardwood corner posts, base sized for the lifting-tackle profile rather than the cargo footprint.
  5. Construction slab work in the Asansol-Durgapur corridor: film-faced shuttering ply, not packing-grade. The two are not interchangeable.

FAQ for Asansol buyers

Is it worth buying plywood from Kerala when sources in Yamunanagar or Kolkata are closer?

For one-off small orders, no. For repeating bulk packing-grade demand — say a full truck a month or more — Kerala mill rates plus our trucking lane are competitive on landed cost, and the consistency of the core construction tends to be tighter because of the contract-factory specification model we operate.

Do you supply ISPM-15 heat-treated pallets for export?

Yes. ISPM-15 compliant heat-treated and stamped pallets and crates are available; we quote the stamp and the certificate together with the order.

What is the minimum order size you ship into West Bengal?

The economics work cleanly from a full 32-ft trailer upward. We can consolidate smaller part-loads where another consignment is already routed in the same direction, but the per-sheet rate moves up accordingly.
