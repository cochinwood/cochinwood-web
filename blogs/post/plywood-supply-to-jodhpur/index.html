Plywood Supply to Jodhpur

Cochin Wood Industries

Jodhpur is one of the few inland cities in India where a plywood enquiry almost always points to an export shipment. Whether the carton headed for Boranada is full of Sheesham dining sets, antique-replica almirahs, guar gum powder for the food industry, or precision-engineered industrial parts, somewhere in that supply chain a packing-case maker is cutting plywood to size. The Cochin Wood group has been supplying plywood to packing and export-oriented industries since 1986, and Jodhpur sits squarely inside the kind of buyer pattern we know well: bulk, repeat, export-grade, no-surprises.

Why Jodhpur's industrial belt matters for plywood

Jodhpur's manufacturing footprint sits in a few well-defined pockets — Boranada (the largest, around 18 km south-west of the city), Sangariya, Mandore, the Heavy Industrial Area, and the newer extensions along the Pali road. The furniture cluster — solid Sheesham, mango wood, reclaimed wood, antique replica — is what put Jodhpur on the global trade map, and it is still the city's loudest export. Add the guar gum processors (Jodhpur and the surrounding belt account for a significant share of India's guar derivatives exports), the handicrafts ecosystem, and a growing engineering sub-cluster, and you have a city that ships physical goods abroad almost every working day.

Every one of those shipments needs a wooden box, a pallet, or container dunnage. That is where our packing-grade plywood, sawn timber and crate components come in.

Sectors we serve in and around Jodhpur

  • Furniture exporters — heavy solid-wood items going to the US, EU, UK and Australia. Crate panels typically 9–12 mm packing-grade, with 18 mm bases for skid-mounted heavy units.
  • Antique-replica and handicraft exporters — mixed-load LCL shipments where panel consistency and nail-holding matter more than face finish.
  • Guar gum and food-grade powder exporters — bagged product stacked on hardwood pallets, usually moved in 20-ft containers, where pallet structural ply and ISPM-15 heat-treated runners are the recurring spend.
  • Engineering and auto-component exporters — heavier crates, often with shock-absorbing internal framing on a plywood-faced base.
  • Marble and stone processors in the wider Rajasthan belt — A-frame crates, plywood-faced bracing, and dunnage planks.

What we ship to Jodhpur

The Jodhpur enquiry list is fairly predictable once you have shipped a few rounds. Below is the typical material flow.

Packing-grade commercial plywood

Our standard packing-grade ply (rubberwood / commercial hardwood core, MR-bonded) is what most Jodhpur crate makers actually want. It is built for box and case work, not for furniture finishing, which keeps the cost honest. Common Jodhpur orders:

ThicknessTypical use in Jodhpur

Sheet size is 8×4 ft standard, but we cut to custom widths for crate makers running standing saws — useful for the antique-replica yards that work to non-standard footprints.

Okoume plywood

For exporters whose buyers specify a cleaner-looking exterior crate — display-piece furniture, premium handicraft, certain EU retailers — Okoume face veneer on the same packing core gives a uniform reddish-brown look without paying for marine-grade material. It is a finish call, not a strength call.

Crates, pallets and container flooring

  • Knock-down crate kits — pre-cut panels, ISPM-15 heat-treated framing, fasteners, shipped flat. Useful when the Jodhpur exporter does not want to run their own ply-cutting line.
  • Two-way and four-way pallets — standard 1200×1000 mm and 1200×800 mm decks for guar gum and bagged-export loads, with 18 mm plywood top decks where required.
  • Container flooring and dunnage — 18 mm boards for floor reinforcement on heavy furniture loads, and rough sawn dunnage for blocking.
  • Sawn hardwood — jackwood and rubberwood runners for crate framing, billed by cft.

Kochi-to-Jodhpur logistics

Jodhpur is about 2,100 km from Kochi by road, and almost everything we send up there moves by full-truck-load. The standard lane runs Kochi → Bengaluru → Hyderabad → Nagpur → Udaipur → Jodhpur, with transit times of roughly 6–8 days for a 32-ft single-axle truck, depending on the season and the driver's planned halts. We also use Kochi → Mumbai (Vashi/Bhiwandi) → Jaipur → Jodhpur when the load needs to consolidate with a Mumbai pickup.

For larger volumes — multi-truck monthly runs that some of the guar gum and furniture exporters consume — we plan loads at the contract factory so a 32-ft truck leaves fully cubed (around 600–700 sheets of 18 mm equivalent, give or take). That keeps the per-sheet landed cost in Jodhpur predictable. EX-factory pricing plus GST plus transport is the rule; we share the freight quote alongside the proforma so there is no last-minute surprise.

For coastal exporters working through Mundra or Pipavav, we can also bridge containerised dispatches from Cochin port — but for inland Jodhpur, road from Kochi is faster and cheaper than any sea+rail combination.

Decision rules: which grade for which Jodhpur load

Default — commercial packing-grade ply

Use this for the vast majority of Jodhpur orders: standard export furniture crates, handicraft boxes, engineering crates, pallet decks for bagged powder exports. It is what the cost-sheet wants.

Step up to Okoume face

When the buyer at the other end has specified an external finish or when the crate doubles as display packaging. Higher cost, better look. Same structural performance as the underlying packing core.

Shuttering ply

Not a packing material — but Jodhpur's construction boom (residential and commercial along the Pali road and the ring road) does generate a parallel demand. We supply film-faced shuttering ply to infrastructure and concrete-frame contractors, separately quoted.

Sawn timber

For runners, bracing, blocking and any structural element of a crate that needs to be solid wood rather than ply. Quoted by cft, separately from the ply roll-up.

FAQ — Jodhpur buyers

Do you supply ISPM-15 compliant packing?

Yes — for exporters who need heat-treated wood, we supply ISPM-15 stamped pallets and crate framing. The plywood panels themselves are exempt from ISPM-15 because they are engineered wood, but the solid-wood components (runners, skids, bracing) must be treated, and we handle that on the dispatch side.

What is the minimum order you accept for a Jodhpur delivery?

We are set up for truck-load economics. A part-load works only if we can consolidate with another Rajasthan-bound shipment in the same week. For one-off small orders we usually recommend buying through a Jaipur stockist rather than paying full freight from Kochi.

Do you handle the freight or do we arrange our own truck?

Either works. We have lane-tested transporters running Kochi-Jodhpur and can include door-delivered freight in the quote, or we can keep it ex-factory and let your Jodhpur logistics team take over at Perumbavoor. Most exporters prefer door-delivered so they can hold a single supplier accountable.
