Plywood Supply to Tuticorin

Cochin Wood Industries

Why Tuticorin is one of South India's most plywood-hungry port cities

Tuticorin (Thoothukudi) is unusual on the Indian map because almost everything that moves out of the city moves in a wooden box. The V.O. Chidambaranar Port handles bulk salt, marine products, processed copper from Sterlite's legacy belt, edible oil, chemical containers, cement clinker and a growing line of project cargo for southern Tamil Nadu's renewable build-out. Every one of those flows needs packing-grade plywood somewhere in the chain — either as the export crate the customs officer stamps in Sector 10A, or as the pallet that takes the load from the SIPCOT shed to the container freight station at Harbour Estate. We at Cochin Wood Industries route a steady stream of packing-grade plywood, ISPM-15 export crates, four-way pallets and container flooring sheets down the NH-744/NH-38 corridor into Tuticorin's industrial belts, and this page is a practical operator's note on how that supply works.

The Tuticorin industrial picture, sector by sector

The plywood demand in Tuticorin doesn't sit in one place. It is spread across five or six distinct industrial pockets, each with a different load profile. Understanding the pockets matters because crate design, ply thickness and pallet footprint all change with the sector.

SIPCOT Industrial Complex, Madathur

The Madathur SIPCOT phase is the heavy-engineering and chemical anchor — units making industrial salts, sulphuric acid derivatives, soda ash, processed kaolin and PVC compounds. Crates here typically carry drum-mounted goods or palletised bags. Floor planks and side walls run on 12 mm and 18 mm BWP packing ply because the loads are corrosive-adjacent and the customs route is direct-port-export.

V.O. Chidambaranar Port and the CFS belt

The container freight stations clustered around Harbour Estate and Mullakkadu need pallet stock more than crate stock. The pallet footprint is mostly 1100 x 1100 mm and 1200 x 1000 mm because outbound flows hit GCC ports (Jebel Ali, Sohar) and South-East Asia. Top and bottom decks are 12 mm packing ply; stringers are pine or rubberwood scantling.

Tuticorin Marine Products cluster (Therespuram, Threspuram, Vembar belt)

Frozen seafood exporters need light, hygiene-compliant export crates and slip sheets. The marine products belt is where our 8 mm and 10 mm rubberwood-core packing ply moves in volume — it goes into the reefer container as box bottoms and partition panels.

Sterlite-legacy copper and metals downstream

Copper rod, cathode and downstream fabricators in the Madathur and Pandarampatti belts buy thicker BWP for heavy crating — typically 18 mm with battens for cathode bundles, and container flooring sheets when project cargo needs an internal load-distribution platform.

Renewable and wind-component vendors

The wind belt south of Tirunelveli, which routes through Tuticorin port, occasionally needs oversized project crates for blade fittings, nacelle sub-assemblies and inverter cabinets. These are one-off jobs but heavy on 18 mm BWP and IS 710-grade boards.

The Kochi-to-Tuticorin logistics lane

The road run from Perumbavoor to Tuticorin is one of the cleaner south-Indian trucking lanes. The standard route is Perumbavoor — Muvattupuzha — Thodupuzha — Kumily — Theni — Madurai — Tirunelveli — Tuticorin, roughly 470 km on NH-744 (the Kochi-Dhanushkodi corridor) and NH-38 (the Madurai-Tuticorin stretch).

A 20-ft full-truck load lifts roughly 600 sheets of 12 mm packing ply. Bigger 32-ft single-axle bodies lift around 1,000 sheets. We dispatch from Perumbavoor by evening, the truck crosses the Walayar check-post overnight, and material is on the SIPCOT customer's floor by the second morning. For port-direct deliveries to CFS yards near Harbour Estate, we can route via the Karaikudi-Aruppukottai bypass and shave roughly an hour off peak-monsoon transit.

Decision rules: which grade for which Tuticorin load?

  • Salt, chemical and corrosive-adjacent goods — go BWP (boiling-water-proof) at 12 mm or 18 mm. The marine-grade glue line is non-negotiable when the crate sits dockside before lift.
  • Marine-products export (frozen seafood) — 8 mm or 10 mm packing ply for box bottoms; partition panels at 6 mm. Light, food-contact-safe coating optional.
  • Copper cathode, metal fabrications, project cargo — 18 mm BWP with battened crate construction and 4-way pallet base.
  • Outbound general cargo to Jebel Ali / Sohar via VOC Port — Okoume-faced packing ply if the consignee specifies, otherwise straight rubberwood-core packing grade.
  • Port-expansion and warehouse contractors — IS 710 shuttering ply; film-faced if the contractor wants pour-repetition.

Frequently asked questions from Tuticorin buyers

Do you supply ISPM-15-stamped export crates ready to load?

Yes. Heat treatment and the ISPM-15 stamp are done at our Perumbavoor facility before dispatch. Buyer gets a phytosanitary-compliant crate landed at SIPCOT or the CFS yard.

What is the minimum order quantity for a Tuticorin run?

For pure plywood sheet supply, one truck (~600-1,000 sheets depending on body size). For built crates and pallets, the MOQ is lower because we consolidate with other Tamil Nadu-bound consignments.

Are rates inclusive of GST and freight to Tuticorin?

No. Card rates are EX-factory Perumbavoor. GST 18 percent and freight to Tuticorin are extra. We share a delivered-to-SIPCOT or delivered-to-Harbour-Estate quote on the proforma invoice once thickness, sheet count and delivery dock are confirmed.
