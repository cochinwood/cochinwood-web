Plywood Supply to Dubai

Cochin Wood Industries

Why Dubai's industrial belt drives steady plywood demand

Dubai is not really a manufacturing city in the traditional sense — it is a re-export consolidation hub. Containers land at Jebel Ali, get broken down, sorted, repacked and shipped out again to Africa, the wider GCC, the Levant and the CIS. That repackaging step is where plywood enters the picture. Electronics from East Asia, textiles from the subcontinent, automotive spares from Europe and project cargo from across the Gulf all need fresh crates, pallets and dunnage before they leave the free zone. Add to that the steady tempo of construction across new districts and the result is a city that absorbs a wide spread of packing-grade plywood, ISPM-15 stamped pallets and shuttering ply month after month. For exporters in Kerala, Dubai is one of the most operationally clean export lanes in the GCC — a short sea leg, predictable port handling and a customer base that thinks in containers rather than truckloads.

The Dubai industrial picture

Most of the wood-packaging demand sits inside a handful of well-defined belts on the western and southern edge of the emirate. Understanding which belt a buyer sits in usually tells you what kind of crate they need.

Jebel Ali Port and JAFZA

Jebel Ali is the largest man-made harbour in the world and the anchor of the Jebel Ali Free Zone (JAFZA). Re-export consolidators, regional distribution centres for electronics brands and bonded warehouses that re-bundle textile shipments are concentrated here. The packing requirement is typically light to medium-duty crates and four-way pallets, used inside containers — so 12mm and 15mm packing-grade plywood with planed softwood framing tends to dominate.

Dubai Industrial City (DIC)

South of Jebel Ali, Dubai Industrial City houses heavier-engineering tenants — machinery assembly, building-materials processing, food and beverage. Here the typical job is a heavy-duty export crate for a single machine or assembled unit, often 18mm packing-grade plywood with hardwood skids, bolt-down corner cleats and silica-gel dunnage.

Dubai Aviation City and DWC

Around Al Maktoum International Airport, the demand profile shifts to aerospace MRO crates and high-value airfreight packing. These are smaller boxes but with tighter tolerance requirements — flat, void-free panels matter more than raw load rating.

Dubai Investments Park and Al Quoz

The interior industrial estates — Dubai Investments Park (DIP) and the older Al Quoz cluster — handle a mix of joinery, furniture and fit-out work for the construction sector. This is where shuttering ply and sawn timber lots tend to land alongside the packing-grade range.

What we ship into Dubai

The Cochin Wood group has been in Perumbavoor since 1986, and over forty years of group manufacturing has shaped a fairly tight, repeatable product range for GCC re-export work. We do not try to be everything for everyone — we ship what the Dubai sector actually consumes, in container quantities.

Packing-grade plywood

Our staple for Dubai is commercial / rubberwood packing-grade plywood, supplied ex-factory in 8x4 ft sheets across the 6mm-to-18mm range. For JAFZA consolidators the workhorse thicknesses are 12mm (light crate walls), 15mm (medium crates, pallet decks) and 18mm (heavy-duty bases and skids). Sheets are supplied with a flat lay, square edges, and a tolerance of "minus" on stated thickness — the same convention international packers expect.

Crate construction

Where we are quoted on finished crates rather than sheets, we build to ISPM-15 standards using heat-treated softwood framing and hardwood runners, with packing-grade plywood panels. Standard configurations include collapsible export crates, closed boarded cases for sensitive equipment, and open-top skidded bases for heavy machinery. Crate fabrication on this lane is usually buyer-spec — sizes, IPPC marking, lashing points and bolted versus nailed corners all get confirmed in the PI stage.

Pallets

Four-way ISPM-15 stamped pallets in standard 1200x1000 and 1200x800 footprints, plus custom oversize bases for project cargo. Block pallets when the buyer is feeding automated handling lines; stringer pallets for general repack work.

Container flooring

For consolidators rebuilding worn FCL boxes, we supply container-flooring grade plywood in 28mm thickness, hardwood-faced, with the standard 19-ply construction the lane expects.

Okoume and shuttering ply

Okoume veneer is offered as a face option on the MR-Packing and BWR grades when the buyer needs a brighter, smoother finish — typical for re-export crates carrying branded electronics where the outer case is visible at retail. Shuttering ply is supplied to construction and fit-out buyers in DIP and Al Quoz on standalone orders.

Decision rules — which grade fits which Dubai job

A few practical rules we apply when scoping a Dubai order.

  • JAFZA consolidation re-pack — default to 12mm or 15mm commercial packing-grade. Volume is high, individual crates are light, the panel does not need to be exhibition-quality.
  • Heavy machinery export from DIC — 18mm packing-grade panels on hardwood-skidded bases. If the consignment is high value or going onward to a humid African destination, specify BWR packing.
  • Branded electronics or premium textile re-export — Okoume face on MR-Packing core. The brighter surface and clean grain matter to the receiving warehouse.
  • Construction fit-out in DIP or Al Quoz — shuttering ply or BWR hardwood plywood per the architect's spec; quoted as a separate line from the packing range.
  • Container floor refurbishment — 28mm hardwood-faced flooring ply only; nothing thinner survives a forklift cycle.

FAQ — common questions from Dubai buyers

Do you supply ISPM-15 stamped pallets and crates?

Yes. All wood-packaging material we ship out of Cochin Port for the GCC carries IPPC marking from a registered heat-treatment facility, with treatment certificates attached to the shipping documents.

What is the minimum order quantity for FCL to Jebel Ali?

We quote in container loads. A 40' HC is the standard unit; mixed loads (packing-grade sheets plus pre-built pallets, for example) are routine. Sub-container quantities ship LCL through Cochin consolidators on agreed lead times.

How do you handle thickness tolerance and moisture content for Gulf-climate transit?

Packing-grade is supplied minus on stated thickness per the standard packing convention. Moisture content is held to the range needed for stable transit through Gulf humidity, and we kiln-dry sawn timber lots before crate fabrication when buyer specs call for it.
