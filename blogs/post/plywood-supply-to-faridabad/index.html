Plywood Supply to Faridabad

Cochin Wood Industries

Faridabad is one of the densest concentrations of metal-working capacity in north India. Between the NIT industrial belt, the Ballabgarh foundries, and the auto-component clusters feeding Eicher, Escorts and JCB lines, the city ships out crates of castings, forgings, gearboxes and farm-equipment subassemblies almost every working hour. Each of those crates needs plywood that will not flex, fail or split when a 600 kg cylinder head is dropped onto a truck bed in 44 degree summer heat. That is the business problem the Cochin Wood group has been solving for industrial buyers for four decades, and Faridabad sits squarely in our delivery lane.

The Faridabad industrial picture

Faridabad is not one industrial estate but a stack of overlapping belts, each with its own packing rhythm. Before you order plywood here, it helps to know which belt the load is moving through, because the crate spec changes with it.

NIT and the old industrial spine

The NIT (New Industrial Township) sectors — broadly Sectors 6, 24, 25, 27, 31 and the older Sector 58/59 cluster — house the original Faridabad engineering economy: sheet-metal pressings, tool-room work, automotive electricals, switchgear, fasteners and a long tail of tier-2 and tier-3 component suppliers. Output here tends to ship in mid-sized crates of 80 kg to 400 kg, often consolidated into LCL or part-load trucking toward Mundra, Pipavav or domestic OEM plants.

Ballabgarh and the foundry belt

South of the city, Ballabgarh and the surrounding sectors are foundry-heavy: grey iron, ductile iron and aluminium castings going to commercial vehicle, tractor and construction-equipment OEMs. Castings are unforgiving cargo — sharp burrs, point loads, and weights that frequently cross 250 kg per piece. Plywood crates here have to act as both a structural frame and an abrasion barrier.

Sectors 58 to 68 and the newer estates

The newer auto-component and assembly units along the Mathura Road belt, plus the Faridabad-Noida-Ghaziabad corridor, lean toward larger consolidated export shipments — gearboxes, hydraulic assemblies, axle parts and farm-equipment subassemblies bound for the GCC, Africa and Southeast Asia. These typically need export-spec, ISPM-15 compliant wooden cases, container loading plans, and stronger pallet stacks.

For exporters loading 20 ft and 40 ft containers out of ICDs and onward to Mundra or Pipavav, we supply thicker packing-grade sheets used as flooring overlay and bracing dunnage — typically 12 mm or 18 mm rubberwood ply, cut to container width with a small clearance for easy fit.

Kochi-to-Faridabad logistics

Faridabad sits roughly 2,350 km north of Kochi. We move material on two main modes, and the right choice depends on lot size and urgency.

Direct trucking lane

For full-truck and part-load consignments, we dispatch 32 ft single-axle and multi-axle trucks via the NH-44 corridor through Bangalore, Hyderabad, Nagpur and Agra, terminating in Faridabad sectors directly. Transit time runs 6 to 8 days door-to-door depending on season and toll-plaza congestion at Delhi-NCR entry points. This is the right mode for finished crates, pallet stacks and time-sensitive sheet orders.

Port-routed economics

For large sheet-only consignments where transit time is less critical, we can load containers FOB Cochin port and route through coastal shipping or rail to inland container depots near Delhi-NCR, with final road leg into Faridabad. This often works out cheaper per sheet on full-container volumes, particularly for buyers with their own ICD account.

We provide a clear breakup of EX-FACTORY rate, transport and GST on every quote so the buyer can compare the two routes and pick.

Decision rules — which grade for which load

Buyers in Faridabad frequently ask which grade is the right call. A short rulebook:

  • Routine domestic crates and pallets (under 500 kg): commercial or rubberwood-faced packing-grade plywood, 10-16 mm. Best cost-per-crate, designed for one-way packing duty.
  • Export crates, premium finish or higher cosmetic spec: Okoume face veneer on the packing or BWR substrate. Lighter, cleaner surface, better stencilling — useful for export buyers shipping to GCC and Vietnam who want a presentable crate at the receiving end.
  • Heavy castings, shock loads, repeated handling: 16-18 mm packing-grade base with hardwood corner reinforcement, or move up to BWR substrate where moisture exposure is likely.
  • Construction, formwork, infra projects on Faridabad sites: shuttering ply (film-faced) — a different product family from packing ply, designed for concrete contact and multiple pours.

We do not push buyers up the price ladder unnecessarily. If a 12 mm packing sheet does the job, that is what we quote.

FAQ

Can you supply ISPM-15 compliant heat-treated crates for export out of Faridabad?

Yes. Heat-treatment and IPPC stamping is standard for any export-bound case we build. The certificate is supplied with the dispatch documents so it clears phytosanitary checks at the loading port.

What is the minimum order for sheets to Faridabad?

We quote both part-load and full-truck consignments. The per-sheet rate is keenest on full-truck loads (roughly 350-400 sheets of 18 mm depending on packing), but smaller indents are workable when consolidated with other Delhi-NCR-bound material.

Do you carry stock or build to order?

Standard packing-grade sheets in popular thicknesses are run through our contract factories on continuous schedules, so lead time from order confirmation to dispatch from Perumbavoor is typically 3-7 working days. Custom crate builds, sized cases and non-standard veneers take longer — we share an exact dispatch date on PI.
