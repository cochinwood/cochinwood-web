Plywood Supply to Patna

Cochin Wood Industries

Patna is not the first city most South Indian plywood mills think about, and that is precisely why the city is interesting. Bihar's capital sits at the intersection of an agri-export economy that is genuinely growing — litchi out of Muzaffarpur, makhana from Darbhanga and Madhubani, mango and vegetables from the Ganga belt — and a slowly expanding engineering and food-processing base around Patliputra, Fatuha and the Bihta-Patna corridor. Every one of those flows eats wooden packaging. The Cochin Wood group has been shipping packing-grade plywood, crates, pallets and container flooring out of Kerala since 1986, and Patna's specific mix of perishables, machinery and FMCG distribution is exactly the kind of demand profile our material is built for.

The Patna industrial picture

Most buyers we speak to in Bihar are not located inside Patna municipal limits — they are sitting on the industrial belts that ring the city and feed its consumption. Knowing those belts matters, because it changes how a truck is routed and how a packing programme is planned.

  • Patliputra Industrial Area — the older estate inside Patna, dense with food-processing units, FMCG repackers and light engineering. Short hauls from here usually mean smaller crates and reusable pallets.
  • Fatuha Industrial Area — about 25 km east of Patna along NH-31, mid-sized food and chemical units. A common origin point for outbound truckloads going to North-East India.
  • Bihta Industrial Area — the newer, larger belt about 35 km west on NH-922, with a mix of food processing, building materials and increasing pharma activity.
  • Muzaffarpur and Hajipur belts — technically outside Patna district but logistically run through Patna. Litchi processing in Muzaffarpur and the Hajipur food-park cluster are heavy seasonal consumers of export-grade crates and slip-sheet pallets.
  • Darbhanga / Mithilanchal makhana corridor — India's makhana capital is moving from loose-bag distribution to graded, branded cartons inside wooden over-pack for long-distance freight. That over-pack is plywood.

The common thread across these belts is that finished goods rarely stay in Bihar. They move by road to Kolkata for sea export, by rail to Delhi NCR for re-distribution, or by air out of Patna and Kolkata for premium perishables. That long onward journey is what makes the packing decision matter.

What we ship into Patna

Packing-grade plywood (the workhorse)

The bulk of what flows from our Perumbavoor base to Bihar is commercial / rubberwood-core packing-grade plywood. For Patna's load profile we see four thicknesses do the heavy lifting:

  • 8 mm and 10 mm — lids and side panels for litchi and mango export crates, where weight savings during air-freight matter.
  • 12 mm — the default for food-processing equipment crates, FMCG over-pack and most general-purpose boxes leaving Fatuha and Bihta.
  • 18 mm — bases, skids and heavy-duty engineering crates, including transformer parts and large machinery moving out of Patliputra.

Sheets ship in standard 8x4 ft, cut-to-size on request. All rates are quoted EX-FACTORY Perumbavoor; GST and transport are charged separately and quoted live based on the prevailing Kochi-to-Patna lane rate.

Wooden crates and ISPM-15 boxes

For makhana, litchi pulp, mango concentrate and processed-food shipments going onward to the GCC, EU or South-East Asia, we fabricate ISPM-15 compliant crates with heat-treated softwood framing and packing-ply panels. Stencil marks (HT, IPPC code, country code) are applied at the factory so the crate is export-ready when it lands at the consignor's dock.

Pallets

Two pallet patterns cover most Patna requirements:

  • 1200 x 1000 mm — the GMA-style footprint suited to FMCG and food-grade warehouse racking in eastern India.
  • 1200 x 800 mm — the Euro footprint used by exporters shipping into European retail chains, common for processed-food and ready-to-eat lines.

Both are built with hardwood blocks and packing-ply deck boards. Customer logos and one-way vs. returnable markings are stencilled before dispatch.

Container flooring

Patna does not have its own container yard at the scale of a coastal port, but reefer and dry containers are dropped here for stuffing of perishable and engineering cargo before they road-haul to Kolkata or Haldia. We supply 28 mm container-flooring boards in the standard 2440 x 1220 mm format for the few specialist body-builders and reefer-retrofitters operating in the Bihta belt.

Kochi-to-Patna logistics

The straight-line distance from our Perumbavoor base to Patna is around 2,100 km by road. The lane we use most often runs Kochi → Bengaluru → Hyderabad → Nagpur → Varanasi → Patna, a 2,250 km trucking distance that a 32-ft single-axle or multi-axle truck typically covers in 5 to 7 days depending on monsoon conditions and Ghat traffic.

Decision rules: which grade for which load

The wrong grade is more expensive than the right grade, every time. The simple rules we walk Patna buyers through:

  1. Use packing-grade plywood when the cargo is one-way, the destination is domestic or short-haul export, and the buyer is sensitive to packaging cost per box. This is the right pick for most FMCG over-pack, litchi and mango export crates, and FMCG over-cartons.
  2. Use Okoume plywood when the end customer is in the EU, Japan or the GCC and the consignee will reuse or inspect the crate. Okoume's clean face veneer also helps when the crate carries premium branding — common for high-value processed-food and pharma shipments.
  3. Use shuttering ply only for the construction case — formwork on infrastructure jobs around the new Patna Metro corridor, Ganga bridge approaches, or the Bihta industrial expansion. It is the wrong choice for packing because the film face is heavy and not cost-justified for one-way use.

FAQ

Do you have a stocking point in Bihar?

No. We ship directly from Perumbavoor on confirmed orders. For repeat buyers running monthly programmes we hold dedicated stock at the factory and dispatch on a fixed weekly truck so the buyer's Patna or Bihta godown sees a predictable inbound rhythm.

What is the minimum order quantity for a Patna delivery?

For straight sheet stock, one full 32-ft truck (roughly 18-20 tonnes) is the economic minimum. For crate and pallet kits we can ship part-loads consolidated with sheet stock. Smaller trial orders are possible but the per-sheet landed cost rises sharply.

How are payment and GST handled for a Bihar delivery?

IGST applies on the inter-state invoice. Standard terms are advance against PI for first-time orders; established buyers move to part-advance, balance-against-LR-copy after the first three clean transactions. The invoice is raised from our Kerala GSTIN against the Bihar consignee's GSTIN.
