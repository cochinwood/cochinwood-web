Plywood Supply to Jeddah

Cochin Wood Industries

Why the Jeddah industrial belt matters for a Kochi plywood shipper

Jeddah is the trade lung of the Red Sea. Jeddah Islamic Port handles the majority of Saudi Arabia's non-oil container traffic, King Abdullah Economic City (KAEC) is pulling new light-manufacturing and logistics tenants up the coast at Rabigh, and the older industrial zones around Jeddah second, third and Phase 4 are still where most of the city's machinery, electrical and consumer-goods packing actually happens. From a plywood supplier's point of view that mix means a steady, year-round pull for export-grade packing material: crates for re-export machinery, pallets for cross-dock warehousing, and dunnage and container flooring for the long Red Sea voyages onward to East Africa, the Levant and Europe. Kochi sits in a useful position to feed that demand — roughly 2,200 nautical miles to Jeddah, on a direct east–west route, with no Suez transit and no Cape-of-Good-Hope detour. That is the practical case for shipping plywood west out of Cochin Port, and it is the lane the Cochin Wood group has been working into for several years now.

The Jeddah industrial picture

Buyers in Jeddah do not all sit in one zone. When we plan a quote we usually segment the city into four belts, because each one prefers a slightly different specification of plywood.

Jeddah Industrial City (Phases 1 through 4)

The old industrial city south-east of the airport — Phase 1, 2, 3 and the newer Phase 4 — is the dense cluster. Plastics, food processing, light electrical, building materials and a lot of contract packing units sit here. Demand is mostly 12 mm and 18 mm packing-grade plywood for crate walls, plus EUR-footprint pallets for warehouse turn.

King Abdullah Economic City (KAEC) and Rabigh

KAEC, about 100 km north of Jeddah, is the newer story. Industrial Valley tenants — automotive parts, food, pharma packaging, building products — typically want heat-treated ISPM-15 crates because their finished goods re-export across the GCC and to Europe. Spec creep here is real: buyers ask for IPPC-stamped lumber, sealed crate seams, and sometimes okoume-faced panels for higher-end consumer goods.

Jeddah Islamic Port re-export shed

Inside and immediately around the port, freight forwarders and consolidators run shed-level packing operations: stuffing, re-crating, and container-load-out of transit cargo. The pull here is fast — they want pallets and crate blanks available off-the-shelf, not built to order.

Makkah construction and project sites

Up the Haramain corridor toward Makkah, the demand is different again. Project sites pull shuttering plywood for concrete work — film-faced 18 mm sheets, multiple re-use cycles, sometimes specified by main contractors with European brand habits. We typically quote film-faced shuttering ply with a hardwood core for this segment, not packing grade.

What we ship to Jeddah

The Cochin Wood group has been operating out of Perumbavoor since 1986 — about forty years of group manufacturing in Kerala's plywood cluster — and the bulk of our Jeddah-bound volume falls into four product families. Specifications below are typical; final quote depends on container plan, load weight and ISPM-15 status.

Packing-grade plywood (the workhorse)

BWR-bonded commercial and rubberwood plywood, 6 mm to 18 mm, in 8x4 sheets. The most common Jeddah ask is 12 mm for crate walls and 18 mm for top/bottom panels and skid boards. We supply in mill-pack bundles for container stuffing and can pre-cut to crate-blank sizes if the buyer's line is set up for that. Material is moisture-tolerant for the Red Sea voyage and the Jeddah summer.

Okoume plywood

Okoume-faced panels go out to KAEC and to a handful of premium consumer-goods packers in Jeddah Industrial. The pitch is the smoother face, the lighter weight (roughly 480–520 kg/m³ versus 600+ for hardwood) and the cleaner stencil/branding surface. We quote okoume only on request — it sits at a different price point and is not interchangeable with standard packing grade.

Wooden crates, cases and pallets

Built-to-spec ISPM-15 (HT-stamped) wooden crates and pallets, sized to either standard EUR (1200x800) or ISO (1200x1000) footprints. We can ship them as flat-pack crate kits to save freight if the Jeddah buyer has a re-assembly bench. Container loading capacity for flat-pack kits is roughly 2.5x to 3x the assembled-crate equivalent.

Container flooring and dunnage

Heavy-duty 18 mm and 21 mm plywood, hardwood-core, for re-flooring reefer and dry containers, and as load-spreading dunnage under heavy machinery. Same lane, same lead time — usually bundled into a mixed container with packing-grade sheets.

The Kochi-to-Jeddah lane

The logistics for this lane are clean. From Cochin Port (ICTT Vallarpadam) to Jeddah Islamic Port is approximately 2,200 nautical miles. Direct west-bound mainline services and a handful of feeder routings via Salalah or Jebel Ali run weekly. Sailing time is typically 9 to 14 days door-to-port depending on the service and any transhipment leg. No Suez transit is involved, which keeps the cost and the risk profile lower than the Med routes.

From our side, inland trucking from Perumbavoor to Vallarpadam runs about 30 km — one of the shorter factory-to-gate hauls in India for an export lane this size. That short pre-carriage matters: it shrinks demurrage exposure and makes it practical to load on the same day the BL date is confirmed. For full-container loads we coordinate stuffing at the factory or at a Cochin Port CFS, depending on whether the cargo needs to be palletised or stuffed loose.

FAQ for Jeddah buyers

Do you ship ISPM-15 / HT-stamped material?

Yes. ISPM-15 heat-treated and IPPC-stamped crates and pallets are the standard for the KAEC and re-export segments. We supply the certification copy with the shipping documents so customs and the Saudi PIF / phytosanitary check at Jeddah Islamic Port move without flags.

What is the minimum order for a Jeddah-bound container?

The economics start to make sense at about 12 to 15 tonnes of plywood — roughly a 20-foot container of 12 mm sheets or about 700 to 800 sheets, depending on the mix. Below that, LCL via the Cochin–Jeddah groupage services is workable but loses the freight advantage.

Can you palletise and shrink-wrap before stuffing?

Yes. Standard EUR or ISO pallets, strapped and shrink-wrapped at the factory, with a cargo manifest matched to the BL. Useful for buyers who run an automated unloading line at the Jeddah end.
