Plywood Supply to Ajmer

Cochin Wood Industries

Ajmer sits in a peculiar industrial sweet spot. Half an hour south-west, Kishangarh runs the world's largest marble market, slabs and blocks moving by the thousands of tonnes every week to ports in Mundra, Pipavav and Nhava Sheva. Half an hour north-east, Beawar and Bhilwara feed one of north India's dense textile and processed-mineral belts. Both ends of that lane need the same thing in volume: dependable packing-grade plywood for export crates, pallets and container floors that don't fail in transit. This is a practical look at how the Cochin Wood group ships into Ajmer, what we make, and how the Kochi-to-Rajasthan logistics actually work.

The Ajmer industrial picture

Ajmer district's manufacturing footprint is split across three concentrations, each with a different appetite for wood-based packaging:

  • Kishangarh marble cluster — over a thousand processing units along the NH-448 corridor and the Kishangarh Marble Association belt. Slab and block exports run to the GCC, Europe and the United States. Each container load typically needs heavy-duty wooden crates or A-frame stillages plus container-floor protection.
  • RIICO industrial areas at Palra, Parbatpura and Kishangarh-Madanganj — mixed engineering, mineral processing, packaging units and ancillary fabrication. Mid-weight crates and ISPM-15 export pallets are the steady draw.
  • Beawar–Bhilwara textile & cement axis (just beyond the district) — synthetic yarn, denim, processed fabric and cement clinker movement that pulls pallets and shrink-stacked bundles. Light to medium-grade ply.

Add the granite and sandstone trade flowing through Makrana and the wider Rajputana belt, and you have a region whose unit economics reward plywood that is dimensionally honest and consistently glued — because rework at a Mundra warehouse is more expensive than the crate itself.

What we ship to Ajmer

Packing-grade plywood

Our MR packing-grade plywood is the workhorse for Kishangarh marble crates and Palra-area engineering exports. Sizes are 8×4 feet standard; thicknesses we ship most often into Rajasthan are 12 mm, 16 mm and 18 mm for crate walls, with 8 mm and 10 mm used for lids, partition dividers and inner blocking. The boards are hot-pressed under the Cochin Wood group's specification set — uniform density, low core gaps, glue line that holds through the dry heat of a Rajasthan summer and the cold of a winter sea leg.

Marble export crates

For Kishangarh's slab and block exporters we supply ply cut to crate-kit dimensions so local fabricators or the buyer's own carpentry shop can assemble fast. The typical brief is a heavy-duty open crate or skid base with cross-bracing, capable of carrying 1.5–4 tonnes of polished slab per unit without flex. 18 mm board is the usual call for base and bracing, 12 mm for side panels.

Wooden pallets

Standard sizes we make to order:

  • 1200 × 1000 mm (Euro-adjacent, common for marble tile cartons)
  • 1200 × 800 mm (Euro standard, for textile and processed-goods exports)
  • 1100 × 1100 mm (Asian standard, for GCC-bound containers)
  • Custom footprints for stone slab A-frames and heavy machinery skids

Pallets can be supplied ISPM-15 heat-treated and stamped for export use — non-negotiable for any container heading out of Mundra or Pipavav to Europe, the US or Australia.

Container flooring ply

For exporters loading dense, point-load cargo (marble blocks, granite tiles stacked on dunnage, heavy castings), 18 mm container-floor ply spreads the load and protects the container floor from puncture damage and rejection fines. We cut to standard 20-foot and 40-foot container interior dimensions.

Okoume plywood

Where the end buyer's spec calls for a lighter, cleaner-finish export crate — typical for high-value textile machinery, electronics or anything where customs inspection happens repeatedly and the crate has to look the part — Okoume face ply is the right call. Light weight, smooth surface, takes stencilling and shipping marks cleanly.

Kochi-to-Ajmer logistics

The route from our Perumbavoor dispatch yards to Ajmer is roughly 2,100 km by road, and we run it as a direct trucking lane rather than rail or coastal-port hop. Two practical reasons:

  1. Rajasthan's inbound rail rakes from Kerala are slow and require terminal handling at Jaipur or Ajmer goods yard — that adds 5–8 days versus a direct truck.
  2. Plywood is volume-sensitive, not weight-sensitive. A 32-foot multi-axle full-load truck moves 800–1,000 sheets economically straight from Perumbavoor to a Kishangarh or Ajmer godown.

Typical transit time is 6–8 days door-to-door depending on Maharashtra and Gujarat checkpoints and the monsoon window. The lane runs Perumbavoor → Salem → Bengaluru → Hubli → Pune bypass → Ahmedabad → Udaipur → Ajmer. For smaller orders we consolidate with other Rajasthan deliveries; for full-truck orders we dispatch direct and the customer receives one freight invoice.

The two failure modes we see most often when buyers under-spec are: 12 mm used where 18 mm is needed (crate flexes, slab cracks in transit), and non-ISPM pallets sent to an export packer who then has to scramble for replacements at the port.

FAQ

What is the minimum order you ship to Ajmer?

Full-truck loads (32-ft multi-axle, around 800–1,000 sheets depending on thickness) make the freight economics work cleanly. Smaller orders are possible via part-load consolidation, but per-sheet freight rises. For repeat buyers in Kishangarh and Palra we hold a stocking pattern that supports faster top-ups.

Do you supply ISPM-15 stamped pallets?

Yes. Heat-treated, IPPC-stamped pallets are standard for export buyers. Specify destination country at order time so we cut to the right footprint.

Can you cut sheets to crate-kit sizes before dispatch?

Yes — we can pre-cut to a buyer-supplied cutting list. This is common for Kishangarh customers who want their carpentry crew to assemble crates rather than cut from full sheets.
