Plywood Supply to Mesaieed

Cochin Wood Industries

Mesaieed Industrial City sits about 40 km south of Doha and is the heaviest piece of industrial real estate in Qatar — a concentrated belt of petrochem, fertilizer, LNG, steel and bulk liquid handling clustered around the deepwater port. Almost every line on that estate eventually exports something heavy in a wooden crate, palletises stainless-steel spares, or imports a packaged spool/skid that arrived on softwood dunnage. That is where Cochin Wood Industries comes in: we ship packing-grade plywood, ISPM-15 crates, hardwood pallets and container flooring out of Kochi into the Qatari industrial supply chain, and Mesaieed is one of the most consistent demand pockets in the GCC.

The Mesaieed industrial picture

Mesaieed is not a generic free zone — it is purpose-built around three heavy verticals that all consume engineered wood packaging in volume. Knowing which belt your loads come from changes the spec we recommend, so it is worth being specific.

Petrochemicals and downstream chemicals

QAPCO, QAFAC, Qatar Vinyl, Qatar Fertiliser Company (QAFCO) and the wider Mesaieed Petrochemical Holding cluster sit inside the estate. The recurring packaging brief is heavy: catalyst drums, reactor internals, valve skids, compressor spares, fertilizer-line refurbishment kits. Crate weights of 800 kg to 3 tonnes per box are routine, and the inner stiffening — gussets, runners and braces — is often more demanding than the skin panel itself.

LNG, gas processing and QatarEnergy operations

Mesaieed is one of the operational hubs for QatarEnergy's liquids and downstream gas business, sitting alongside the Ras Laffan complex further north. LNG project work flows through Mesaieed in the form of turbine spares, cryogenic valves, instrument racks, and long-lead E&I packages. These need crates that survive multi-modal handling, monsoon-grade humidity in the originating port, and 35–45°C heat on arrival.

Steel, port logistics and marine fabrication

Qatar Steel (in its current and successor forms), Qatar Aluminium Extrusion and the dry-bulk operations of Mesaieed Port mean a constant flow of steel coils, billets, extrusions and offshore-fabrication packages. Container flooring replacement, dunnage planking and heavy-duty pallets sit in this belt rather than crating.

What we ship to Mesaieed

The Cochin Wood group has been operating out of Perumbavoor, Kerala since 1986 — roughly forty years of group manufacturing in plywood and sawn timber — and Cochin Wood Industries Pvt Ltd is the trading and export face that consolidates this output for buyers in Mesaieed and the wider GCC. The product range that lands in Qatar is narrower than the catalogue: most Mesaieed orders fall into four buckets.

Packing-grade plywood (commercial and rubberwood)

This is the workhorse. We ship 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 15, 16 and 18 mm in 8×4 ft sheets, MR-bonded, designed to be sawn and nailed in a crate shop rather than polished. 12 mm and 18 mm move in the largest volumes — 12 mm for crate skins and lid panels, 18 mm for base sleds and load-bearing decks under petrochem and LNG spares.

Okoume-faced and BWR packing plywood

For petrochem and LNG buyers who need a longer shelf life on the crate (multiple handlings, outdoor staging in the Mesaieed sun, possible re-export onward), Okoume-faced and BWR-grade boards make sense. The Okoume face takes stencilling cleanly for project marking, and BWR resin tolerates the humidity swing between the Kochi monsoon load-out and the Qatari summer.

ISPM-15 hardwood crates and pallets

Anything wooden that enters Qatar must comply with ISPM-15 — heat-treated, stamped, and traceable. We build closed crates, open crates, skid bases, and four-way and two-way pallets to the buyer's drawing, or to standard footprints: 1200×1000, 1200×800, 1100×1100, and custom oversize for transformer and turbine work. Hardwood runners are jackwood or rubberwood depending on load class.

Container flooring and dunnage

For Mesaieed Port operators and CFS yards rebuilding reefers and dry boxes, we supply 28 mm container-flooring grade panels in standard 2400×1160 mm sizes. Loose dunnage planks in sawn hardwood — 100×100 and 150×150 sections — round out the offer for steel and aluminium shipments.

Decision rules: packing grade vs Okoume vs shuttering

The wrong specification is the most expensive mistake in this trade. A quick filter for Mesaieed buyers:

  • Use commercial / rubberwood packing-grade plywood when the crate is single-trip, the cargo is reasonably tolerant, and cost-per-square-foot matters. This covers the majority of fertilizer-line, general spares, and steel-mill ancillary shipments.
  • Step up to Okoume-faced or BWR packing when the crate will be staged outdoors at Mesaieed for weeks, will see multiple handlings, or carries optical, electronic or precision-machined cargo that cannot afford moisture pickup. Most LNG and instrument shipments sit here.
  • Use shuttering plywood only where the panel itself is the working surface — formwork for jetty extensions, civil works inside the estate, marine fabrication moulds — not for crating. It is overbuilt for packaging and underbuilt for some chemical exposures.
  • Choose ISPM-15 hardwood pallets over plywood pallets for steel coils, aluminium extrusions, and any load over 1.5 tonnes static. Plywood-deck pallets work well for fertilizer bags and consumer goods, not for heavy industrials.

FAQ for Mesaieed buyers

Do your crates and pallets meet ISPM-15 for Qatar customs?

Yes. All hardwood crates, pallets and dunnage destined for Qatar are heat-treated and stamped with the IPPC mark and the treating facility code. Phytosanitary certification is arranged at Kochi before loading.

What minimum order makes a Kochi-to-Mesaieed shipment economic?

A single 20' GP container is the practical floor — roughly 22–24 tonnes of mixed packing-grade plywood, or around 1,000 sq ft of finished crates depending on the build. Sub-container LCL works for sample orders but the per-unit freight gets heavy.

Can you build to a Mesaieed buyer's drawing rather than catalogue sizes?

Yes — most crate orders are drawing-to-build. Send the cargo dimensions, target gross weight, and any handling notes (forklift pockets, lifting lugs, stencilling), and we quote the panel mix, hardwood frame and ISPM-15 treatment together.
