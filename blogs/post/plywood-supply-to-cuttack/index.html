Plywood Supply to Cuttack

Cochin Wood Industries

Cuttack sits at one of the more under-discussed industrial intersections in eastern India. The Mahanadi-Kathajodi twin-river city is the historic capital of Odisha, the home of filigree silver (Tarakasi), a serious handloom and textile cluster, and — less romantically but more relevant to anyone shipping plywood — the gateway to the Choudwar sponge iron and ferro-alloy belt. Every one of those sectors generates outbound cargo that has to be packed into wooden crates, stacked on pallets or sat on a container floor before it leaves the state. That is where the Cochin Wood group comes in.

The Cuttack industrial picture

To plan a plywood supply lane into a city, you have to understand what the city actually makes and ships. Cuttack does four things at industrial scale, and each one buys plywood differently.

Choudwar — sponge iron, ferro-alloys, steel

Choudwar Industrial Estate, about 12 km north of Cuttack city across the Mahanadi, is one of the original IDCO estates and the heart of the local metallurgical belt. Sponge iron (DRI) plants, induction furnaces, ferro-chrome units and re-rolling mills cluster here, extending up toward the Jagatpur and Athagarh nodes. The cargo is heavy, high-density and abrasive: billets, ingots, ferro-alloy lumps, machined castings, spares. For these loads the packaging question is rarely "does plywood look nice" — it is "will the box and pallet hold 800 kg without flexing".

Jagatpur, Madhupatna and the SME belt

Jagatpur Industrial Estate on the city's western fringe, plus Madhupatna and the older Khapuria cluster, host a long tail of engineering job-shops, auto-component units, transformer and switchgear assemblers, plastic moulders and food processors. This is classic export-crate territory — mixed SKUs, moderate weights, frequent shipments via Paradip or Kolkata.

Textiles and handloom — Bayanika, Sambalpuri, Cuttack handloom

Odisha's handloom and textile export footprint runs from Bargarh and Sambalpur through Cuttack, where weaver co-operatives, Odisha State Handloom (Boyanika) hubs and private exporters consolidate stock. Textile cargo is lighter but volumetric; it travels in marine-grade plywood crates with internal partitions and moisture protection.

Tarakasi filigree and high-value handicrafts

Cuttack's filigree silver, brassware and stone-craft exporters move low-volume, very high-value consignments. Air freight is common, and the crates have to be precision-fit with foam inserts. The plywood spec here is about face quality and dimensional accuracy, not raw strength.

What we ship to Cuttack

Okoume face veneer — for handicraft and high-spec exports

Okoume is a face-veneer option on our packing and BWR grades — not a separate "grade" on its own. It gives a clean, light, tight-pored surface that handicraft and silver exporters in Cuttack like for presentation crates and ISPM-15 stencilled boxes that have to look as good as they perform. Okoume-faced sheets are quoted on request because the price moves with veneer availability.

Pallets and container flooring

For Choudwar's metallurgical loads and Paradip-bound textile consignments, the standard pallet decks we cut are 1200x1000 mm and 1100x1100 mm, generally 18 mm deck on a hardwood block-and-runner frame. Where customers run their own pallet shop, we supply the deck sheets in bulk and let them assemble. For 20-ft and 40-ft container floor repairs and heavy-cargo stuffing, 18 mm BWR packing ply in 8x4 ft is the workhorse.

Sawn timber for crate frames

We also dispatch jackwood and rubberwood runners alongside the plywood. Jackwood at Rs 400/cft (quarter-inch minus tolerance) is the usual frame for heavier crates; rubberwood runners are quoted to size.

Kochi to Cuttack — the logistics

Cuttack is roughly 1,700 km from our Perumbavoor dispatch yard by road. Two practical lanes exist:

  1. Road-direct via NH-16 (Chennai-Vijayawada-Visakhapatnam-Bhubaneswar): a 32-foot single-axle full truck takes 5-7 days door-to-door depending on weighbridge and check-post conditions. This is the right answer for full truckloads of 4-5 containers' worth of sheets.
  2. Coastal feeder via Cochin Port / Paradip: for large recurring requirements, sheet cargo can move as a coastal container from Cochin to Paradip, then road-trail the 75 km into Cuttack and Choudwar. Slower in transit but cheaper per sheet at scale, and avoids the inland fuel and toll burn.

For most Cuttack buyers placing their first order with us, the road-direct option wins on simplicity. Once monthly volume crosses a couple of truckloads, the coastal route starts paying back.

Decision rules — which grade for which Cuttack load

  • Sponge iron, ferro-alloy, billet crates (Choudwar): 12 mm or 15 mm commercial packing ply on a jackwood runner frame. Skip okoume — the load doesn't see it.
  • Engineering, auto-component, transformer exports (Jagatpur): 12 mm packing ply panels, 18 mm base. BWR upgrade if the route crosses monsoon transit.
  • Textile and handloom exports (Bayanika, private weavers): 6 mm or 8 mm packing ply with internal moisture-barrier liner. Cost-sensitive segment — don't over-spec.
  • Filigree, brass, stone handicrafts: okoume-faced packing ply, 8-10 mm, with precision foam inserts. Presentation matters as much as strength.
  • Pallet decks and container flooring: 18 mm BWR packing ply, 8x4 ft.
  • Shuttering ply is the right call only if the buyer is on a Cuttack construction site (Mahanadi bridge work, township builds, IDCO infrastructure). It is not a packing material — please don't use it as one.

FAQ

Do you have a depot or dealer in Cuttack?

Not at the moment. We dispatch direct from Perumbavoor to the buyer's yard in Choudwar, Jagatpur or wherever the crate workshop sits. For repeat buyers we hold a quote-locked rate so you don't have to re-negotiate every truck.

What's the minimum order to make Kochi-to-Cuttack worthwhile?

Practically, a full single-axle truck (about 600-700 sheets of 12 mm in 8x4 ft, or equivalent mix). Smaller orders work if they piggyback on an existing eastern-India dispatch — worth asking us when a lane is open.

Can you supply ISPM-15 compliant material?

The ISPM-15 stamp belongs on the finished crate from a registered fumigator, not on the raw plywood. We supply the panels and runners to spec; the heat-treatment certification is done at the crate workshop. Our hardwood ply already meets the moisture and species norms most Cuttack exporters need.
