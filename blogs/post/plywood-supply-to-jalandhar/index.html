Plywood Supply to Jalandhar

Cochin Wood Industries

Why Jalandhar sits on our daily despatch board

Jalandhar is one of the few Indian cities where a packing-grade plywood truck is almost always met with an export carton or crate waiting to be built. The Doaba belt is unusual in that three serious export verticals share the same set of carpenters and packing yards: sports goods, hand-tools and surgical instruments. Each ships in different box sizes, but they all need the same things from a plywood supplier 2,400 km away in Kerala — dimensional stability, a reliable transit time, and a sheet that the local crate-makers can nail and band without splitting. This is a quick operator's view of what the Cochin Wood group ships to Jalandhar, why the lane works, and how to specify the right grade for the load.

The Jalandhar industrial picture

The city's export economy is concentrated in a handful of well-known belts. Anyone running a packing line in Jalandhar will recognise these names — and the plywood flowing into them is broadly the same story across the lot.

Sports goods cluster (alongside Meerut)

Focal Point Phase I to VIII, Basti Nau, Industrial Area-A and the units around Mithapur Road carry most of the sports goods manufacturing. Cricket bats, hockey sticks, footballs, boxing equipment, gym hardware and inflatables move out from here to the UK, Australia, the Gulf and Africa. The packing job is largely export cartons and lightweight crates — flat sheets cut to size, sometimes a die-cut insert.

Hand-tools — the world's largest exporter to Western markets

Jalandhar is the single largest export cluster for hand tools sold into Europe and North America. Wrenches, pliers, hammers, garden tools, agricultural implements — the volumes are heavy and the crates need to hold up under stacked sea transit. Focal Point and the Leather Complex Road belt run a lot of this work, plus the smaller yards around Kapurthala Road and Nakodar Road.

Surgical instruments

Forceps, scalpels, orthopaedic tools and dental instruments — small unit weight but high value, often shipped in nested plywood inner boxes with foam, then crated for the outer. This is precision packing work and the buyers are unforgiving on moisture, splinters and odour.

Adjacent flows

Auto parts and tractor components from the Phagwara–Jalandhar corridor, leather goods from the Leather Complex, and a growing line of agri-machinery exports all add to the same packing demand. Anything export-bound that needs ISPM-15 compliant crating eventually sits on a plywood pallet or inside a plywood box.

Okoume plywood

For the export crates that get visually inspected on arrival — premium surgical kits, branded sports equipment, anything that goes into a Western retail supply chain — Okoume face plies give a cleaner external finish and lower weight per crate. We quote Okoume separately because the pricing tracks African log markets, not the Indian commercial board.

Pallets and crate kits

Standard despatches into Punjab include 1200 x 1000 mm and 1200 x 800 mm pallets with hardwood runners (jackwood at Rs 400/cft or rubberwood as per size), and pre-cut crate sets where the buyer wants to assemble locally. Container flooring (typically 28 mm marine-pattern board for 20-ft and 40-ft container repairs and one-way export crates) is a recurring line.

Sawn timber

Jackwood and rubberwood runners go up with the plywood when the buyer's own carpentry is building bespoke crates around heavier machinery or auto components.

Kochi-to-Jalandhar logistics

The lane is roughly 2,400 km by road. Loaded trucks out of Perumbavoor and the Kochi belt typically run via NH 544 to Salem, NH 44 through Bangalore-Hyderabad-Nagpur, then up via Jhansi-Agra-Delhi-Ambala-Jalandhar. Realistic transit is 6 to 8 days for a single-driver truck, 4 to 5 days with a relay driver, depending on Punjab entry checkpoint timings.

For larger orders we run full-truck despatches direct to the buyer's yard in Focal Point or wherever the packing line sits. For smaller mixed loads we consolidate via established Kochi-to-North-India transporters and offload at the Jalandhar transport nagar. Coastal-shipping to JNPT with onward road to Jalandhar is an option for very large container-equivalent volumes, but on most lanes road-direct from Kochi wins on cost and headache.

One operator note: Punjab's entry and weighment formalities are smoother for full-load consignments with clean GST paperwork than for partials, so we usually advise customers to time their indents to fit a full truck rather than three small ones in a month.

Decision rules — packing vs Okoume vs shuttering

  • Standard hand-tools and sports goods export crates: commercial / rubberwood packing grade in 12 mm for sides and 15-16 mm for the base. This is the workhorse for Jalandhar.
  • Surgical instruments and premium retail-bound sports goods: Okoume face on the outer crate when the brand cares about presentation; commercial packing grade for the inner boxes.
  • Heavy machinery, auto-component and agri-equipment crates: 18 mm packing grade base with jackwood runners; 15 mm packing grade sides.
  • Container flooring repairs / one-way export container builds: 28 mm board, BWR glue line, often paired with hardwood bearers.
  • Shuttering ply: reserved for Jalandhar's construction and infra buyers, not the packing belt. We do despatch it on the same lane but it is a separate conversation.

FAQ for Jalandhar buyers

What is the minimum order you ship to Punjab?

A single full truck (roughly 8 to 10 tonnes of plywood depending on thickness mix). Smaller orders are possible via consolidated transport but the per-sheet landed cost goes up noticeably, so most regular buyers indent in full-truck lots.

How firm are delivery dates from Kochi?

For a confirmed indent against a proforma invoice, the truck is loaded within 3 to 5 working days from our factory dispatch yard, then 6 to 8 days in transit. We share the lorry receipt and driver number on loading; buyers in Focal Point usually plan their packing-line schedule around an 11 to 14 day total window from PI to gate.

Do you supply ISPM-15 heat-treated crates?

Yes — we supply the plywood and the runners; the heat-treatment stamp is applied by accredited fumigation units at the buyer's location, which is the more practical arrangement for export crates being built and stuffed in Jalandhar itself.
