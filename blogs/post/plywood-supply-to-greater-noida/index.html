Plywood Supply to Greater Noida

Cochin Wood Industries

Why Greater Noida is on our supply map

Greater Noida is one of the densest export-packing belts in North India. Within a 25 km radius you have the Honda Cars India plant at Sector 40, Yamaha Motor at Surajpur, New Holland Tractors at Sector Ecotech-III, JBM and Minda component lines, hyperscale data-centre builds at Knowledge Park V, and the Yamuna Expressway corridor pushing toward Jewar Airport. Every one of those facilities pushes out cargo that has to be crated, palletised, and dunnaged in wood before it leaves the gate. That is the demand we ship plywood against, and this page explains how a Kerala-based maker delivers cost-competitively into a city 2,400 km north.

The Greater Noida industrial picture

The packing demand in Greater Noida isn't one cluster — it is at least four overlapping ones, and the plywood spec changes with each.

Auto and auto-component cluster

The Surajpur–Kasna belt (Sectors Ecotech-I through XII, Site-V, and the UPSIDC industrial area) is the heaviest user. Two-wheeler CKD kits going to Latin America and Africa, four-wheeler sub-assemblies, tractor PTO housings, and Tier-1 stamping/forging deliveries all move in plywood cases. The Yamaha CKD lane and the New Holland Tractors export lane out of Greater Noida are particularly steady.

Data centres and IT hardware

The Knowledge Park belt (II, III, V) and the Tech Zone IV cluster around Pari Chowk now host hyperscale and colocation builds. Rack delivery, UPS and chiller crating, and switchgear protection during fit-out drive a specific kind of demand — fewer crates, but heavier units, often single-piece loads above one tonne where the plywood case is structural, not just a wrap.

Film City and engineered goods

The upcoming Film City off the Yamuna Expressway plus the existing engineered-goods makers (pumps, valves, transformers in YEIDA Sectors 28 and 29) generate a steady packing case requirement that is sensitive to FOB price but not to ISPM-15 certification, since most of it moves domestic.

Jewar (Noida International Airport)

As Jewar ramps up, air-freight-grade crates — lighter, dimensionally tight, often 12mm plywood on a softwood frame — will become a growing segment. We are already quoting against that brief from forwarders setting up around Sector 32 and the Multi-Modal Cargo Hub.

What we ship into Greater Noida

Cochin Wood Industries supplies four product families against Greater Noida demand, all manufactured to CWI specifications through our contract factories in the Perumbavoor cluster.

Packing-grade plywood (sheet form)

This is the workhorse. We ship 8x4 ft sheets in 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 15, 16, and 18 mm thicknesses, hardwood face with rubberwood or commercial hardwood core, MR or BWR glue line depending on what the buyer's QC asks for. Selling rates run from ₹24/sq.ft for 6mm up to ₹42/sq.ft for 18mm, ex-factory Perumbavoor, GST and freight extra. For Greater Noida buyers ordering by the truckload, we load full 32-ft single-axle trucks (≈25 tonnes) so the per-sheet inbound freight is at its lowest.

Built-up crates and packing cases

For buyers who do not want to run their own case-making line, we supply finished cases — nailed or bolted, with skid bases, runner-and-batten sides, and lid construction matching the buyer's drawing. Jackwood runners at ₹400/cft are standard for the heavier auto and machinery crates; rubberwood runners on lighter cases.

Wooden pallets

Two-way and four-way entry pallets in 1200x800, 1200x1000, and 1100x1100 mm footprints, in standard-duty and heavy-duty load classes. For data-centre rack movement we typically build a 1200x1000 four-way pallet with a 38–50 mm top deck — strong enough for a 1,200 kg single load with the forklift entry the loading bay actually has.

Container flooring and dunnage

18 mm BWR plywood for container floor repair and one-trip container conversions, plus 12–18 mm sheets cut to dunnage sizes for in-container blocking and bracing. This is a smaller line but a useful one for the CHA and freight-forwarder cluster around the ICD at Dadri.

Kochi to Greater Noida — the logistics that matter

The honest question every North Indian buyer asks is: why source plywood from Kerala when there are mills in UP itself? Two answers.

First, raw material economics. The Perumbavoor belt is built on rubberwood and locally aggregated hardwoods — short, fast turnover, lower core cost than the gurjan-import-dependent mills further north. That gap is usually wider than the freight cost over 2,400 km.

Second, the route is well-trodden. We dispatch from Perumbavoor (≈30 km from Kochi port) by road on the standard NH-44 / Mumbai–Delhi corridor. Transit time for a single-axle 32-ft truck is 5–7 days to the Greater Noida industrial belts, and 6–8 days for a heavier 22-wheeler if the buyer prefers fewer, larger drops. For very large monthly volumes we can also offer rail-on-truck out of the Whitefield/Bangalore container terminals, which trims a day off the transit but adds handling cost.

Decision rules: which grade for which Greater Noida load

A quick operator-level guide for buyers choosing a spec.

  • Packing-grade rubberwood/commercial MR ply — for domestic-moving cases, in-plant pallets, dunnage, and the bulk of the auto-component CKD that doesn't need ISPM-15 fumigation certification. Lowest landed cost per sheet.
  • Okoume face packing ply — when the buyer's export customer specifies a clean, light-coloured face (European auto OEMs and some West African importers do). Cosmetically better, marginally more expensive; we quote on request rather than off the standard card.
  • BWR plywood — for container flooring, exposed dunnage, and any case that may sit in a port stack through a monsoon. Worth the upcharge when the alternative is field rework.
  • Shuttering plywood — separate product line; we send to data-centre civil contractors and Jewar Airport civil packages. Different tooling, quoted separately.

FAQ

How do you handle ISPM-15 for export crates?

We supply the plywood and casework; ISPM-15 fumigation and stamping is typically handled at the buyer's premises or by their nominated treatment agency. Where the buyer needs it, we can coordinate ISPM-15 heat-treated softwood runners through our partner mills.

Can you deliver direct to a sector address inside Greater Noida?

Yes. We deliver to factory gates across YEIDA, GNIDA, and the UPSIDC sectors. For heavy 22-wheelers we confirm the buyer's gate height and turning radius before dispatch — narrow sector roads inside older industrial blocks need a smaller vehicle.

What is the minimum order?

For sheet supply our economic minimum is one full truck (~25 tonnes) to keep per-sheet freight rational. For finished crates and pallets we can ship part loads consolidated with sheet stock, which makes sense for buyers in the 50–200 case-per-month range.
