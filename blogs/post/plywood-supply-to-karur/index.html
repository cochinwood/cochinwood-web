Plywood Supply to Karur

Cochin Wood Industries

Why Karur is on our weekly truck list

Karur sits at the centre of one of India's densest home-textiles export clusters. Bedlinen, terry towels, kitchen linen, table linen and made-ups roll out of mills around the Karur-Pugalur-Aravakurichi belt every week, headed to ports at Tuticorin, Chennai and Cochin and onward to the United States, the European Union, the United Kingdom and the Gulf. With Tamil Nadu home-textiles exports tracking around the Rs 8,000-crore mark in recent years and Karur contributing the lion's share, every consignment needs a wooden bale-crate, a heat-treated pallet, or container dunnage that won't fail in transit. That packaging demand is what brings the Cochin Wood group's trucks up the NH-544 / NH-83 corridor every week.

The Karur industrial picture

Karur's packaging demand isn't one homogeneous market. It splits across four overlapping pockets that buy plywood in different formats:

The home-textiles export belt

Concentrated around Thennilai Road, Andankoil East, Pasupathipalayam and the Karur-Erode Road, this is the heart of the bedlinen and terry-towel industry. Compressed fabric bales need plywood top/bottom caps and edge protectors; full-container shipments need 1140 mm x 1140 mm export pallets and 9 mm to 12 mm plywood load-floor.

SIDCO and SIPCOT estates

The SIDCO Industrial Estate at Thalapatti and the SIPCOT Industrial Complex at Pugalur house a mix of engineering, auto-component and chemical units. These plants need ISPM-15 stamped crates for heavier machinery exports, not just bale-grade ply.

Bus-body and automotive cluster

Karur has a long-standing bus-body building reputation, with workshops along the Karur-Trichy NH route. They consume 18 mm and thicker shuttering and commercial plywood for internal panels, jigs and form-work.

Aravakurichi paper and packaging sub-cluster

The Aravakurichi-Kulithalai stretch carries paper mills and converters who blend corrugated outers with wooden pallets and bottoms for heavy loads. This is a mixed-material zone and we typically supply the wooden-pallet side, not the corrugated side.

Kochi to Karur: the route and the economics

The lane is roughly 280 km from Perumbavoor / Kochi to Karur via NH-544 (Salem road) and NH-83. A loaded trailer leaves the factory in the evening, clears the Walayar check-post, runs through Coimbatore and reaches Karur in 8 to 10 driving hours — most consignments unload by the next morning. We typically run:

  • Full trailer (32 ft / 16 MT) — best economics for bulk plywood sheets, around 800 to 1,000 sheets per trailer depending on thickness.
  • Container truck (20 ft / 9 MT) — for ISPM-15 crate kits and mixed loads with pallets.
  • Part-loads via Coimbatore-Karur consolidators — for smaller monthly off-takes; transit adds about 24 hours.

Because Karur is a regular lane for us, freight cost per sheet stays competitive against North-Kerala-based suppliers despite the longer distance — the trip is direct, the road is good, and the return-leg from Karur often carries textiles back towards Cochin Port, which helps trucker pricing.

Which grade for which load?

Use packing-grade (MR) plywood when

  • The crate is single-trip, port-to-port
  • You are capping or boxing fabric bales, light home-textile cartons
  • Budget per crate matters more than aesthetics

Use Okoume face-veneer plywood when

  • The end-customer sees the crate (premium brand exports)
  • You need a lighter, cleaner-grain face on the outer skin
  • Crate is for high-value machinery, automotive jigs, art / display goods

Use shuttering / 18 mm commercial plywood when

  • The job is bus-body internals, jigs, fixtures or re-usable form-work
  • The crate must survive 3-plus trips
  • You are building pallet decks for heavy machinery exports

FAQ

Do you supply ISPM-15 certified crates ready for export?

Yes. We work with registered HT-treatment facilities and stamp crates with the IPPC mark before dispatch to Karur. Documentation accompanies the consignment so it clears customs without query at Tuticorin or Chennai.

What is the minimum order quantity to Karur?

Economical MOQ is a part-load of about 200 sheets or a full pallet-kit consignment. We also consolidate smaller monthly off-takes for Aravakurichi and Karur-town customers via our Coimbatore part-load partner.

Can you cut sheets to non-standard sizes for fabric-bale caps?

Yes. We cut to size at the factory before loading — the Karur bedlinen and terry-towel sector regularly takes 1200 mm x 600 mm and 1500 mm x 750 mm caps to suit specific bale presses. Cutting charge is nominal and reduces wastage at your end.
