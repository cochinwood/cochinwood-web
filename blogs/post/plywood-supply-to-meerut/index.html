Plywood Supply to Meerut

Cochin Wood Industries

Meerut is one of those western UP cities that quietly does more export packing than people realise. The sports goods belt around Suraj Kund and Sotiganj alone moves containers of cricket bats, balls and protective gear to the UK, Australia, the Gulf and Africa every month. Add the publishing-machinery cluster around Partapur, the scissors-and-shears makers, and the steady stream of auto-component shipments out of Modipuram, and you have a city whose packing-plywood demand is steady, year-round, and surprisingly specific. This is the kind of buyer profile the Cochin Wood group is built around — bulk packing-grade plywood, crate sets, pallets and container flooring, shipped from Kochi to wherever the export consignment is being stuffed.

The Meerut industrial picture

Meerut sits about 70 km north-east of Delhi on NH-58, which puts it squarely inside the National Capital Region's manufacturing pull but with its own distinct industrial DNA. A few belts matter for plywood demand:

  • Suraj Kund and Sotiganj sports goods cluster — the historic centre of the Indian cricket-equipment trade. Bats, balls, pads, gloves and protective wear are made here and exported through ICDs at Tughlakabad, Loni and Garhi Harsaru. Most of this freight ships in wooden crates or on plywood-decked pallets because air-freight insurance underwriters and overseas buyers ask for it.
  • Partapur Industrial Area — the larger UPSIDC estate on the Delhi road. Printing-and-publishing machinery (Meerut still has a working publishing and bookbinding equipment trade), transformer and electrical component manufacturers, and a number of food-processing units all sit here. Heavy, dense machine consignments need ISPM-15 crates and proper bracing-grade plywood.
  • Mohkampur, Udyog Puram and Modipuram — auto components, agricultural implements and metal fabrication. Pallet-heavy buyers.
  • Scissors and shears trade in the old city — smaller cartons mostly, but bulk export consolidators in this trade still buy plywood-bottom master cases.

The common thread: most of Meerut's high-value shipments leave the country, and packing failure is not an option. That puts a real floor under the quality of plywood the city's packing-case makers and exporters can use.

What we ship to Meerut

Cochin Wood Industries supplies four product families into Meerut. The mix on any given truck depends on what is being packed.

Packing-grade plywood

This is the workhorse. Hardwood-core, phenolic-bonded plywood in 8 ft x 4 ft sheets, available in 6 mm, 8 mm, 10 mm, 12 mm, 14 mm, 15 mm, 16 mm and 18 mm. For Meerut's sports goods exporters, 8 mm and 10 mm are the most common — light enough to keep the cubic-metre weight of a cricket-bat container in check, strong enough to survive port handling. For the publishing-machinery and transformer crowd, 12 mm to 18 mm is the right band because the loads are dense and the crate has to take stacking pressure in the container.

Knocked-down crate sets

For exporters who don't want to manage a captive carpentry shop, we ship pre-cut crate components — top, bottom, sides, ends, runners — fumigated and stencilled ISPM-15 where required. This shortens the loading window in Partapur from days to hours.

Pallets

Two-way and four-way entry pallets, hardwood blocks or stringers, sized to the buyer's container plan. Standard sizes for Meerut buyers tend to be 1200 x 1000 mm (Euro-style for UK/EU lanes) and 1100 x 1100 mm (Gulf and ASEAN lanes). Heat-treated and stamped where the destination requires it.

Container flooring plywood

Heavy 18 mm plywood for repair and refit of dry vans and trailer beds. The Modipuram and Mohkampur trucking fleets running between Meerut and the Mundra/Nhava Sheva ICDs are steady buyers for this.

Kochi to Meerut: the logistics

Meerut sits roughly 2,400 km by road from our Kochi loading yards. The lane we use most:

  • Route: Kochi → Salem → Bengaluru → Hyderabad → Nagpur → Gwalior → Agra → Meerut, mostly on NH-44 and NH-58.
  • Transit time: 6 to 8 days for a single-driver truck, 4 to 5 days with a two-driver relay. We plan to either.
  • Truck size: standard option is a 32-ft single-axle multi-axle carrying about 18 to 20 tonnes of mixed-thickness plywood. For full-crate orders we use 32-ft high-cube bodies that handle bulky knocked-down sets without crushing.
  • Alternative: for very large orders we will FOB Cochin to Mundra or JNPT by coastal vessel and rail the container to Tughlakabad ICD for last-mile to Meerut. This works for orders that fill a 20-ft or 40-ft container outright.

GST and transport are billed extra on top of our ex-factory price card. We share a landed-cost estimate when we send the proforma invoice so the Meerut buyer knows the full delivered number before committing.

FAQ for Meerut buyers

Do you supply ISPM-15 stamped crates and pallets?

Yes. We heat-treat at registered facilities and stencil the IPPC mark, country code IN, treatment code HT and the registered facility number. This is the standard for exports to the EU, UK, Australia and most other destinations Meerut ships to.

What is the minimum order you will ship to Meerut?

One full truck — roughly 18 to 20 tonnes of plywood, or the equivalent in crate sets and pallets. Smaller part-loads are possible if we can consolidate with another Delhi-NCR delivery, but full-truck pricing is always better for the buyer.

How do payment and GST work for an out-of-state buyer?

We bill ex-factory Kochi with IGST applicable for an inter-state supply to UP. Transport is billed separately or arranged by the buyer's nominated transporter. We share the proforma invoice with the full breakup so there are no surprises at delivery.
