Plywood Supply to Duqm

Cochin Wood Industries

Duqm has moved faster than most procurement teams expected. What was a fishing town on the Arabian Sea a decade ago is now SEZAD — the Special Economic Zone at Duqm — with a drydock running heavy MRO contracts, a 230,000 bpd refinery commissioned, a green-hydrogen ecosystem under build-out, and a deep-water port that has reshaped how Gulf cargo moves. For an Indian plywood supplier, that combination produces a very specific demand pattern: heavy, high-value, often awkwardly shaped equipment that needs to leave a fabrication shop, survive an ocean leg, clear customs, and arrive at a site address inside SEZAD without damage. That is a crate-and-pallet problem, and it is the workflow our packing-grade plywood, sawn timber and shuttering ply are built around.

The Duqm industrial picture — what is actually being built

If you are sourcing packaging materials into Duqm, it helps to know which clusters are generating the orders. The footprint is wider than most one-line summaries suggest.

SEZAD's core zones

  • Duqm Drydock — Oman Drydock Company runs two graving docks and is a regional hub for VLCC, FPSO and naval refits. MRO work means heavy spares, replacement valves, propeller assemblies and turbine sections moving in and out — almost all of which travel in heavy-duty wooden crates.
  • Duqm Refinery and Petrochemical Industries (OQ8) — the 230,000 bpd refinery and its downstream petchem expansion mean continuous flow of catalysts, instrumentation, spare pumps and compressor parts. Catalyst drums, instrument transmitters and rotating equipment all need crate-grade ply or pallet-mounted boxes.
  • Green hydrogen and renewables belt — Hyport Duqm, Hyduqm and other green-H2 / ammonia projects are bringing in electrolyser stacks, transformers and module skids from European and Asian fabricators. ISPM-15 compliant crating is mandatory.
  • Heavy industry, steel and minerals — Jindal Shadeed's expansion, Karwa Motors' assembly, vehicle CKD lines, and the cement / mining feedstock corridor all pull pallet volumes for component movement inside the zone.
  • Fisheries and food processing cluster — at the southern edge of SEZAD, this sector runs on hardwood pallets and lighter crates for export consignments to GCC retail.
  • Port of Duqm and logistics corridor — the dry port, container freight stations, and the Duqm–Thumrait–Salalah road link generate pallet demand for re-stuffing and warehousing operations.

Each of these clusters has a different unit economics. The drydock and refinery generate low-volume, high-value, heavy crates. Green-H2 EPC contractors generate spikes of large, awkward modules. The fisheries and logistics layers generate steady pallet pull-through. A supplier that can move across all three formats has the easier conversation.

Okoume face plywood

Where the cargo is being re-exported into European buyers, or where the EPC tender specifies a smoother, lighter face with cleaner appearance, the Okoume option moves up. Okoume is a face-veneer option on our MR-Packing and BWR cores — it is not a separate "grade" but a face choice that changes weight, finish and price. We quote Okoume separately on request because the spec depends on what is being shipped.

Crates, pallets and container flooring

  • Heavy-duty equipment crates — built around 15 mm and 18 mm panels with hardwood frames; designed for 1–10 tonne payloads, the typical refinery spare and drydock part envelope.
  • Standard export pallets — four-way entry, 1200x1000 mm and 1100x1100 mm being the two formats most Duqm logistics terminals handle without slowing down.
  • Heat-treated (HT) and ISPM-15 marked — required for any wood entering Oman; this is a non-negotiable customs reality and a frequent rejection cause if the supplier is not set up for it.
  • Container flooring and skids — 18 mm and thicker for replacement floor sections, machinery skids, and roll-on roll-off staging.
  • Sawn timber — jackwood at INR 400/cft (¼-inch minus tolerance) and rubberwood runners for cleats, gussets and skid construction.

Kochi-to-Duqm logistics

The distance from Kochi to Duqm is roughly 1,700 nautical miles by sea. Container services routed via transhipment hubs typically land cargo in Duqm in 14 to 21 days from Cochin port, depending on the carrier and connection point. Bulk break-bulk and project cargo can also load at Kochi for direct GCC sailings. For most plywood, crate and pallet shipments we work on FOB Cochin or CFR Duqm terms; the buyer chooses the incoterm based on whether they have a freight forwarder already on the lane.

Two operational notes worth flagging:

  • ISPM-15 documentation has to travel with the shipment, not arrive separately by email. We mark and certify at packing, not at port.
  • Moisture content is a real issue on the Kochi–Duqm leg. Kerala monsoon stock that has not been kiln-dried below 12% MC can develop fungal staining in the container during transit. We dry-stack and condition material before despatch for this reason.

Decision rules — which spec for which Duqm load

Use packing-grade (MR-Packing)

When the cargo is internal-zone movement, MRO spares moving between drydock and fabrication shop, fisheries and food-cluster pallets, and standard refinery spares packing. This is the default for most SEZAD packaging tenders.

Use Okoume-faced ply

When the receiver is a European fabricator (common for green-H2 module re-export), when the crate will be photographed and audited, or when weight per panel matters and a cleaner face is specified. Okoume sits about 15–25% above commercial packing on price.

Use shuttering ply

When the customer is a SEZAD civil contractor casting foundations, jetty extensions, refinery tankage pads, or zone road formwork — not a packaging buyer. Different conversation, different spec sheet.

FAQ

Do you ship ISPM-15 certified wood to Oman?

Yes. All crates, pallets and sawn timber going to Duqm leave Kochi heat-treated and stamped per ISPM-15. Phytosanitary certification is arranged at port. Customs in Oman rejects untreated wood at inspection, so we do not ship any other way.

What is the minimum order size for a Duqm shipment?

Practical minimum is one 20-foot container — roughly 500–650 sheets of 18 mm packing ply, or a mixed load of crates and pallets. Smaller orders can ride as LCL through Cochin port but the per-unit freight cost usually does not work out in the buyer's favour below half a container.

Can you build to a SEZAD contractor's drawing?

Yes. Most refinery and drydock spares ship in custom crates against an outline drawing and a weight statement. Share the drawing, weight, lifting points and any sea-fastening requirements and we quote against the build.
