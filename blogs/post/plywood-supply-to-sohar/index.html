Plywood Supply to Sohar

Cochin Wood Industries

Sohar sits in a strange and useful spot on the GCC map. It is not the political capital, it is not the financial capital, but for anyone who actually moves heavy industrial cargo, Sohar Industrial Port and the surrounding Al Batinah belt is where the steel coils, aluminium billets, and refinery sub-assemblies physically leave the country. That activity sets a very specific demand for plywood: not architectural sheets for showrooms, but tough, dimensionally honest boards that survive forklift loads, sea-spray, and three-week transits. The Cochin Wood group has spent forty years working on exactly this kind of plywood out of Perumbavoor, and we ship into the Sohar industrial belt from our Kochi base regularly enough that the route, the documentation, and the spec sheet are now routine.

The Sohar industrial picture

Sohar's industrial economy is anchored by three clusters, and each one drives a different plywood requirement. Knowing which cluster the buyer is in usually tells us within five minutes what board, what thickness, and what crate profile to quote.

Steel and metals at Sohar Port

Jindal Shadeed's integrated steel complex and the Vale Oman iron-ore pelletising operation sit inside the Sohar Industrial Port footprint. The downstream rebar mills, wire-rod drawers, and cold-rolling units that have grown around them ship heavy, dense, abrasive cargo. Steel coils, billets, and pipe bundles all need plywood that can carry concentrated point loads without crushing, and that will not splinter under banding tension.

Aluminium and the Sohar Aluminium cluster

Sohar Aluminium's smelter and the satellite extrusion and rolling mills inside the Sohar Free Zone push out billets, slabs, and finished extrusions to Europe and South Asia. Aluminium cargo is lighter than steel per cubic metre but far more surface-sensitive, so the plywood specification leans toward smooth-faced packing grades with tighter face quality.

Refining, petrochemicals, and capital equipment

Sohar Refinery and the Liwa Plastics complex generate a steady flow of equipment movement, both for original installation and for shutdown and turnaround windows. Pumps, heat exchangers, control panels, and skid-mounted modules ride out on custom plywood crates. These are the lower-volume but higher-spec orders, where ISPM-15 marking, moisture control, and corner protection matter more than sheer board count.

Free Zone fabricators and general industry

Sohar Free Zone (SOHAR Port and Freezone) hosts food processing, prefab, marine fabrication, and a growing cluster of light engineering tenants on the Al Batinah coastal strip. These tenants order in smaller, mixed batches: a few pallets here, a custom crate there, and occasionally a full container of sheets for their own internal packing line.

What we ship into Sohar

Our standard Sohar quotation usually covers four product families. We try to keep the spec language simple so the receiving warehouse and the freight forwarder both read the same thing.

Packing-grade plywood (MR / commercial)

This is the workhorse. Hardwood face and back, hardwood-core construction, Moisture Resistant glue line, sized for industrial packing rather than furniture. Common thicknesses we move to Sohar:

ThicknessTypical Sohar use
6 mmCrate panel cladding, light box sides
8 mmMid-weight crates, top panels, partitions
12 mmStandard crate bodies for steel and aluminium
15 mm and 18 mmCrate floors, heavy-equipment skids, container flooring

Standard size is 8 x 4 feet (2440 x 1220 mm). Tighter cut sizes are produced to the customer's bill of materials when the volume justifies it.

Okoume-faced plywood

Okoume is offered as a face veneer option on our packing-grade and BWR cores, not a separate grade. It comes into play in Sohar mainly for aluminium and finished-goods crates where face cleanliness matters, and for export buyers who simply prefer the lighter, more uniform face for their own brand crates. Light, smooth, paint-friendly, and visually consistent crate to crate.

Wooden crates and pallets

We supply both flat-pack crate kits (cheaper to freight, assembled at the Sohar end) and assembled crates and pallets where the customer wants ready-to-use units off the container. Common pallet footprints we build for the Sohar lane:

  • 1200 x 1000 mm — the dominant GCC industrial size, suits most steel and aluminium loads
  • 1200 x 800 mm — Euro footprint, for tenants whose downstream customers are in Europe
  • 1100 x 1100 mm — for buyers feeding into South-East Asian destinations

All export wood packaging from our side is ISPM-15 heat-treated and stamp-marked at the treatment yard, which is the spec Omani customs expects.

Container flooring and shuttering ply

For container repair yards and reefer / dry-box refurbishers in the Sohar Port area, we supply 28 mm container-floor grade boards on request. Shuttering ply (filmfaced and plain) is the other line that goes into the Sohar belt, mostly for construction contractors working on plant expansions inside the Free Zone.

Kochi-to-Sohar logistics

The Sohar lane is one of the more comfortable export routes for us. The voyage from Cochin Port to Sohar Port runs roughly 1,500 nautical miles across the Arabian Sea. Direct services and transhipment via Jebel Ali or Khor Fakkan are both routinely available, and our typical port-to-port transit sits in the 6 to 10 day band depending on the carrier rotation. Door-to-port from our Perumbavoor consolidation yard to the Cochin gate is a half-day trucking move, which keeps inland costs low compared to factories sitting further north in India.

On documentation we run the standard kit — commercial invoice, packing list, Certificate of Origin, ISPM-15 fumigation certificate for the wood packaging, and Bill of Lading. Buyers usually take delivery on FOB Cochin or CFR Sohar terms; we will quote either, and we are comfortable working with the buyer's nominated forwarder if there is an existing relationship.

Decision rules — which board for which load

Most Sohar enquiries collapse into one of three load profiles, and the right plywood call is usually clear once we know the cargo.

Heavy, abrasive, dense loads (steel, billets, pipe)

Packing-grade hardwood plywood at 12 mm for body panels and 15 to 18 mm for crate floors and skids. Face cleanliness is not the priority — load-bearing and impact resistance are. Standard MR commercial face is fine.

Surface-sensitive, mid-weight loads (aluminium, finished metals, machined parts)

Okoume-faced packing grade for outer panels and lid; standard hardwood ply for the structural frame. The Okoume face protects the cargo from facing scuffs and gives the buyer a cleaner crate to put their brand on.

Capital equipment and project cargo (pumps, panels, skids)

BWR-glue plywood, 18 mm minimum on the base, custom-built crate with internal bracing. For project shipments that may sit on a quayside for weeks, BWR (Boiling Water Resistant) glue is worth the small premium — it survives humidity, rain, and condensation far better than plain MR.

FAQ

Do you supply ISPM-15 compliant wood packaging for Oman?

Yes. All export crates, pallets, and dunnage going from us are heat-treated at an approved IPPC facility and carry the ISPM-15 stamp. The fumigation certificate goes into the export document set so the cargo clears Sohar customs without holds.

Can you produce against a buyer's drawing or bill of materials?

Yes. Custom crate sizes, internal bracing, foam-lined inserts, and labelled panels are routine for our packing line. We ask for a clear drawing or a sample crate; turnaround on first article is usually a week.

What is the minimum order for a Sohar shipment?

Practical minimum is one 20-foot or 40-foot container, mixed product if needed. For smaller volumes we can consolidate with other GCC-bound cargo, but lead time stretches because we wait for the consolidation to fill.
