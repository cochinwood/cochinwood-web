Plywood Supply to Raipur

Cochin Wood Industries

Raipur sits at the centre of one of India's heaviest industrial corridors. The city is the administrative capital of Chhattisgarh, but its real economic identity is steel, iron, sponge iron, and rice — three sectors that move enormous tonnages out by road and rail every working day. Every steel coil, every sponge iron billet bound for a foundry, and every premium basmati or non-basmati consignment leaving Raipur needs packaging that can survive multi-modal handling, monsoon humidity, and in many cases an ISPM-15 export leg. That is where packing-grade plywood, crates, pallets, and container flooring become a routine purchase item rather than an occasional spend. This guide explains what the Cochin Wood group ships into Raipur, why Kochi-to-Raipur is a workable lane despite the distance, and how to match grade to load.

The Raipur industrial picture

Raipur's manufacturing footprint is concentrated in a handful of well-defined belts. Understanding which belt a buyer sits in usually tells us what packing they need.

Siltara industrial area

Siltara, on the northern edge of Raipur, is dominated by steel — integrated steel plants, rolling mills, sponge iron units, and ferro alloy producers. Loads here are dense, sharp-edged, and frequently bound for domestic dispatch by truck or rail rake. Crates and pallets need to handle point loads from billets, plates, and coils without splitting at the corners.

Urla industrial area

Urla is the older, more diversified estate — steel re-rolling, structural fabrication, engineering, and a long tail of ancillary units. Smaller engineering exporters here ship machined components, castings, and fabricated assemblies, often into the Middle East. Wooden boxes with ISPM-15 stamped pallets and ply linings are routine.

Atal Nagar (Naya Raipur) and the Sirgitti / Bhilai overflow

Atal Nagar is the planned capital city, attracting newer manufacturing and logistics parks. Bhilai, an hour west, is the Steel Authority of India anchor and pulls a long shadow of ancillary packaging demand across the corridor. Buyers from Bhilai and Sirgitti regularly source from Raipur consolidators.

The rice milling cluster

Chhattisgarh is one of India's largest paddy producers, and Raipur, Tilda, and Dhamtari host hundreds of rice mills. Export-grade rice — particularly to the GCC and African markets — moves in wooden crates or on pallets inside containers. The packaging spec is lighter than steel, but the volume is steady year-round.

What we ship to Raipur

The Cochin Wood group has been in Perumbavoor since 1986, and 40+ years of group manufacturing experience sits behind the specifications we supply today. Three product families move into Raipur most often.

Packing-grade plywood

This is the workhorse for the steel and rice belts. Standard thicknesses sold into Raipur:

  • 8mm / 10mm — light crates, internal partitions, rice export box panels.
  • 12mm — the most common general-purpose packing thickness; tops and sides of engineering crates.
  • 15mm / 16mm — heavier engineering and steel component crates, pallet decks for medium loads.
  • 18mm — heavy steel crates, pallet stringers, container flooring patches, and load-bearing tops.

Sizes are made to packing-case standards rather than furniture standards. Sheets are pressed for moisture tolerance suitable for monsoon-season storage at the consignee end. All quoted ex-factory; GST and transport extra.

Crate construction inputs

Most Raipur crate makers buy plywood sheets plus sawn timber runners. We supply hardwood and rubberwood runners cut to the typical sections used in box construction — heavier sections for steel crates, lighter sections for rice and engineering boxes. Jackwood runners at ¼-inch-minus tolerance are available for buyers who specify them.

Pallet sizes

Standard pallet decks supplied to the Raipur belt include 1200×1000 mm (the most common export footprint into the GCC), 1100×1100 mm (Asian standard), and custom sizes for steel coil cradles. Deck thickness usually 15mm or 18mm depending on the load class.

Container flooring

For exporters loading 20-ft or 40-ft containers with heavy steel and engineering goods, container flooring patches and load-spreading sheets are quoted on request. This typically uses 18mm packing-grade ply and is sized to the container floor and load points.

Kochi to Raipur — logistics

Raipur is roughly 1,500 km from Kochi by road. Two routings work in practice:

  • Direct trucking lane — Kochi / Ernakulam → Bengaluru → Hyderabad → Nagpur → Raipur. Multi-axle trailers run this route in 4 to 6 days depending on season, check-post conditions, and driver changeovers.
  • Coastal port + inland — for very large consignments, Cochin port to Visakhapatnam by coastal shipping and then inland by rake or truck. Slower but cheaper per cubic foot once volumes cross full container lots.

The freight rate is the single largest variable in landed cost. Direct truck FTL is usually the right answer for crate makers ordering 1 to 2 truckloads per cycle. We quote ex-factory and let the buyer choose transport, or arrange transport on request.

FAQ

How long does delivery to Raipur take from Kochi?

4 to 6 days by direct trucking lane (Kochi → Bengaluru → Hyderabad → Nagpur → Raipur) for an FTL load. Coastal port plus inland is slower but can be cheaper at scale. Confirmed once thickness, quantity, and delivery point in Raipur are agreed.

Do you supply ISPM-15 compliant material for export crates?

Plywood as a manufactured board is exempt from ISPM-15 marking under ISPM-15 itself. The runners, blocks, and any solid wood components in the crate must be ISPM-15 heat-treated and stamped — we can supply runners specified accordingly. Crate fabrication is done at the buyer's end or through their nominated box maker.

What is the minimum order size for a Raipur delivery?

The economics work best at full truckload (roughly 800-1,000 sheets of 12mm equivalent). Smaller orders can be combined with other consignments on the south-to-central lane but quote depends on combinability at the time of booking.
