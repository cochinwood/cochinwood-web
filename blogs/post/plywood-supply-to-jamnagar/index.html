Plywood Supply to Jamnagar

Cochin Wood Industries

Why Jamnagar is a packing-plywood town

Jamnagar is a strange and useful city for anyone who sells packing-grade plywood. In one district you have the world's largest brass parts cluster shipping small, dense, high-value components in plywood-lined cartons; the world's largest single-location oil refinery on the coast at Motikhavdi crating spares and equipment; and Sikka Port handling project cargo that needs ISPM-15 wooden cases. Three different load profiles, three different plywood specs, one freight lane down to it from Kerala. Below is how the Cochin Wood group thinks about supply into Jamnagar — what we ship, why, and how the Kochi-to-Jamnagar leg actually works.

The Jamnagar industrial picture

The brass cluster is the loudest signal. Jamnagar accounts for the dominant share of India's brass parts exports, and most of that output moves through micro and small units concentrated in GIDC Phase I, Phase II and Phase III at Shankar Tekri, plus the older Dared, Digjam and Hapa belts. These are cable glands, sanitary fittings, automotive terminals, valve bodies, electrical accessories — small components, but each kilogram has real value, and every consignment for Europe, the Gulf or North America needs to be palletised, sometimes crated, and almost always export-compliant.

Around that you have:

  • Reliance Jamnagar refinery at Motikhavdi and the SEZ at Moti Khavdi — process equipment, instrumentation spares, catalyst drums, project cargo. Crates here tend to be heavy, often custom-built, occasionally tropicalised.
  • Sikka Port and Bedi Port — bulk and project cargo, with refinery-linked outbound and inbound steel structures, vessels and pipes that need timber dunnage and crating.
  • Bandhani textile and food processing units around Khambhalia Road — lighter crating, more pallet work.
  • Dhrol and Jamnagar GIDC engineering shops — CNC components, pump castings, valves bound for OEMs across India and the Gulf.

Each of these segments buys plywood differently. The brass exporter wants a clean 8 mm or 10 mm sheet that crates a 25 kg carton without splitting at the staple line. The refinery contractor wants 18 mm BWR for crates that may sit on a project site through a monsoon. The pallet maker wants consistent thickness and weight so loaded pallets stack predictably in a container.

Crate construction

For Jamnagar's heavier outbound — refinery spares, large brass valve bodies, finished pump assemblies — we supply timber and plywood pre-cut to the customer's crate drawing, or we ship the materials and let the buyer's carpentry shop build to ISPM-15 if the consignment is bound for export through Mundra or Pipavav. Typical crate skin is 12 mm or 18 mm packing-grade ply over a hardwood framework, with jackwood or rubberwood runners.

Pallets

For Jamnagar pallet makers and for in-house refinery and SEZ pallet replacements, we supply the deck plywood (10 mm and 12 mm) plus sawn runners. Common footprints are the standard 1200 x 1000 mm export pallet, the 1100 x 1100 mm GCC pallet (heavily used for shipments out of Sikka to UAE and Saudi), and the 1200 x 800 mm euro pallet for European brass buyers.

Container flooring

For container repair shops working near Sikka and along the Mundra feeder lane, we supply 18 mm hardwood plywood as flooring replacement stock. Not the headline volume, but a steady ancillary line that often pairs with the same lorry as a packing-ply order.

Kochi to Jamnagar — the logistics reality

The road distance from Perumbavoor to Jamnagar is roughly 2000 km, typically routed Salem - Bengaluru - Hubballi - Pune - Surat - Rajkot - Jamnagar, or via the Konkan corridor depending on the operator. A full-truck 32-foot multi-axle load lands in five to seven days door-to-door once it leaves our dispatch yard, assuming no monsoon disruption on the Western Ghats.

A few practical notes from running this lane:

  • 32-foot SXL with around 9.5 tonnes of packing ply is the sweet spot. Below that, per-sheet freight gets ugly.
  • For partial loads, we consolidate with other Gujarat-bound consignments out of Kochi — adds two to three days but cuts freight cost meaningfully for first-time Jamnagar buyers.
  • Coastal break-bulk via Cochin to Pipavav is an option for very large project orders, but the road lane is usually faster and cheaper for the kind of 5 to 15 tonne consignments Jamnagar typically orders.
  • Delivery is normally to the buyer's godown in Shankar Tekri, Dared or the GIDC plots; refinery-bound material goes to the contractor's yard, not direct to the refinery gate.

Which grade for which Jamnagar load

Three decision rules that have held up across this freight lane:

  1. Packing-grade commercial or rubberwood ply — default for brass carton work, pallet decks, and any crate that ships and then gets discarded. Cost-efficient, predictable, calibrated for one-trip packaging.
  2. Okoume face plywood — quote on request. Worth specifying when the buyer wants a cleaner face for export crates that will sit on a customer's receiving dock and represent the brand, or for premium GCC-bound consignments. Priced separately from the packing card.
  3. Shuttering ply — for the construction side of Jamnagar's expansion (warehousing in GIDC, civil works at the SEZ). Different spec, different conversation, but on the same truck if the buyer is mixed-segment.

Jamnagar buyer FAQ

Do you supply ISPM-15 fumigated crates direct?

We supply the plywood and timber. ISPM-15 stamping is done by the buyer's carpentry shop with a certified fumigator, which is the standard practice across Gujarat's export belt. We can recommend specs that suit ISPM-15 compliance.

What is your minimum order to Jamnagar?

For a dedicated truck, one full 32-foot load. For consolidated freight, we work down to roughly 2.5 to 3 tonnes — useful for first orders or trial batches before a buyer commits to FTL.

How long has the Cochin Wood group been doing this?

The Cochin Wood group has operated out of Perumbavoor since 1986, more than four decades of plywood and timber. CWI Pvt Ltd is the corporate vehicle through which we run the modern packing and Okoume business, including the Gujarat lane.
