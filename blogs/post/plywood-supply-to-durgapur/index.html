Plywood Supply to Durgapur

Cochin Wood Industries

Durgapur runs on heavy metal. The Durgapur Steel Plant rolls coils and plates that need to leave the city in protective wooden cases. Mining and Allied Machinery Corporation (MAMC) and the cluster of foundries, forging units and alloy steel makers around the DSP-MAMC belt all ship finished goods that have to survive a 1,500 to 2,500 km road or rail journey before they reach the end customer. Every one of those movements creates a plywood requirement — packing-grade sheets for case panels, hardwood runners for skids, and container flooring for export consolidation. This is the bread-and-butter of what we ship into West Bengal from Kochi, and Durgapur is one of the more interesting addresses on the lane because the load profile is so heavy and so specific.

The Durgapur industrial picture

Durgapur is a planned steel township in Paschim Bardhaman district, about 175 km north-west of Kolkata along NH-19. It is one of the older Tier-2 industrial cities in eastern India and the second-largest urban-industrial centre in West Bengal after Kolkata. For a plywood supplier, the city is interesting because the bulk demand is concentrated, not scattered — a handful of estates absorb most of the wooden packaging tonnage.

Estates and belts to know

  • Durgapur Steel Plant (SAIL) township and approach roads — coil packing, plate banding skids, and ancillary structural shipments.
  • MAMC industrial belt — heavy mining machinery, draglines, conveyor parts. These ship in custom-built crates with thick base boards.
  • Durgapur Projects Ltd (DPL) area — fabrication and power-equipment ancillaries.
  • WBIIDC industrial estates: Bidhannagar (Durgapur), Bamunara, Rajbandh, Muchipara, Gopalpur — secondary engineering, alloy steel re-rollers, refractories, and a growing cluster of light engineering units that crate finished goods for dispatch.
  • Sponge iron and ferro-alloy clusters around Raniganj–Andal–Pandaveswar — adjacent to Durgapur and pulling from the same packing supply chain.

The common thread is heavy goods that need wooden packaging built to handle weight and abrasion, not consumer-grade boxes. That is exactly the slot packing-grade plywood is designed for.

What we ship to Durgapur

For Durgapur, three product lines pull most of the volume.

2. Sawn timber runners and skids

Plywood panels are only half the crate. The structural skeleton — runners, headers, base skids — is sawn hardwood. We supply jackwood at Rs 400 per cft (quarter-inch minus tolerance) and rubberwood runners as per the sizes the customer's design calls for. Most Durgapur crate makers cut their own runners from sized stock, so we ship in standard scantling.

3. Container flooring and export crates

Exporters routing through Kolkata/Haldia ports for shipments out of eastern India often need 18 mm BWP-equivalent panels for container flooring or for crates that will see a sea voyage. Where the box has to last six weeks in marine humidity, we recommend stepping up to the boiling-water-resistant grade rather than running standard packing ply.

4. Okoume-faced premium packing

Okoume is a face-veneer option layered onto the packing or BWR core. It is the right choice when the consignee is overseas, when crate aesthetics matter (display goods, precision instruments) or when the carton needs a smoother, brand-printable surface. Quoted separately on request.

Kochi to Durgapur — the logistics reality

The road distance from Kochi to Durgapur is approximately 2,050 km. The practical route runs Kochi → Bengaluru → Hyderabad → Vijayawada → Vizag → Bhubaneswar → Kharagpur → Durgapur, mostly on NH-44 / NH-16 / NH-19. We dispatch in full-truck loads — a single 32-ft multi-axle trailer carries roughly 24-26 tonnes of sheet stock, and a container load works out cheaper per sq.ft than LCL once you cross about 800 sheets of 12 mm.

  • Transit time: typically 6-8 days door-to-door by road, depending on the season and Odisha-coast weather.
  • Rail option: for very large standing orders, parcel-rake or container-rake economics start to make sense, but most Durgapur buyers stay on road for flexibility.
  • Port routing: for export-bound buyers, we can also ship FOB Cochin or DDU Haldia/Kolkata when the end customer is overseas — useful for the MAMC-style heavy-machinery exporter.

Decision rules — which grade for which load?

Use packing-grade (commercial / rubberwood-faced) when:

  • The crate will be opened at the destination within 4-6 weeks.
  • The freight is road or short-haul rail within India.
  • Cost-per-sheet matters and the load is well under the panel's structural limit.

Use BWR / boiling-water-resistant when:

  • The crate sees humid storage, monsoon transit or a sea leg.
  • The end use is container flooring or marine-export packing.

Use shuttering ply when:

  • The need is concrete formwork for plant expansion, not packing — common for projects inside the DSP and DPL complexes.

Add an Okoume face when:

  • The crate carries premium or branded goods.
  • The buyer specifies a smoother surface or better screw-holding for repeated assembly.

FAQ

What is the minimum order quantity you ship to Durgapur?

Most Durgapur dispatches are full-truck loads. Mixed thickness on one truck is fine. For trial orders we can co-load with another West Bengal destination, but the freight per sq.ft is meaningfully better at full-truck volumes.

How long from order confirmation to delivery in Durgapur?

For standard packing-grade thicknesses we ship within 3-5 working days from advance confirmation, plus 6-8 days transit. Custom-cut sizes or Okoume-faced runs add a few days.

Are your rates inclusive of GST and freight?

The ex-factory rates published above are exclusive. GST (12% on plywood) and freight to Durgapur are quoted line-wise on the proforma invoice. Freight depends on diesel and return-load availability on the lane.
