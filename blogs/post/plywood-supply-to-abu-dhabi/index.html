Plywood Supply to Abu Dhabi

Cochin Wood Industries

Why Abu Dhabi's industrial belt pulls steady plywood demand from Kochi

Abu Dhabi is not just the federal capital of the UAE — it is the operational backbone of the country's oil, gas, and heavy-engineering economy. Between the Khalifa Industrial Zone Abu Dhabi (KIZAD), the Mussafah industrial belt, and the offshore service yards feeding ADNOC's upstream and downstream operations, the emirate generates a constant flow of project cargo, modular equipment, and spare-parts crating that all has to leave the country in compliant, ISPM-15-marked wooden packaging. Kochi sits roughly 2,000 nautical miles away across the Arabian Sea — close enough that container economics work in our favour and far enough that local Indian plywood from Perumbavoor reaches Khalifa Port at delivered costs that Southeast Asian suppliers cannot match on lead time. That equation is why the Cochin Wood group has shipped packing-grade plywood, knocked-down crate kits, and pallet stock into the Abu Dhabi corridor for years now, and why this guide unpacks how that supply chain actually runs.

The Abu Dhabi industrial picture

Before talking specifications, it helps to understand which industrial estates and sectors actually generate the plywood demand. Abu Dhabi is geographically vast, but the packaging consumption is concentrated in a few belts.

KIZAD and Khalifa Port

KIZAD is one of the largest integrated trade and logistics zones in the region — petrochemicals, metals, polymers, EV components, pharmaceuticals, food processing, and a growing cluster of heavy fabrication. It sits adjacent to Khalifa Port, which has the deepest draft of any port in the UAE and is geared for ULCS class vessels. Manufacturers inside KIZAD generate export cargo year-round that requires bulk packing-grade plywood for crating finished modules, machinery skids, and pipe spools.

ADNOC upstream and downstream

The Abu Dhabi National Oil Company and its operating subsidiaries — onshore, offshore, refining, gas processing — drive a heavy spare-parts and turnaround economy. Valves, pumps, instrumentation, drill-bits, and high-pressure tubulars all move in custom-built wooden crates with ISPM-15 stamps. The vendor ecosystem in Mussafah and Musaffah West (M-zones) is where most of this crating is fabricated, and it runs on continuous plywood supply.

Mussafah and ICAD

The Industrial City of Abu Dhabi (ICAD I through IV) hosts steel fabrication, building materials, automotive workshops, and contract manufacturers. This belt absorbs a lot of pallet stock and shuttering ply — pallets for inbound and outbound logistics, shuttering for the construction projects still active across Saadiyat, Yas, and the inland Al Ain corridor.

What we ship into Abu Dhabi

The product mix into Abu Dhabi is more focused than what we ship into, say, a furniture market. Operators here are buying wood as an industrial input, not a finished good, so specification and consistency matter more than aesthetics.

Packing-grade plywood

The workhorse SKU. Standard sheet size 8 ft x 4 ft (2440 x 1220 mm), thicknesses from 6 mm up to 18 mm, MR-bonded for moisture resistance through humid sea transit. Common moves into Abu Dhabi:

  • 12 mm and 15 mm — crate walls and tops for medium machinery and ADNOC spare-parts cases
  • 18 mm — base boards and skid surfaces under heavy oil-and-gas equipment
  • 9 mm and 6 mm — light internal partitions, dunnage, and inner blocking

Where the buyer needs higher dimensional stability or a denser face — particularly for high-value modules where surface marking matters — we move them to Okoume face veneers on the same MR-packing or BWR construction. Okoume is not a separate grade; it is a face-veneer option that gives a smoother, more uniform skin for premium crates.

Built-up crates and knock-down kits

For Abu Dhabi customers who want to skip the in-house fabrication step, we ship pre-built crates or knock-down kits cut to the project's bill of materials, with cleats, corner blocks, and lid panels labelled per case. This works well for one-time project cargo out of KIZAD where the receiver does not want to keep idle carpentry capacity.

Kochi-to-Abu Dhabi logistics

The maritime lane is straightforward and one of the reasons Kerala-origin plywood is cost-competitive into the UAE. Cargo is consolidated at our Perumbavoor / Kochi loading point, trucked to Cochin Port (or Tuticorin / Mundra depending on the line), and sailed to Khalifa Port or Jebel Ali. Common parameters:

  • Sea distance: roughly 2,000 nautical miles Kochi to Khalifa Port
  • Transit time: typically 8 to 12 days port-to-port, depending on the carrier and transhipment
  • Container modes: 20 ft and 40 ft dry containers; FCL is the standard for plywood given the cube efficiency
  • Documentation: commercial invoice, packing list, BL, COO, phytosanitary or ISPM-15 certification where applicable, plus any FTA preference certificates

For project crates and pallets, ISPM-15 heat-treatment marking is non-negotiable at UAE customs, and we ship pre-marked stock so the receiver does not have to re-treat at destination.

Decision rules — which product fits which Abu Dhabi load

A practical decision matrix for buyers in the emirate:

  1. If you are crating standard industrial machinery, valves, pumps, or spare parts for ADNOC vendors, fabrication shops, or general KIZAD manufacturers — our MR-packing-grade plywood at 12 mm to 18 mm is the right specification. Cost-efficient, stable, ISPM-compliant.
  2. If the cargo is premium, high-visibility, or going to a customer-sensitive end recipient — request Okoume face veneer on the same MR-packing or BWR core. The finish reads cleaner and resists denting better in long sea moves.
  3. If you are buying for construction shuttering on Abu Dhabi infra and real-estate projects — that is shuttering ply, a separate SKU with a phenolic film face. It is the correct call for repeated concrete pours; using packing-grade for shuttering wastes money and breaks faster.
  4. If you need raw sawn timber or jackwood / rubberwood runners for crate cleats, dunnage, or pallet stringers — we ship sawn timber alongside the ply on the same container.

FAQ

Do you supply ISPM-15-treated plywood and crates to Abu Dhabi?

Yes. Built-up crates and pallets ship with ISPM-15 heat-treatment marking from origin. Sheet plywood does not require the mark, but any solid-wood components do.

What is the minimum order quantity for Abu Dhabi shipments?

FCL is the practical floor — one 20 ft container of plywood is the smallest economic move because of fixed sea-freight and customs handling costs. For built-up crates or pallets we can mix SKUs inside one container.

Can you deliver to KIZAD or Mussafah directly, or only to the port?

Standard quotation is CIF or FOB to the named UAE port. Inland delivery to KIZAD, ICAD, or Mussafah is arranged on request through a local clearing agent — we can recommend operators we have worked with on the lane.
