Plywood Supply to Udaipur

Cochin Wood Industries

Udaipur is not a typical plywood market. The buyers who matter here are not furniture workshops or interior contractors — they are zinc smelter packing departments, marble export yards, brass handicraft consolidators and the truck-body builders who feed all three. Each of these segments has a very specific plywood requirement, and most of them care more about edge integrity, moisture behaviour and weight per crate than they do about face cosmetics. That is the niche the Cochin Wood group has served from Kochi since 1986 — and it is exactly why a southern Kerala supplier shows up on Rajasthan packing lines roughly 1,900 km away.

The Udaipur industrial picture

The Udaipur district carries an unusually heavy industrial footprint for a city better known as a tourist destination. The plywood demand we see flowing out of it concentrates in five distinct belts:

  • Chanderiya Lead-Zinc Smelter (Hindustan Zinc) — roughly 13 km from Udaipur city, the largest single zinc smelting complex feeding the world's second-largest zinc producer. Ingot bundles, cathode plates and silver dore boxes all need export-grade wooden packaging.
  • Madri Industrial Area (RIICO) — Sector 1 through 4, packed with auxiliary engineering units, mineral processors and electrical equipment fabricators that crate finished goods for despatch across India and the Gulf.
  • Sukher Industrial Area — mixed engineering and handicraft units that export brass and metalware to the US and EU.
  • Kaladwas and Debari — the zinc-and-chemicals corridor along NH-48 east of the city, including Debari Zinc Smelter and ancillary acid/sulphuric units.
  • The marble belt — Rakhi, Morwad, Sukher and the Kankroli–Rajsamand stretch — Udaipur is the consolidation point for Rajasthan marble heading to Mumbai's JNPT, Mundra and Pipavav for export to the Middle East and East Asia. Marble blocks and slabs ride on heavy-duty wooden bases and side-protected crates.

Layered on top of these is the handicraft sector — silver jewellery, miniature paintings, brass sculptures, marble inlay work — much of it sold through Jaipur and Delhi exporters but crated locally in Udaipur. These are smaller boxes but high-value contents, so the plywood spec matters.

What we ship to Udaipur

Udaipur buyers typically pull four product lines from the Cochin Wood group's range. The fit depends on what is being packed.

Packing-grade plywood (commercial / rubberwood)

The workhorse for crate walls, lids and internal partitions. Common thicknesses we move on the Kochi–Udaipur lane:

ThicknessTypical Udaipur use
6 mmHandicraft inner boxes, marble slab face-protection sheets
8–10 mmLight engineering crates, brass-ware export boxes
12 mmZinc ingot stacking boxes, medium engineering crates
15–16 mmHeavy crate side walls, marble block crates
18 mmCrate bases, container flooring, heavy machinery skids

Sheets ship in standard 8×4 ft, with 8×6 and 7×4 cut sizes available against confirmed orders. All packing-grade is despatched ex-factory; GST and freight are extra. As a rule we work to purchase rate + ₹2.50/sq.ft on the packing range so the costing stays transparent.

Okoume plywood

Okoume is a premium-grade face veneer option for buyers who export to markets that inspect crate quality — Europe, the GCC and parts of East Asia. Where a marble exporter in Sukher or a precision-engineering unit in Madri needs a cleaner face, tighter glue line and lower variance crate-to-crate, Okoume is the right call. It is priced separately from the commercial packing card and quoted on request, typically against monthly volumes.

Pallets and ISPM-15 crates

For Hindustan Zinc despatches and any marble or handicraft container heading abroad, packaging must comply with ISPM-15 — the international heat-treatment standard for wood packaging in export. Standard pallet footprints we build to:

  • 1200 × 800 mm Euro pallet — common for engineering and electronics exports
  • 1200 × 1000 mm — the heavier base used for zinc ingot bundles
  • 1100 × 1100 mm — for ocean-container utilisation on long-haul routes
  • Custom skids — heavy-duty bearers for marble blocks, often 150–200 mm thick sawn timber tops on plywood deck

Container flooring and shuttering ply

Container floor replacement ply (typically 28 mm hardwood) and shuttering plywood for the construction projects feeding Udaipur's hospitality and housing build-out are both supplied on order. Shuttering ply is useful for Sukher and Madri civil works as well as for the steady infrastructure spend around the lakes and approach roads.

Sawn timber

Jackwood runners at ₹400/cft (¼-inch minus tolerance) and rubberwood runners as per size complete the crate bill of materials — bearers, cleats and corner posts that take the structural load while plywood handles the wall span.

Kochi-to-Udaipur logistics

Udaipur sits roughly 1,900 km by road from Kochi. The standard trucking lane runs Kochi → Bangalore → Pune → Ahmedabad → Udaipur on the NH-48 / NH-8 corridor. A full-truck movement is normally 6 to 8 days door-to-door depending on toll-gate season and Rajasthan border RTO clearance. Material despatches ex-factory from our Perumbavoor base, and we coordinate transport on request — though most repeat buyers prefer to nominate their own transporter once the lane is established.

For partial loads or fast-moving SKUs, container-based movement via Cochin Port → Mundra (sea leg) → Udaipur (road leg of about 750 km) can work out cost-competitive on certain thickness mixes, particularly where the buyer is already pulling other imports through Mundra. It is worth pricing both options on a thickness-and-volume basis before committing.

Decision rules: which grade for which Udaipur load

  • Zinc ingot crates and brass/electrical engineering exports — packing-grade commercial plywood, 12–18 mm depending on box size, ISPM-15 heat treated. The face is not the priority; edge crush resistance and consistent glue line are.
  • Marble block and slab crates heading to JNPT/Mundra — 15–18 mm packing-grade for walls, 18 mm or sawn-timber-on-ply skids for the base. Internal cushioning is the marble exporter's job; our job is the structural cage.
  • Premium handicraft, brass sculpture and silver consignments — Okoume-faced packing ply, 8–12 mm. The cosmetic uplift matters when international customs inspectors open boxes randomly.
  • Civil and infrastructure work in Udaipur city — shuttering plywood, BWR-bonded. Different product family, same Kochi origin.

FAQ

What is the minimum order quantity for Udaipur despatch?

A full truckload (roughly 200–250 sheets depending on thickness mix) keeps freight per sq.ft at a sensible level. Smaller orders are possible but the per-sheet landed cost rises sharply, so we usually recommend consolidating with another local buyer or planning a quarterly intake instead.

Do you handle the ISPM-15 fumigation and stamp?

Yes. Heat-treated, IPPC-stamped material can be supplied for any export-bound crate or pallet. Specify ISPM-15 at the quotation stage so it is built into the line item rather than added as an afterthought.

Can you build the crates in Udaipur instead of shipping flat sheets?

For most segments it is more economical to ship plywood and sawn timber to Udaipur and build crates at the packing line — the cube of empty crates is wasteful on a 1,900 km haul. We supply the boards, runners and ISPM-15-treated bearers cut to size; local fabricators in Madri and Sukher handle assembly.
