Plywood Supply to Tiruppur — Packing Ply, Crates, Pallets

Cochin Wood Industries

Why Tiruppur sits on our regular trucking lane

Tiruppur ships somewhere around Rs 30,000 crore worth of knitwear out of India every year, and that volume does not move in cardboard alone. Garment bales need clean, dimensionally stable plywood cases that hold up in a 40-foot ocean container for 30-45 days at sea. The hosiery machinery cluster around the SIPCOT estate ships textile knitting machines, dyeing equipment and compressors to buyers across the GCC, Bangladesh, Vietnam and Africa — and those export consignments need ISPM-15 compliant wooden crates with the IPPC stamp. From our Kochi base, Tiruppur is one of the most efficient lanes we run: roughly 190 km up NH-544 through the Walayar ghat, a 5-7 hour truck haul depending on Salem-side traffic. The Cochin Wood group has been working this Kerala-Tamil Nadu border belt since 1986, so the freight, paperwork and supplier network are well-worn.

The Tiruppur industrial picture

For a city of its size, Tiruppur packs an unusual concentration of export-grade manufacturing. The plywood demand here splits across four broad sectors, and understanding which one a buyer sits in changes what we recommend.

Knitwear and garment exports

The core belt — Mannarai, Kongu Nagar, Iduvai, Veerapandi, Palladam Road — is dense with knitwear units. T-shirts, polos, infantwear and athleisure ship in compressed bales, usually packed in 4-way entry pallets or lightweight plywood crates lined with polythene. For a typical 20-foot container of finished garments, the wood content is modest but the plywood has to be moisture-resistant: humidity in the Bay of Bengal sea route can wreck a poorly chosen pack.

Hosiery and textile machinery

SIPCOT Tiruppur Phase I and Phase II host several knitting-machine assemblers, dyeing-machine fabricators and circular-knitting OEMs. These exports are heavy point loads — a single machine can sit at 800-2,500 kg — and the crates must be ISPM-15 heat-treated with the IPPC mark. This is where we ship our heavier packing-grade plywood (12mm, 15mm, 18mm) along with seasoned hardwood runners.

Dyeing, processing and finishing units

The Perumanallur, Andipalayam and Mudalipalayam clusters handle wet processing. They are mostly local-load buyers — domestic dispatches of fabric rolls on pallets — and tend to use 6mm-10mm commercial plywood for lighter wooden cases.

Engineering ancillaries on the Avinashi-Tiruppur corridor

Spinning-mill spares, compressor parts and electrical components from this stretch often need a sturdy mixed-load crate. These are smaller-volume but higher-frequency orders.

Pallet sizes that work for the city

For garment exports out of Chennai and Tuticorin ports, the standard pallet footprints we cut for Tiruppur buyers are 1200x1000mm and 1100x1100mm, 4-way entry, with a deck of 12mm packing-grade ply on rubberwood runners. For the GCC route, 1200x800mm Euro footprint is the more common ask.

Container flooring

Repair-grade 18mm marine and apitong-substitute boards are available for fleet operators running Tiruppur-Chennai-Tuticorin shuttles. For new builds we quote on full container sets.

Kochi-to-Tiruppur logistics

The route is straightforward. From our Perumbavoor loading point we run up NH-544 (Salem-Kochi) via Angamaly, Walayar, Coimbatore and Avinashi, into Tiruppur. The full distance is about 190 km. Loaded transit on a 32-foot full-body or a 22-foot SXL truck is typically 5-7 hours, depending on the Walayar check-post and Coimbatore bypass traffic. We send most Tiruppur loads through Coimbatore-based transporters who already run the lane daily, so we can usually piggyback on existing capacity rather than dedicate a vehicle. For smaller LCL-type orders below 3 tonnes, we consolidate with other Tamil Nadu deliveries and the lead time goes to 2-3 days. For exporters with tight container stuffing windows, we can hold a dispatch slot in our schedule and confirm a truck the same day a PI is signed.

Decision rules — which grade for which job

This is where most buyer confusion sits. A short version of how we guide the choice:

  • Packing-grade commercial plywood (rubberwood core) — the right call for 80% of Tiruppur loads. Garment cases, fabric pallets, machinery sub-crates for the domestic and GCC routes. Best cost-per-sheet for honest, repeat-buy packing.
  • Okoume-faced plywood — when the buyer's overseas customer specifies a clean, light, blemish-free face — typical for European garment buyers and premium machinery exports. Quoted on request; not a stock item at packing-grade rates.
  • Shuttering ply — for site construction and infra contractors in and around Tiruppur (warehouse expansion, dyeing-effluent tank works). Not interchangeable with packing-grade; the resin system is different.
  • BWR/BWP-grade — for crates going via sea on the longer 35-45 day legs to West Africa and South America, where moisture cycling is severe.

The thickness-to-load rule we apply: under 500 kg point load, 10-12mm is enough; 500-1,500 kg, step up to 15mm walls and 18mm floor; above 1,500 kg, 18mm floor with 75mm hardwood runners and corner bracing.

FAQ for Tiruppur buyers

Do you supply ISPM-15 stamped crates ready-to-load?

We supply the plywood and sawn timber inputs at our Kochi end. ISPM-15 heat-treatment and IPPC stamping is done at the buyer's end or at the buyer's nominated fumigator in Tiruppur — most knitwear and machinery exporters already have a tied fumigator on the Avinashi Road belt. We can recommend known operators if needed.

What's the minimum order to make a truckload from Kochi worthwhile?A standard 32-foot full-body lifts roughly 500-600 sheets of 8mm packing ply, or about 350-400 sheets of 18mm. For LCL part-loads we consolidate with other Tamil Nadu deliveries; minimum sensible LCL is around 50-100 sheets, with a 2-3 day lead time.

Can you hold inventory or run a scheduled monthly dispatch?

For repeat exporters in the SIPCOT belt placing 1-2 trucks a month, yes — we can lock a monthly dispatch slot and indexed pricing against our purchase rates. Walk us through the spec and volume and we will structure it on the PI.
