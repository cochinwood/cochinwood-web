Questions buyers ask

Block board & flush doors, answered.

What is the difference between block board and plywood?

Block board has a core of solid hardwood battens, which makes it lighter and stiffer over long spans — good for doors, shutters and tabletops. Plywood is cross-laminated veneers, stronger in every direction. For long flat panels, block board resists sag better.

What core do your flush doors use?

A solid / block core with hardwood lipping on all four edges, bonded BWR or BWP, so the door holds hinges and locks and resists moisture.

What finishes are available?

Veneered, pre-laminated, PU-finished and skin doors — standard residential sizes in stock, bespoke sizes to order.
