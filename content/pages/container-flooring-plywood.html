Questions buyers ask

Container flooring plywood, answered.

What is container flooring plywood?

It is a high-density hardwood plywood, usually 28 mm thick, that forms the structural floor of a shipping container. It carries the full cargo load and a moving forklift, so it must meet the IICL TB-001 standard for container floorboards.

What standard does it follow?

IICL TB-001, issued by the Institute of International Container Lessors. It sets the bending strength, screw-holding, glue-bond and thickness tolerance a board must pass to be fitted as a container floor.

What thickness and panel size do you supply?

The standard is 28 mm at 2440 × 1220 mm (also 2400 × 1160 mm). We also press 21, 27 and 30 mm, with tongue-and-groove or bevel edges cut to your drawing.

What timber is the core?

A full hardwood core — keruing / apitong or equivalent dense tropical hardwood — bonded with phenol-formaldehyde (WBP) for a 100% waterproof glue line and a density of 700–800 kg/m³.

How is it different from shuttering plywood?

Shuttering plywood is film-faced formwork for concrete. Container flooring is denser, thicker, anti-slip and graded to IICL TB-001 to take rolling cargo loads — a different board for a different job.

Can you export it, and what is the minimum order?
