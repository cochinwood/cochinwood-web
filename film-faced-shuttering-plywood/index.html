Questions buyers ask

Film-faced shuttering plywood, answered.

How many pours does film-faced shuttering plywood last?

A high-density film-faced panel handled well gives 20–40+ pours; a cheap panel can fail in under 10. Cost per pour, not sheet price, is the real number. Full cost-per-pour guide →

What film weight do you supply?

120–220 gsm phenolic film on both faces — heavier film for more re-uses and a finer concrete finish.

What thickness and sizes?

12, 15, 18, 21 and 25 mm at 1220 × 2440 mm (and custom). 12 mm and 18 mm are the most common for slab and wall forms.

Is shuttering plywood the same as container flooring?

No. Shuttering is formwork for concrete; container flooring is a denser, anti-slip board graded to IICL TB-001. See container flooring →
