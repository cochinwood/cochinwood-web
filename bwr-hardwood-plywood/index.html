Questions buyers ask

BWR plywood, answered.

What is BWR plywood?

BWR (boiling-water-resistant) is an IS 303 commercial grade with a melamine-fortified glue line that resists steam, humidity and occasional wetting — the right board for kitchens and bathrooms.

What is the difference between BWR and MR plywood?

BWR uses a fortified glue line for humid areas; MR is urea-formaldehyde bonded for dry interiors and furniture. Same standard (IS 303), different water resistance. See MR commercial plywood →

Is BWR the same as marine plywood?

No. BWR resists humidity and occasional wetting; marine BWP (IS 710) is 100% phenolic and boiling-waterproof for constant water. IS 710 vs IS 303 explained →
